# Revelation Chapter 8 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 13 verses align across all three sources (Vulgate, SBLGNT, Delitzsch).

## Content Alignment

### 8:1 — "silentium" vs. "sige" vs. "demamah"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *silentium* ("silence"), Greek *sige* ("silence"), Hebrew *demamah* ("stillness/silence"). All three align. The Hebrew *demamah* is the exact word from 1 Kgs 19:12 (*qol demamah daqqah*, "a still small voice"). Hab 2:20 and Zech 2:17 also use related forms for silence before the LORD.
- **Note**: Excellent word choice. *Demamah* carries the specific biblical connotation of reverent silence before divine action.

### 8:3 — "thuribulum" vs. "libanoton" vs. "machtat"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *thuribulum aureum* ("golden censer"), Greek *libanoton chrysoun* ("golden censer/incense-holder"). The Hebrew has *machtat zahav* ("golden fire-pan/censer"). *Machtat* appears in Lev 16:12, Num 16:6, and 2 Chr 26:19 for the ritual censers used in temple worship.
- **Note**: *Machtat* is precisely the right temple-vessel term. The connection to the Day of Atonement ritual (Lev 16:12, where the high priest fills a *machtat* with coals from the altar) is theologically significant.

### 8:5 — Word order variance
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *tonitrua, et voces, et fulgura, et terraemotus* ("thunder, voices, lightning, earthquake"). The Greek has *brontai kai phonai kai astrapai kai seismos* ("thunder, voices, lightning, earthquake"). The Hebrew has *qolot ur'amim uveraqim vara'ash* ("voices and thunder and lightning and earthquake"), placing *qolot* first.
- **Note**: The Hebrew reorders to begin with *qolot*, which echoes the Sinai theophany language of Ex 19:16 (*qolot uveraqim*, "thunder and lightning"). Minor reordering.

### 8:7 — "tertia pars terrae combusta est" — Vulgate addition
- **Issue**: The Vulgate includes *tertia pars terrae combusta est* ("a third of the earth was burned") before the trees. The SBLGNT does not include "a third of the earth" in most critical editions — it reads only that a third of the trees and all green grass were burned. The Hebrew has *va-tisaref shelishit ha-etz* ("a third of the trees burned"), omitting the "third of the earth" found in the Vulgate.
- **Vulgate**: *et tertia pars terrae combusta est, et tertia pars arborum concremata est*
- **Greek**: *kai to triton tes ges katekae, kai to triton ton dendron katekae* (variant — some MSS include "earth")
- **Hebrew**: *va-tisaref shelishit ha-etz*
- **Note**: The Hebrew omits the Vulgate's "third of the earth" clause. The textual tradition is divided here, but the Vulgate includes it. For Vulgate conformity, this should be noted.

### 8:9 — Verb voice
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *mortua est* ("died") for the sea creatures, and *interiit* ("perished") for the ships. The Greek similarly has *apethane* ("died") and *diephtharasan* ("were destroyed"). The Hebrew has *va-tamat* ("died") and *nishchatah* ("was destroyed"), aligning well with both.
- **Note**: Good alignment.

### 8:11 — "Absinthium" — naming
- **Issue**: The Vulgate names the star *Absinthium* ("Wormwood"), the Greek *Apsinthos* ("Wormwood"), and the Hebrew *la'anah* ("wormwood"). *La'anah* appears in Deut 29:17, Jer 9:14, 23:15, Lam 3:15, 19, Amos 5:7. In Jer 23:15, God feeds false prophets with *la'anah* and gives them *mei-rosh* ("poisoned water") — a close parallel to the bitter waters here.
- **Note**: The Hebrew perfectly captures the OT wormwood allusion, especially Jer 23:15.

### 8:13 — "aquilae" vs. "aetou" vs. "nesher"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *unius aquilae* ("one eagle"), and the Greek likewise *henos aetou* ("one eagle"). Some manuscripts and the TR read *angelos* ("angel") instead of *aetou* ("eagle"). The Hebrew has *nesher echad* ("one eagle"), following the critical/Vulgate reading.
- **Note**: *Nesher* is the standard biblical Hebrew eagle/vulture (Ex 19:4, Deut 32:11, Isa 40:31). Good.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 8:1 — chatzi sha'ah
- **Hebrew**: *ka-chatzi sha'ah* ("about half an hour")
- **Note**: *Sha'ah* ("hour") is post-biblical in its precise temporal sense. Biblical Hebrew would typically express time with *et* (time) or *rega* (moment). However, *sha'ah* appears in Dan 3:6 (Aramaic *sha'ata*) and became standard in Mishnaic Hebrew. The usage is acceptable for the Delitzsch register.

### 8:2 — shofarot for "tubae"
- **Hebrew**: *shiv'ah shofarot*
- **Note**: *Shofar* rather than *chatzotzrah* (silver trumpet). The shofar is the instrument of theophany (Ex 19:16, Josh 6:4), the Jubilee (Lev 25:9), and eschatological warning (Joel 2:1, Zeph 1:16). *Chatzotzrah* is the priestly signal trumpet (Num 10:2). Delitzsch's choice of *shofar* is theologically deliberate, connecting the Revelation trumpets to OT theophanic and eschatological contexts rather than routine priestly ritual. The choice of *shofar* over *chatzotzrah* is maintained consistently throughout chapters 8-11.

### 8:3 — qetoret for "incensa"
- **Hebrew**: *qetoret harbeh* ("much incense")
- **Note**: *Qetoret* is the specific temple incense (Ex 30:7-8, 37-38). This is the sacred incense compound described in Ex 30:34-38, which was reserved exclusively for tabernacle/temple use. The word choice reinforces the heavenly-temple imagery.

### 8:5 — Sinai echoes
- **Hebrew**: *va-yiqach ha-mal'akh et ha-machtah va-yemale'eha esh me-al ha-mizbeach va-yashlekh al ha-aretz*
- **Note**: The entire sequence — taking fire from the altar, casting it to earth, followed by thunder, lightning, and earthquake — reprises the Sinai theophany (Ex 19:16-18) and Ezekiel's coal-scattering (Ezek 10:2). The Hebrew narrative style (*va-yiqach... va-yemale'eha... va-yashlekh*) uses the classic *vav-consecutive* chain that marks biblical Hebrew narrative prose.

### 8:7 — Exodus plague allusion
- **Hebrew**: *va-yehi barad va-esh belulim be-dam*
- **Note**: The hail and fire mixed with blood echoes the seventh plague of Egypt (Ex 9:23-24, *va-yehi barad ve-esh mitlaqqachat be-tokh ha-barad*, "there was hail, and fire flashing within the hail"). *Belulim* ("mixed") adds the blood element, escalating beyond the Exodus plague. The root *b-l-l* ("mix") is from Lev 2:4 and other cultic contexts.

### 8:10-11 — kokhav gadol / la'anah
- **Hebrew**: *kokhav gadol bo'er ka-lappid*
- **Note**: *Lappid* ("torch/lamp") echoes Gen 15:17 (the covenant ceremony, *lappid esh*, "flaming torch") and Judg 7:16 (Gideon's torches). *La'anah* for the star's name, as noted above, draws powerfully from the prophetic judgment tradition.

## Other Notes

### OT Allusion Density
Chapter 8 draws extensively from Exodus plague and Sinai theophany traditions:
- **Ex 9:23-24**: Hail and fire — v. 7
- **Ex 19:16-18**: Thunder, lightning, earthquake at Sinai — v. 5
- **Lev 16:12**: Censer from the altar — v. 3
- **Ezek 10:2**: Scattering coals over the city — v. 5
- **Jer 23:15**: Wormwood and poisoned water — v. 11
- **Joel 2:31**: Darkened sun — v. 12
- **1 Kgs 19:12**: Silence before the LORD — v. 1

### Structural Note
Chapter 8 forms a bridge: the silence of v. 1 closes the seal sequence, while the trumpets (vv. 2-13) open a new sequence. The Hebrew *demamah* for the silence and *shofar* for the trumpets create a unified sonic vocabulary drawn from OT theophany.
