# Revelation Chapter 14 — Translation Notes

## Versification

All 20 verses align across all three sources (Vulgate, SBLGNT, Delitzsch). No versification discrepancies.

## Content Alignment

### 14:1 — "Agnus stabat supra montem Sion" / "to arnion hestos epi to oros Sion" / "seh omed al har Tziyon"
- **Vulgate**: *Agnus stabat supra montem Sion* ("the Lamb was standing on Mount Zion")
- **Greek**: *to arnion hestos epi to oros Sion* ("the Lamb standing on Mount Zion")
- **Hebrew**: *seh omed al har Tziyon* ("a Lamb standing on Mount Zion")
- **Note**: All three align. *Har Tziyon* is the standard biblical designation (Ps 2:6, *ve-ani nasakhti malki al Tziyon har qodshi*, "I have installed my king on Zion, my holy hill"; Isa 24:23, Joel 3:5 [2:32], Obad 1:17, Mic 4:7). The Lamb on Zion fulfills Ps 2:6, where the anointed king is set upon Zion. The juxtaposition with chapter 13 (beast's dominion) and chapter 14 (Lamb's Zion) recreates the Ps 2 conflict between the nations raging and God's anointed reigning.

### 14:1 — "nomen ejus et nomen Patris ejus" / "to onoma autou kai to onoma tou patros autou"
- **Vulgate**: *habentes nomen ejus, et nomen Patris ejus scriptum in frontibus suis* ("having his name and the name of his Father written on their foreheads")
- **Hebrew**: *u-shemo ve-shem aviv katuv al mitzchtam* ("and his name and his father's name written on their foreheads")
- **Note**: All three align. The seal on the forehead of the 144,000 (*katuv al mitzchtam*) directly contrasts with the beast's mark on the forehead (13:16). This echoes Ezek 9:4 (protective *tav*) and Ex 28:36-38 (the golden plate on the high priest's forehead inscribed *qodesh la-YHVH*, "Holy to the LORD"). The 144,000 bear a priestly seal.

### 14:3 — "canticum novum" / "oden kainen" / "shir chadash"
- **Hebrew**: *va-yashiru shir chadash* ("and they sang a new song")
- **Note**: *Shir chadash* ("new song") is a fixed OT expression: Ps 33:3, 40:4, 96:1, 98:1, 144:9, 149:1, Isa 42:10. All three sources align. The phrase carries connotations of divine deliverance — each OT "new song" celebrates a new act of God's salvation.

### 14:4 — "virgines" / "parthenoi" / "ke-vetulot"
- **Vulgate**: *virgines enim sunt* ("for they are virgins")
- **Greek**: *parthenoi gar eisin* ("for they are virgins")
- **Hebrew**: *ki ke-vetulot hemmah* ("for they are like virgins")
- **Flag**: The Hebrew adds *ke-* ("like/as"), making them "like virgins" rather than simply "virgins." Both the Vulgate and Greek state flatly that they *are* virgins. The Hebrew introduces a simile that softens the claim. This diverges from both source texts.

### 14:4 — "primitiae Deo et Agno" / "aparche to theo kai to arnio" / "le-reshit bikkurim le-Elohim ve-la-seh"
- **Vulgate**: *primitiae Deo, et Agno* ("firstfruits to God and the Lamb")
- **Greek**: *aparche to theo kai to arnio* ("firstfruits to God and to the Lamb")
- **Hebrew**: *le-reshit bikkurim le-Elohim ve-la-seh* ("as the first of the firstfruits to God and to the Lamb")
- **Note**: The Hebrew doubles the firstfruit language: *reshit bikkurim* ("first of the firstfruits") combines *reshit* (Gen 1:1, Ex 23:19) and *bikkurim* (Ex 23:16, 34:22, Num 18:13). The phrase *reshit bikkurim* appears in Ex 23:19 and 34:26 (*reshit bikkurei admatekha*, "the first of the firstfruits of your land"). This is slightly more emphatic than either the Vulgate or Greek but uses excellent OT vocabulary.

### 14:5 — "sine macula... ante thronum Dei" / Vulgate addition
- **Vulgate**: *sine macula enim sunt ante thronum Dei* ("for they are without blemish before the throne of God")
- **Greek**: *amomoi eisin* ("they are blameless") — the SBLGNT omits *enopion tou thronou tou theou* ("before the throne of God")
- **Hebrew**: *ki temimim hemmah lifnei kisse ha-Elohim* ("for they are blameless before the throne of God")
- **Note**: The Hebrew includes *lifnei kisse ha-Elohim* ("before the throne of God"), following the Vulgate against the SBLGNT. Good Vulgate alignment. *Temimim* ("blameless/whole") is the standard OT sacrificial term (Ex 12:5, Lev 1:3, 3:1, 22:21) — the 144,000 are described with the vocabulary of an unblemished offering.

### 14:5 — "mendacium" / "pseudos" / "tarmit"
- **Vulgate**: *non est inventum mendacium* ("falsehood was not found")
- **Greek**: *ouch heurethe pseudos* ("falsehood was not found")
- **Hebrew**: *ve-tarmit lo nimtze'ah be-fihem* ("and deceit was not found in their mouth")
- **Note**: The Hebrew uses *tarmit* ("deceit/fraud") rather than *sheqer* ("lie/falsehood"), which would be the more direct equivalent of *mendacium*/*pseudos*. *Tarmit* appears in Jer 8:5, 14:14, 23:26, Zeph 3:13 (*lo yedabberu khazav ve-lo yimmatze be-fihem leshon tarmit*, "they shall not speak lies nor shall a deceitful tongue be found in their mouth"). The Zeph 3:13 parallel is particularly close and may have influenced Delitzsch's word choice. Good OT allusion.

### 14:7 — "Timete Dominum" vs. "Phobethete ton theon" vs. "yir'u et ha-Elohim"
- **Vulgate**: *Timete Dominum* ("Fear the Lord")
- **Greek**: *Phobethete ton theon* ("Fear God")
- **Hebrew**: *yir'u et ha-Elohim* ("Fear God")
- **Flag**: The Vulgate has *Dominum* ("the Lord") while the Greek has *ton theon* ("God"). The Hebrew follows the Greek with *ha-Elohim* ("God") rather than using *YHVH* or *Adonai* for the Vulgate's *Dominum*. Since the Vulgate prioritizes *Dominum*, the Hebrew diverges here.

### 14:8 — "Cecidit, cecidit Babylon illa magna"
- **Vulgate**: *Cecidit, cecidit Babylon illa magna*
- **Greek**: *Epesen, epesen Babylon he megale*
- **Hebrew**: *nafelah nafelah Bavel ha-ir ha-gedolah* ("fallen, fallen is Babylon the great city")
- **Note**: All three align. The doubled *nafelah nafelah* ("fallen, fallen") echoes Isa 21:9 (*nafelah nafelah Bavel*, "fallen, fallen is Babylon"). The Hebrew is virtually identical to the Isaiah source text. The Hebrew adds *ha-ir* ("the city"), which is in the Vulgate (*illa*) but not explicit in the Greek. Good alignment.

### 14:8 — "a vino irae fornicationis suae" / "wine of wrath of fornication"
- **Hebrew**: *ki hishqetah khol ha-goyim mi-yein chamat taznutah* ("for she made all the nations drink from the wine of the wrath of her fornication")
- **Note**: *Yein chamat taznutah* ("wine of the wrath of her fornication") combines Jer 25:15 (*kos ha-yayin ha-chemah*, "this cup of the wine of wrath") with Jer 51:7 (*kos zahav Bavel be-yad YHVH meshakkeret kol ha-aretz mi-yeinah shatu goyim*, "Babylon was a golden cup in the LORD's hand, making all the earth drunk; the nations drank of her wine"). The Hebrew synthesis of these Jeremiah passages is excellent.

### 14:10 — "vino irae Dei" / "wine of God's wrath"
- **Hebrew**: *mi-yein chamat Elohim ha-nimsakh ve-lo mahul be-khos za'mo* ("from the wine of the wrath of God, mixed full strength and not diluted, in the cup of his indignation")
- **Note**: The Hebrew adds *ve-lo mahul* ("and not diluted"), which interpretively renders the Greek *tou kekerasmenou akratou* ("mixed unmixed" — a paradoxical expression meaning poured out at full strength). The Vulgate has *quod mistum est mero* ("which is mixed with pure wine"). *Nimsakh* ("poured/mixed") and *mahul* ("diluted/watered down") are both suitable Hebrew terms. *Khos za'mo* ("cup of his indignation") echoes Isa 51:17, 22 (*kos chamat YHVH... kos ha-tar'elah*, "the cup of the LORD's wrath... the cup of staggering") and Jer 25:15-16.

### 14:13 — "Beati mortui qui in Domino moriuntur"
- **Vulgate**: *Beati mortui qui in Domino moriuntur. Amodo jam dicit Spiritus*
- **Greek**: *Makarioi hoi nekroi hoi en kyrio apothneskontes ap' arti. nai, legei to pneuma*
- **Hebrew**: *ashrei ha-metim asher yamutu ba-Adon me-attah omnam ken amar ha-Ruach*
- **Note**: All three sources align well. *Ashrei* ("blessed/happy") is the classic OT beatitude formula (Ps 1:1, 32:1-2, 84:5-6, 119:1-2, 128:1). *Ashrei ha-metim asher yamutu ba-Adon* echoes the OT beatitude pattern while expressing a distinctly NT theology of dying "in the Lord." The Spirit's affirmation (*amar ha-Ruach*) is identified with the Holy Spirit.

### 14:14 — "similem Filio hominis" / "homoion huion anthropou" / "ki-demut ben adam"
- **Hebrew**: *ki-demut ben adam* ("like the likeness of a son of man")
- **Note**: *Ki-demut ben adam* echoes Dan 7:13 (*ke-var enash*, "like a son of man" in Aramaic). The Hebrew uses *demut* ("likeness") for *homoion*/*similem*, which echoes Ezek 1:26 (*ke-mar'eh adam*, "like the appearance of a man") and Dan 10:16 (*ki-demut benei adam*, "one like the sons of men"). *Ben adam* is the standard Hebrew for "son of man" throughout Ezekiel (Ezek 2:1, 3, 6, etc.) and Ps 8:5.

### 14:15 — Harvest imagery
- **Hebrew**: *shelach magalekha u-qetzor ki va'ah ha-et li-qetzor ki yavesh qetzir ha-aretz* ("send your sickle and reap, for the time to reap has come, for the harvest of the earth is dry/ripe")
- **Note**: *Magal* ("sickle") appears in Joel 4:13 [3:13] (*shilchu magal ki vashal qatzir*, "put in the sickle, for the harvest is ripe"). The Joel passage is the direct OT source for Rev 14:15-16. The Hebrew of Revelation uses nearly identical vocabulary to Joel, maintaining the prophetic harvest connection. *Qetzir* ("harvest") echoes Jer 51:33 (*bat Bavel ki-goren... et qetzir lah*, "daughter of Babylon is like a threshing floor... her harvest time is coming").

### 14:18-19 — Grape harvest / winepress
- **Hebrew**: *shelach magalekha ha-meluttash u-vetzor et ashkelot gefen ha-aretz ki vashelu inbeihem* ("send your sharp sickle and gather the clusters of the vine of the earth, for their grapes are ripe")
- **Note**: *Ashkelot* ("clusters") appears in Num 13:23-24 (*va-yikhretu mi-sham zemorah ve-eshkol anavim echad*, "and they cut down a branch with a single cluster of grapes" — from the Valley of Eshcol). *Gefen* ("vine") and *ashkelot* together evoke the Promised Land reconnaissance narrative.

### 14:19-20 — "lacum irae Dei" / "gat chamat Elohim"
- **Hebrew**: *va-yashlikhem be-gat chamat Elohim ha-gedolah* ("and he cast them into the great winepress of the wrath of God")
- **Note**: *Gat* ("winepress") appears in Isa 63:3 (*purah darachti levaddi*, "I have trodden the winepress alone") and Joel 4:13 [3:13] (*lechu redu ki male'ah gat*, "go down, for the winepress is full"). The winepress of divine judgment is a major prophetic image. Lam 1:15 also uses it (*gat darakh Adonai li-vtulat bat Yehudah*, "the Lord has trodden as in a winepress the virgin daughter of Judah").

### 14:20 — Blood up to the horses' bridles
- **Hebrew**: *va-yetze dam min ha-gat va-ya'al ad risnei ha-susim derekh elef ve-shesh me'ot ris* ("and blood came out from the winepress and rose to the horses' bridles for a distance of 1,600 stadia")
- **Note**: *Risnei ha-susim* ("bridles of the horses") — *resen* appears in Isa 30:28 (*ve-resen mat'eh al lechyei ammim*, "a bridle that leads astray, on the jaws of the peoples") and Ps 32:9 (*be-meteg va-resen edyo livlom*, "with bit and bridle his ornament must be held in"). The measure *ris* (stadia) is transliterated from Greek, as biblical Hebrew has no equivalent unit for this distance.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 14:1 — har Tziyon
- **Note**: Using the unadorned *har Tziyon* rather than a longer form captures the covenantal weight of the name. Every OT use of *har Tziyon* carries theological density — it is the place of God's presence, His chosen dwelling (Ps 132:13, Isa 8:18, 24:23, Joel 3:5 [2:32]).

### 14:2 — tofsei kinnor menagnim be-kinnoroteihem
- **Hebrew**: *ke-qol tofsei kinnor menagnim be-kinnoroteihem* ("like the sound of harp players playing on their harps")
- **Note**: *Kinnor* ("harp/lyre") is David's instrument (1 Sam 16:16, 23, 18:10, 19:9) and the instrument of Levitical worship (1 Chr 15:16, 21, 25:1-6, 2 Chr 5:12). *Menagnim* ("playing/making music") appears in 1 Sam 16:16-17 of David's harp playing. The vocabulary explicitly connects the heavenly worship to the Davidic/Levitical musical tradition.

### 14:3 — va-yashiru
- **Hebrew**: *va-yashiru shir chadash* ("and they sang a new song")
- **Note**: The wayyiqtol form *va-yashiru* echoes the Song of the Sea (Ex 15:1, *az yashir Moshe*, "then Moses sang") — in fact, the verb form and structure are nearly identical. This sets up the explicit reference to the Song of Moses in 15:3.

### 14:7 — la-oseh shamayim va-aretz
- **Hebrew**: *ve-hishtachavu la-oseh shamayim va-aretz et ha-yam u-ma'yenot ha-mayim* ("and worship the one who made heaven and earth, the sea and the springs of waters")
- **Note**: *La-oseh shamayim va-aretz* echoes Ps 115:15, 121:2, 124:8, 134:3, 146:6 (*oseh shamayim va-aretz*, "maker of heaven and earth"). The addition of *ha-yam u-ma'yenot ha-mayim* ("the sea and springs of waters") echoes Ps 146:6 and the creation account. This is a creation-theology doxology using standard psalmist vocabulary.

### 14:19 — va-yanef ha-mal'akh et magalo
- **Hebrew**: *va-yanef ha-mal'akh et magalo al ha-aretz* ("and the angel swung his sickle over the earth")
- **Note**: *Va-yanef* (hiphil of *n-v-f*, "to wave/swing") is used in ritual contexts for the wave offering (Ex 29:24, 26, Lev 7:30, 8:27, 9:21, 14:12, 23:11-12, 20, Num 6:20). The use of this ritual term for the angel's harvesting action adds a liturgical/sacrificial dimension — the grape harvest is framed as a priestly act.

## Other Notes

### Red-Text Candidates
- **14:13**: *ashrei ha-metim asher yamutu ba-Adon me-attah* — The heavenly voice commands John to write this beatitude. The Spirit (*ha-Ruach*) then affirms: *omnam ken amar ha-Ruach*. The Spirit's words (*le-ma'an yanuchu me-amalam ki ma'aseihem holkhim achareihem*, "that they may rest from their labors, for their works follow them") are words of the Holy Spirit and are a red-text candidate.

### OT Allusion Density
- **Ps 2:6**: Lamb on Mount Zion — v. 1
- **Ps 33:3, 96:1, 98:1, 149:1**: New song — v. 3
- **Zeph 3:13**: No deceit in their mouth — v. 5
- **Isa 21:9**: Fallen, fallen is Babylon — v. 8
- **Jer 25:15, 51:7**: Cup of wrath — vv. 8, 10
- **Isa 51:17, 22**: Cup of God's indignation — v. 10
- **Dan 7:13**: One like a son of man — v. 14
- **Joel 4:13 [3:13]**: Sickle and harvest/winepress — vv. 15, 18
- **Isa 63:3**: Treading the winepress — v. 20

### Theological Note — Three Angels
The three angels of 14:6-12 deliver a threefold proclamation: (1) fear God the Creator, (2) Babylon has fallen, (3) judgment on beast-worshippers. This tripartite structure mirrors the three woes of chapters 8-9 (8:13) but inverts them — where the woes pronounced judgment on the earth, the three angels announce the gospel (*besorat olam*, "eternal gospel," v. 6), Babylon's doom, and the vindication of the saints. The *besorat olam* ("eternal gospel") is the only use of *besorah* ("gospel/good news") in Revelation, connecting to Isa 40:9, 52:7, 61:1 (*le-vasser anavim*, "to bring good news to the poor").
