# Revelation Chapter 12 — Translation Notes

## Versification

The Vulgate and Delitzsch both include 12:18 as a distinct verse. The SBLGNT also has 12:18 (beginning of 13:1 in some traditions). The Vulgate text for 12:18 is *Et stetit supra arenam maris* ("And he stood upon the sand of the sea"). The Hebrew field for 12:18 is empty — the content has been absorbed into 13:1 (the Hebrew of 13:1 begins *va-ehi omed al chol ha-yam*, "and I was standing on the sand of the sea"), which follows a variant reading where the subject is "I" (John) rather than "he" (the dragon). This is a significant versification and textual issue.

- **12:18 in Vulgate**: *Et stetit supra arenam maris* — subject is "he" (the dragon), third person.
- **12:18 in SBLGNT**: *kai estathe epi ten ammon tes thalasses* — the critical text reads *estathe* ("he stood"), making the dragon the subject. Some manuscripts read *estathen* ("I stood"), making John the subject.
- **Hebrew**: Empty at 12:18; content merged into 13:1 with first-person reading (*va-ehi omed*, "and I was standing"), following the variant that makes John the subject.
- **Flag**: The Hebrew follows the minority reading (John standing) against both the Vulgate and the SBLGNT critical text (dragon standing). Since we prioritize the Vulgate, this should be noted as a divergence.

All other verses (12:1-17) align across the three sources.

## Content Alignment

### 12:1 — "signum magnum" / "semeion mega" / "ot gadol"
- **Vulgate**: *signum magnum apparuit in caelo*
- **Greek**: *semeion mega ophthe en to ourano*
- **Hebrew**: *ve-ot gadol nir'ah ba-shamayim*
- **Note**: All three align well. *Ot* ("sign") is the standard biblical Hebrew term for portent or sign (Gen 1:14, Ex 4:8, Isa 7:14). The use of *nir'ah* (niphal of *r-'-h*, "was seen/appeared") is excellent biblical Hebrew, matching the niphal forms of theophanic appearance (Gen 12:7, 17:1, Ex 3:2).

### 12:3 — "draco magnus rufus" vs. "tannin gadol adom ka-esh"
- **Vulgate**: *draco magnus rufus* ("great red dragon")
- **Greek**: *drakon megas pyrros* ("great fiery-red dragon")
- **Hebrew**: *tannin gadol adom ka-esh* ("great dragon, red as fire")
- **Note**: The Hebrew adds *ka-esh* ("as fire"), which is not explicit in either the Vulgate or the Greek, though the Greek *pyrros* (from *pyr*, "fire") connotes a fiery red. The Hebrew expansion makes the fire association explicit. *Tannin* is the standard OT term for sea monster/dragon (Gen 1:21, Ex 7:9-10, Isa 27:1, 51:9, Ezek 29:3, 32:2). The identification of the dragon with the *tannin* connects this passage to the cosmic conflict tradition of Isa 27:1 (*Leviathan... ha-tannin asher ba-yam*).

### 12:5 — "qui recturus erat" / "hos mellei poimainein" / "asher yir'eh"
- **Vulgate**: *qui recturus erat omnes gentes in virga ferrea* ("who was to rule all nations with a rod of iron")
- **Greek**: *hos mellei poimainein panta ta ethne en rabdo sidera* ("who is about to shepherd all the nations with a rod of iron")
- **Hebrew**: *asher yir'eh khol ha-goyim be-shevet barzel* ("who will shepherd all the nations with a rod of iron")
- **Note**: All three align. The Hebrew *yir'eh* ("will shepherd," from *r-'-h*) echoes Ps 2:9 LXX (*poimaneis autous*, "you shall shepherd them"). The Masoretic Ps 2:9 reads *tero'em* ("you shall break them," from *r-'-'*), but the LXX reads *poimaneis* ("you shall shepherd"). The Hebrew of Revelation here follows the LXX reading of Ps 2:9 rather than the MT — an interesting case where the NT Hebrew aligns with the Septuagint interpretation. *Shevet barzel* ("rod of iron") is directly from Ps 2:9. This is a strong Vulgate-aligned rendering.

### 12:7 — "praelium magnum" vs. "polemos" vs. "milchamah"
- **Vulgate**: *praelium magnum in caelo* ("a great battle in heaven") — includes *magnum* ("great")
- **Greek**: *polemos en to ourano* ("war in heaven") — no "great"
- **Hebrew**: *milchamah ba-shamayim* ("war in heaven") — no "great"
- **Flag**: The Hebrew follows the Greek against the Vulgate in omitting "great" (*gadol*). The Vulgate adds *magnum*, which is not in the SBLGNT. Since we prioritize the Vulgate, this is a minor divergence worth noting.

### 12:9 — "diabolus et Satanas" vs. "malshin ve-satan"
- **Vulgate**: *qui vocatur diabolus, et Satanas* ("who is called the devil and Satan")
- **Greek**: *ho kaloumenos Diabolos kai ho Satanas* ("who is called the Devil and Satan")
- **Hebrew**: *asher niqra shemo malshin ve-satan* ("whose name is called Slanderer and Adversary")
- **Note**: The Hebrew renders *Diabolos* as *malshin* ("slanderer/informer") rather than transliterating. This is an interpretive rendering — *malshin* is a rabbinic Hebrew term (from the root *l-sh-n*, "tongue"), not classical biblical Hebrew. The word is used in later Hebrew for an informer or accuser. The biblical Hebrew equivalent would be *satan* itself (which Delitzsch does use for *Satanas*) or possibly *meqatreg* (Aramaic loan). The choice of *malshin* reflects post-biblical Hebrew usage.

### 12:9 — "serpens antiquus" / "ophis ho archaios" / "ha-nachash ha-qadmoni"
- **Hebrew**: *ha-nachash ha-qadmoni* ("the ancient serpent")
- **Note**: *Ha-nachash ha-qadmoni* echoes Gen 3:1 (*ve-ha-nachash hayah arum*, "and the serpent was cunning"). The adjective *qadmoni* ("ancient/primeval") is excellent biblical Hebrew — it appears in Gen 15:19 (the Kadmonites), Ezek 47:18, Joel 2:20 (*ha-tzefoni*, the northern one, contrasts with *ha-qadmoni*, the eastern/ancient one). The pairing *nachash + qadmoni* effectively identifies the dragon of Rev 12 with the Genesis serpent.

### 12:10 — "accusator fratrum nostrorum" / "kategor" / "soten acheinu"
- **Vulgate**: *accusator fratrum nostrorum* ("accuser of our brothers")
- **Greek**: *ho kategor ton adelphon hemon* ("the accuser of our brothers")
- **Hebrew**: *soten acheinu* ("accuser of our brothers")
- **Note**: The Hebrew uses *soten* (a participial form suggesting "one who accuses"), which is related to *satan* but distinct. The classical OT precedent is Job 1-2 and Zech 3:1, where the *satan* stands as accuser before God. The phrase *ha-omed le-sitnam lifnei Eloheinu yomam va-lailah* ("who stands to accuse them before our God day and night") closely echoes Zech 3:1 (*ha-satan omed al yemino le-sitno*, "the Satan standing at his right hand to accuse him").

### 12:11 — "propter sanguinem Agni" / "dia to haima tou arniou" / "be-dam ha-seh"
- **Hebrew**: *ve-hem nitzchuhu be-dam ha-seh u-vi-devar edutam* ("and they conquered him by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of their testimony")
- **Note**: All three sources align. *Be-dam ha-seh* ("by the blood of the Lamb") uses the standard Delitzsch rendering for the Lamb (*seh* rather than *kebes* or *taleh*). The *seh* echoes Ex 12:3-5 (the Passover lamb selection), reinforcing the Paschal theology.

### 12:12 — "diabolus" vs. "ha-malshin"
- **Vulgate**: *descendit diabolus ad vos* ("the devil has descended to you")
- **Hebrew**: *yarad aleikhem ha-malshin* ("the slanderer has descended to you")
- **Note**: Again *malshin* for *diabolus*. See note at 12:9. The Hebrew consistently renders *diabolos* as *malshin* throughout the chapter.

### 12:14 — "tempus et tempora et dimidium temporis" / Daniel 7:25 allusion
- **Vulgate**: *per tempus et tempora, et dimidium temporis*
- **Greek**: *kairon kai kairous kai hemisu kairou*
- **Hebrew**: *mo'ed mo'adim va-chetzi* ("a season, seasons, and a half")
- **Note**: This is a direct allusion to Dan 7:25 and 12:7. The Hebrew *mo'ed mo'adim va-chetzi* closely follows the Aramaic of Dan 7:25 (*iddan ve-iddanin u-felag iddan*). The choice of *mo'ed* ("appointed time/season") rather than a transliteration of the Aramaic *iddan* is a deliberate hebraization. *Mo'ed* has strong liturgical/calendrical connotations (Gen 1:14, Lev 23:2, Ps 104:19), tying the time period to God's ordained calendar.

### 12:15-16 — The serpent's flood and the earth's rescue
- **Note**: All three sources align closely. The Hebrew *va-tiftach ha-aretz et piha va-tivla et ha-nahar* ("and the earth opened its mouth and swallowed the river") echoes Num 16:32 (*va-tiftach ha-aretz et piha va-tivla otam*, "and the earth opened its mouth and swallowed them") from the Korah rebellion. The verbal parallels are striking — the earth personified as an instrument of divine deliverance, opening its mouth to swallow a threat.

### 12:17 — "mandata Dei" / "entolas tou theou" / "piqqudei Elohim"
- **Vulgate**: *qui custodiunt mandata Dei, et habent testimonium Jesu Christi*
- **Greek**: *ton terounton tas entolas tou theou kai echonton ten martyrian Iesou*
- **Hebrew**: *ha-shomrim piqqudei Elohim va-asher edut Yeshua be-fihem*
- **Note**: The Hebrew adds *be-fihem* ("in their mouth"), which is not present in either the Vulgate or the Greek. Both say they "have" (*habent*/*echonton*) the testimony of Jesus, but the Hebrew specifies it is "in their mouth." This is an expansion beyond the source texts.
- **Flag**: The Vulgate includes *Jesu Christi* ("of Jesus Christ") while the SBLGNT has only *Iesou* ("of Jesus") without *Christou*. The Hebrew has *Yeshua* without *ha-Mashiach*, following the Greek against the Vulgate here.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 12:1 — ishah asher ha-shemesh levushah
- **Hebrew**: *ishah asher ha-shemesh levushah ve-ha-yareach tachat ragleha*
- **Note**: *Levushah* ("her garment") used to express "clothed with the sun" is a creative rendering. The image of the sun as clothing echoes Ps 104:2 (*oteh or ka-salmah*, "wrapping light as a garment") — God himself clothed in light. The Marian typology here (the Woman as the Blessed Virgin in Catholic tradition) is enhanced by this echo of divine clothing language.

### 12:2 — va-tiz'aq ba-chavaleha u-ve-tzirei lidtah
- **Hebrew**: *va-tiz'aq ba-chavaleha u-ve-tzirei lidtah* ("she cried out in her birth pangs and in the pains of her bearing")
- **Note**: *Chavalim* ("birth pangs") is the standard OT term (Isa 13:8, 26:17, Hos 13:13, Mic 4:9-10). *Tzirei ledah* ("birth pains") also appears in Isa 13:8 and Jer 22:23. The double expression captures the Greek hendiadys (*odinousa kai basanizomene*, "travailing and being tormented"). The language strongly evokes the messianic birth pangs tradition of Isa 66:7-8.

### 12:4 — va-yityatzev ha-tannin lifnei ha-ishah
- **Hebrew**: *va-yityatzev ha-tannin lifnei ha-ishah*
- **Note**: *Va-yityatzev* (hithpael of *y-tz-v*, "he stationed himself") is excellent biblical Hebrew. The form appears in 1 Sam 17:16 (Goliath presenting himself), Ex 8:16 (Moses confronting Pharaoh), and Job 1:6, 2:1 (the *satan* presenting himself before God). The latter parallel is particularly significant — the same verb form is used for the accuser's stance before the divine court.

### 12:7 — Mikha'el hu u-mal'akhav nilchamim
- **Hebrew**: *Mikha'el hu u-mal'akhav nilchamim ba-tannin*
- **Note**: The syntax *hu u-mal'akhav* ("he and his angels") follows classical Hebrew war narrative patterns (cf. Josh 10:7, Judg 4:10, 2 Sam 10:17). The niphal participle *nilchamim* ("fighting") is standard military Hebrew.

### 12:9 — seducit universum orbem / ha-madiach tevel kulah
- **Hebrew**: *ha-madiach tevel kulah* ("who leads astray the entire world")
- **Note**: *Madiach* (hiphil participle of *n-d-ch*, "to lead astray/seduce") is powerful biblical vocabulary. It is the term used in Deut 13:14 for those who lead a city into idolatry (*anashim venei veliyyal... va-yaddichu et yoshvei iram*). The legal/covenantal background of *hidiach* — specifically the enticement to apostasy — makes this a theologically precise rendering. *Tevel* ("world/inhabited earth") appears in Isa 14:17, 26:9, Ps 19:5, 93:1.

### 12:14 — kanfei ha-nesher ha-gadol
- **Hebrew**: *shtei kanfei ha-nesher ha-gadol*
- **Note**: *Kanfei nesher* ("eagle's wings") echoes Ex 19:4 (*va-essa etkhem al kanfei nesharim*, "and I bore you on eagles' wings") — God's own description of the Exodus deliverance. Deut 32:11 (*ke-nesher ya'ir qinno al gozalav yerachef yifros kenafav yiqqachehu yissa'ehu al evroto*, "as an eagle stirs up its nest, hovers over its young, spreads its wings, takes them, bears them on its pinions") also resonates. The Woman's flight into the wilderness on eagle's wings is thus framed as a new Exodus.

## Other Notes

### Red-Text Candidates
- **12:10**: The heavenly voice declares *attah ba'ah yeshuat Eloheinu ve-uzzo u-malkhuto u-memshelet meshicho* — this is a heavenly proclamation, possibly spoken by a collective angelic/saintly voice, not directly by a member of the Trinity. Not red-text.
- **No direct speech of the Holy Trinity** occurs in this chapter. The chapter narrates events rather than quoting divine speech.

### Marian Typology (Catholic Significance)
In Catholic tradition, the Woman of Revelation 12 is identified as the Blessed Virgin Mary, the Church, and Israel — all three levels of meaning operating simultaneously. The imagery of 12:1 (clothed with the sun, moon under feet, crown of twelve stars) is the basis for the iconographic tradition of the Immaculate Conception. The flight to the wilderness (12:6, 14) parallels the Flight into Egypt (Mt 2:13-15). The Catholic liturgical reading emphasizes the Woman as Mary who bears Christ (*ben zakhar*, "a male son") and whose *other* offspring (*yeter zar'ah*, "the rest of her seed") are the faithful Church (12:17).

### OT Allusion Density
- **Gen 1:21**: *Tannin* — primordial sea creature
- **Gen 3:1, 15**: *Ha-nachash ha-qadmoni* — the ancient serpent; enmity between serpent and woman's seed
- **Ex 19:4, Deut 32:11**: Eagle's wings — Exodus deliverance
- **Num 16:32**: Earth opening its mouth to swallow — Korah
- **Ps 2:9**: Rod of iron — messianic rule (via LXX reading)
- **Ps 104:2**: Clothed in light — the sun as garment
- **Isa 27:1**: *Leviathan/tannin* — eschatological dragon slaying
- **Isa 66:7-8**: Messianic birth pangs
- **Dan 7:25, 12:7**: Time, times, and half a time
- **Job 1:6, 2:1**: Satan presenting himself before God
- **Zech 3:1**: Satan as accuser

### Genesis 3:15 Fulfillment
Rev 12 is the most explicit NT echo of the Protoevangelium (Gen 3:15). The Woman, the dragon/serpent, the male child, and the enmity between the serpent and her seed — all elements of Gen 3:15 are present. The Hebrew translation heightens this by using *nachash* for the serpent and *zar'ah* ("her seed") in 12:17, the exact vocabulary of Gen 3:15 (*ve-eivah ashit beinekha u-vein ha-ishah u-vein zar'akha u-vein zar'ah*, "and I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her seed").
