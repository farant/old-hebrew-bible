# Revelation Chapter 4 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 11 verses align across all three sources (Vulgate, SBLGNT, Delitzsch).

## Content Alignment

### 4:1 — "ostium" vs. "thyra" vs. "sha'ar"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *ostium* ("door/gate") and the Greek has *thyra* ("door"). The Hebrew renders this as *sha'ar* ("gate"), which in biblical Hebrew typically refers to a larger city or temple gate rather than a domestic door (*delet* or *petach*). This is a reasonable interpretive choice given the heavenly context.
- **Vulgate**: *ecce ostium apertum in caelo*
- **Hebrew**: *sha'ar niftach ba-shamayim*
- **Note**: *Sha'ar* echoes Gen 28:17 (*sha'ar ha-shamayim*, "gate of heaven") from Jacob's ladder vision. The Delitzsch choice creates a subtle OT allusion. Good.

### 4:2 — "sedes" vs. "thronos" vs. "kisse"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate uses *sedes* ("seat/throne") interchangeably in vv. 2-4, while the Greek consistently uses *thronos* ("throne"). The Hebrew consistently uses *kisse* ("throne"), which is standard biblical Hebrew (1 Kgs 22:19, Isa 6:1, Ezek 1:26). All three traditions align in substance.
- **Note**: No divergence. Good alignment.

### 4:3 — Gemstone identifications
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *jaspidis et sardinis* ("jasper and sardius"), the Greek has *iaspidi kai sardio*. The Hebrew renders these as *yashfeh va-odem*, which directly correspond to two of the twelve stones on the High Priest's breastplate (Ex 28:17-20). The emerald (*smaragdinae* / *smaragdino*) is rendered *bareqet*, also a breastplate stone (Ex 28:17).
- **Vulgate**: *similis erat aspectui lapidis jaspidis, et sardinis... smaragdinae*
- **Hebrew**: *yashfeh va-odem... bareqet*
- **Note**: Outstanding. All three gemstones are mapped to their Exodus breastplate equivalents. This connects the heavenly vision to the Aaronic priesthood, reinforcing the liturgical symbolism of the throne room.

### 4:5 — "fulgura, et voces, et tonitrua" word order
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *fulgura, et voces, et tonitrua* ("lightning, voices, thunder"). The Greek has *astrapai kai phonai kai brontai* (same order: lightning, voices, thunder). The Hebrew has *beraqim ur'amim veqolot* ("lightning and thunder and voices"), reversing the order of the last two elements.
- **Note**: Minor reordering. The Hebrew follows a more natural Hebrew grouping of natural phenomena together (*beraqim ur'amim*) before *qolot*. This echoes Ex 19:16 (*qolot uveraqim*, "thunder and lightning" at Sinai) but reverses the Exodus order.

### 4:8 — "qui erat, et qui est, et qui venturus est" — reversed order
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads *qui erat, et qui est, et qui venturus est* ("who was, and who is, and who is to come"). The Greek reads *ho en kai ho on kai ho erchomenos* ("who was and who is and who is coming"). The Hebrew has *hayah vehoveh veyavo* ("was and is and will come"). All three follow the same past-present-future sequence, aligning well.
- **Note**: The Hebrew *hayah vehoveh veyavo* plays on the root *h-y-h* exactly as in 1:4. Good consistency. The entire Trisagion (*qadosh qadosh qadosh*) directly echoes Isa 6:3, linking the heavenly throne room to Isaiah's temple vision. The combination of the Trisagion with *YHVH Elohim Tzeva'ot* replicates the Isaiah text almost verbatim.

### 4:9 — "benedictionem" vs. "eucharistian" vs. "todah"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *benedictionem* ("blessing"), but the Greek has *eucharistian* ("thanksgiving"). The Hebrew renders this as *todah* ("thanksgiving"), siding with the Greek meaning rather than the Vulgate.
- **Vulgate**: *gloriam, et honorem, et benedictionem*
- **Greek**: *doxan kai timen kai eucharistian*
- **Hebrew**: *kavod vehadar vetodah*
- **Note**: *Benedictio* and *eucharistia* are semantically different. The Hebrew *todah* ("thanksgiving," cf. Lev 7:12, Ps 50:14) correctly renders the Greek *eucharistia* but diverges from the Vulgate *benedictio*. For Vulgate conformity, *berakhah* ("blessing") would be expected.

### 4:11 — "virtutem" vs. "dynamin" vs. "oz"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *gloriam, et honorem, et virtutem* ("glory, honor, and power"). The Greek has *doxan kai timen kai dynamin* ("glory, honor, and power"). The Hebrew has *kavod vehadar va'oz* ("glory, splendor, and strength"). The Hebrew *hadar* ("splendor/majesty") is not a standard rendering of *honor/timeh*. More precise would be *kavod* for glory and *yaqar* for honor.
- **Note**: *Hadar* (cf. Ps 96:6, 1 Chr 16:27) carries overtones of divine majesty rather than "honor" in the sense of *timeh*. This is an interpretive enrichment rather than strict translation.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 4:1 — Sinai theophany language
- **Hebrew**: *qol shofar midabber elai* ("voice of a trumpet speaking to me")
- **Note**: Excellent. The trumpet-voice connection echoes Ex 19:16, 19 and directly reprises Rev 1:10. *Shofar* (ram's horn) rather than *chatzotzrah* (silver trumpet) is the correct choice, as the shofar is associated with divine theophanies (Ex 19:16, Josh 6:4-5).

### 4:6 — chayyot for "animalia/zoa"
- **Hebrew**: *arba chayyot mele'ot einayim*
- **Note**: Outstanding. *Chayyot* ("living creatures") is the exact term from Ezek 1:5 (*demut arba chayyot*). This is not merely a translation choice but a deliberate identification of the Revelation creatures with Ezekiel's *chayyot*. The Greek *zoa* and Latin *animalia* are generic; the Hebrew *chayyot* is theologically precise.

### 4:6 — yam zekhukhit
- **Hebrew**: *yam zekhukhit ke'ein ha-qarach*
- **Note**: *Yam zekhukhit* ("sea of glass") is a creative compound. *Zekhukhit* ("glass") is attested in Job 28:17 (*lo ya'arkenah zahav u-zekhukhit*). *Ke'ein ha-qarach* ("like the appearance of crystal/ice") echoes Ezek 1:22 (*ke'ein ha-qarach ha-nora*, "like the terrible crystal"). Excellent OT allusion.

### 4:7 — Four living creatures
- **Hebrew**: *ke'aryeh... ke-shor... kifnei adam... ke-nesher me'ofef*
- **Note**: The four creature faces (lion, ox, human, eagle) directly correspond to Ezek 1:10, though in a different order. Ezekiel has human-lion-ox-eagle (all on each creature), while Revelation assigns one face per creature. The Hebrew vocabulary matches Ezekiel precisely: *aryeh*, *shor*, *adam*, *nesher*.

### 4:8 — shesh kenafayim
- **Hebrew**: *shesh kenafayim shesh kenafayim le-echat*
- **Note**: The repetition *shesh kenafayim shesh kenafayim* ("six wings, six wings") has a liturgical cadence. Six wings connects to Isa 6:2 (the seraphim each had six wings), while the *chayyot* designation connects to Ezek 1 (where the creatures have four wings). Revelation combines Isaiah's seraphim and Ezekiel's chayyot into a single vision. The Hebrew captures this dual allusion.

### 4:11 — bara'ta for "creasti"
- **Hebrew**: *attah bara'ta ha-kol*
- **Note**: *Bara* ("create") is the supreme creative verb in biblical Hebrew, used exclusively of divine action (Gen 1:1, Isa 45:7). The doxology echoes Gen 1:1 and Neh 9:6.

## Other Notes

### OT Allusion Density
Revelation 4 is essentially a composite of three major OT throne-room visions:
- **Isa 6:1-4**: Throne, Trisagion, seraphim with six wings
- **Ezek 1:4-28**: Four living creatures (*chayyot*), crystal expanse, throne above, lightning
- **1 Kgs 22:19**: The LORD seated on His throne with the heavenly host
- **Ex 19:16-19**: Thunder, lightning, trumpet, divine voice

The Delitzsch Hebrew translation handles these convergences with exceptional skill, using the precise vocabulary from each OT source text.

### Theological Note — 4:8
The Trisagion (*qadosh qadosh qadosh YHVH Elohim Tzeva'ot*) is a verbatim replication of Isa 6:3. In the original Isaiah context, this is directed to YHWH. The Revelation context applies it to the One seated on the throne, maintaining the same monotheistic theology. Catholic liturgical tradition identifies this as the *Sanctus* of the Mass, linking heavenly and earthly worship.
