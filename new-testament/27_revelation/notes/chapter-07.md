# Revelation Chapter 7 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 17 verses align across all three sources (Vulgate, SBLGNT, Delitzsch).

## Content Alignment

### 7:1 — "angulos" vs. "gonias" vs. "kanfot"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *quatuor angulos terrae* ("four corners of the earth"), the Greek *tessaras gonias tes ges* ("four corners of the earth"). The Hebrew has *arba kanfot ha-aretz* ("four wings/corners of the earth"), which is a direct quotation of Isa 11:12 (*me-arba kanfot ha-aretz*, "from the four corners of the earth"). Also cf. Ezek 7:2.
- **Note**: Excellent. The Hebrew uses the exact OT phrasing. *Kanfot* literally means "wings" but idiomatically means "corners/extremities."

### 7:4 — The number 144,000
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree on the number: *centum quadraginta quatuor millia* / *hekaton tesserakonta tessares chiliades* / *me'ah elef ve-arba'im ve-arba'ah alef*. The Hebrew counting syntax is correct.
- **Note**: No divergence.

### 7:5-8 — Tribal list
- **Issue**: The tribal order and names in Revelation differ from all OT tribal lists. Dan is omitted and Manasseh replaces him. The Hebrew names follow standard biblical Hebrew spellings: *Yehudah, Re'uven, Gad, Asher, Naftali, Menashe, Shim'on, Levi, Yissakhar, Zevulun, Yosef, Binyamin*.
- **Note**: The Hebrew spellings are correct biblical forms. The placement of Judah first (rather than Reuben as firstborn) is significant — it reflects the Messianic primacy of the tribe of Judah (Gen 49:8-12).

### 7:9 — "turbam magnam" vs. "ochlos polys"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *turbam magnam* ("great crowd") and Greek *ochlos polys* ("great multitude") agree. The Hebrew *hamon rav* ("great multitude") is well-chosen — *hamon* appears in Ezek 39:11 and Dan 10:6 for a great throng or multitude.
- **Note**: Good alignment.

### 7:9 — "palmae" vs. "phoinikes" vs. "kappot temarim"
- **Issue**: All three agree on palm branches. The Hebrew *kappot temarim* ("palm fronds") directly echoes Lev 23:40 (*kappot temarim*) from the Feast of Tabernacles (Sukkot) liturgy. This creates a strong connection between the heavenly worship and the Festival of Booths.
- **Note**: Excellent OT allusion. The Sukkot connection is liturgically significant in Jewish-Christian interpretation.

### 7:14 — "laverunt... dealbaverunt" vs. "eplynan... eleukanan"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate uses two verbs: *laverunt* ("washed") and *dealbaverunt* ("whitened/made white"). The Greek likewise: *eplynan* ("washed") and *eleukanan* ("made white"). The Hebrew has *vayekhabsu et-simlotam vayalbinim be-dam ha-seh* ("they washed their garments and whitened them in the blood of the Lamb"), preserving both verbs. All three align.
- **Note**: Good. The paradox of whitening garments in blood is preserved in all three traditions.

### 7:15 — "habitabit super illos" vs. "skenosei ep' autous"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *habitabit super illos* ("will dwell over them"). The Greek has *skenosei ep autous* ("will tabernacle/tent over them"). The Hebrew has *yitten mishkano aleihem* ("will set his tabernacle/dwelling over them"), which captures the *skene/mishkan* ("tabernacle") connection that the Vulgate *habitabit* obscures.
- **Vulgate**: *habitabit super illos*
- **Greek**: *skenosei ep' autous*
- **Hebrew**: *yitten mishkano aleihem*
- **Note**: The Hebrew is more precise than the Vulgate here, preserving the tabernacle/Shekinah theology. *Mishkan* directly connects to the Exodus tabernacle (Ex 25:9, 26:1ff.) and to *Shekinah* (the divine dwelling presence). The Greek *skenoo* is itself derived from Semitic *sh-k-n*.

### 7:16-17 — Isaiah 49:10 quotation
- **Issue**: These verses are a near-quotation of Isa 49:10 (*lo yir'avu velo yitzma'u velo yakkem sharav va-shamesh*, "they shall not hunger nor thirst, neither shall heat nor sun strike them"). The Hebrew captures this: *lo yir'avu od velo yitzma'u velo yakkem shemesh vesharav*.
- **Vulgate**: *non esurient, neque sitient amplius, nec cadet super illos sol, neque ullus aestus*
- **Hebrew**: *lo yir'avu od velo yitzma'u velo yakkem shemesh vesharav*
- **Note**: Outstanding. The Hebrew replicates Isaiah 49:10 almost verbatim, using the same vocabulary. The word order (*shemesh vesharav*) matches Isaiah precisely.

### 7:17 — "reget" vs. "poimanei" vs. "yir'em"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has *reget illos* ("will rule/guide them"). The Greek *poimanei autous* ("will shepherd them"). The Hebrew *yir'em* ("will shepherd them") follows the Greek pastoral image. *Ra'ah* ("shepherd") is the standard OT shepherd verb (Ps 23:1, Isa 40:11, Ezek 34:23).
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the Greek's "shepherd" over the Vulgate's "rule." The shepherd image is theologically richer, connecting to Ps 23:1 (*YHVH ro'i*) and Ezek 34:23 (*ro'eh echad*). However, the Vulgate *reget* does carry the sense of "guide/lead" which is the alternate meaning of *poimaino*.

### 7:17 — "absterget Deus omnem lacrimam"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree that God will wipe away every tear. The Hebrew *u-machah Elohim kol dim'ah me-eineihem* is a near-quotation of Isa 25:8 (*u-machah Adonai YHVH dim'ah me-al kol-panim*, "and the Lord GOD will wipe away tears from all faces").
- **Note**: The Hebrew echoes Isaiah 25:8 precisely. This allusion recurs at Rev 21:4.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 7:2 — mizrach-shemesh
- **Hebrew**: *mi-mizrach-shemesh* ("from the rising of the sun")
- **Note**: *Mizrach shemesh* is a classic biblical Hebrew expression for "east" (Judg 11:18, Isa 41:25, 45:6). The longer form *mizrach shemesh* (rather than simply *mizrach*) adds a poetic/archaic register appropriate for the apocalyptic genre.

### 7:3 — chatamnu for "signemus"
- **Hebrew**: *ad im chatamnu et avdei Eloheinu al mitzechotam*
- **Note**: *Chatam* ("seal") with the seal pressed on the forehead echoes Ezek 9:4, where a *tav* (mark) is placed on the foreheads of the righteous in Jerusalem. The verbal connection is strong: Ezek 9:4 uses *hitvita tav* while Revelation uses *chatam*, but the concept of a protective divine mark on the forehead is the same.

### 7:10 — ha-yeshu'ah
- **Hebrew**: *ha-yeshu'ah le-Eloheinu*
- **Note**: *Yeshu'ah* ("salvation") is a loaded term: it is the etymological root of the name *Yeshua* (Jesus). The cry "Salvation to our God!" in Hebrew implicitly invokes the name of Christ. Cf. Ps 3:9 (*la-YHVH ha-yeshu'ah*, "salvation belongs to the LORD"), Ex 14:13.

### 7:14 — ha-tzarah ha-gedolah
- **Hebrew**: *ha-ba'im min ha-tzarah ha-gedolah*
- **Note**: *Ha-tzarah ha-gedolah* ("the great tribulation") echoes Dan 12:1 (*ve-hayetah et tzarah asher lo nihyetah*, "and there shall be a time of trouble such as never was"). *Tzarah* is the standard OT term for distress/tribulation (Ps 22:12, 34:18, 46:2).

### 7:15 — mesharatim for "serviunt"
- **Hebrew**: *u-mesharatim oto be-heikhalo yomam va-lailah*
- **Note**: *Mesharet* ("minister/serve") is the priestly/Levitical service verb (Ex 28:35, Num 3:6, 1 Sam 2:11). It denotes liturgical service rather than slave service (*avad*). This captures the heavenly priesthood of the redeemed.

## Other Notes

### OT Allusion Density
Chapter 7 draws primarily from:
- **Isa 11:12**: Four corners of the earth — v. 1
- **Ezek 9:4-6**: Sealing the foreheads of the righteous — v. 3
- **Lev 23:40**: Palm branches at Sukkot — v. 9
- **Isa 49:10**: No hunger, thirst, or heat — v. 16
- **Isa 25:8**: Wiping away tears — v. 17
- **Ps 23:1-2**: Shepherd leading to waters — v. 17
- **Ezek 34:23**: One shepherd — v. 17

### Theological Note — 7:15
The Hebrew *mishkan* ("tabernacle/dwelling") for God dwelling over His people creates a Shekinah theology connection. The divine presence that dwelt in the Tabernacle (*mishkan*) and Temple now dwells directly over the redeemed. This is the fulfillment of the covenant formula: "I will dwell among them" (Ex 25:8, 29:45, Lev 26:11-12, Ezek 37:27).
