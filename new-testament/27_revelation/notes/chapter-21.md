# Revelation Chapter 21 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 27 verses align across all three sources (Vulgate, SBLGNT, Delitzsch). No versification discrepancies.

### 21:2 — "Ego Joannes" — Vulgate addition
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads *Et ego Joannes vidi* ("And I John saw"), explicitly naming John. The SBLGNT reads simply *kai ten polin ten hagian... eidon* ("and the holy city... I saw"). The Hebrew *va-ereh ve-hinneh ha-ir ha-qedoshah* ("and I saw, behold, the holy city") does not include John's name, following the Greek.
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the Greek against the Vulgate here. The Vulgate's insertion of *ego Joannes* is a TR/Byzantine tradition addition. For strict Vulgate conformity, the Hebrew could include *va-ani Yochanan ra'iti*.

## Content Alignment

### 21:1 — "shamayim chadashim ve-eretz chadashah"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree. The Hebrew *shamayim chadashim ve-eretz chadashah* ("new heavens and a new earth") is verbatim from Isa 65:17 (*hinni bore shamayim chadashim ve-eretz chadashah*, "behold, I am creating new heavens and a new earth") and Isa 66:22 (*ki ka-asher ha-shamayim ha-chadashim ve-ha-aretz ha-chadashah*, "for as the new heavens and the new earth").
- **Note**: The Hebrew translation preserves the Isaiah quotation exactly. This is the defining OT text for the new creation. Outstanding.

### 21:2 — "ka-kallah ha-mequshetet le-va'alah"
- **Issue**: All three traditions describe the city as a bride adorned for her husband. The Hebrew *ka-kallah ha-mequshetet le-va'alah* ("like the bride adorned for her husband") uses *mequshetet* ("adorned/decorated"), from the root *q-sh-t*, found in Jer 4:30 (*ki titqashti zahav*, "though you adorn yourself with gold") and Jer 31:4 (*od ta'di tuppayikh*, related bridal imagery).
- **Note**: The bridal imagery connects to Isa 61:10 (*ka-kallah ta'deh kheleyah*, "as a bride adorns herself with her jewels") and Isa 62:5 (*u-mesos chatan al kallah*, "as the bridegroom rejoices over the bride").

### 21:3 — "mishkan Elohim im benei ha-adam" — Covenant formula
- **Issue**: All three traditions present the covenant formula. The Hebrew *hinneh mishkan Elohim im benei ha-adam ve-shakhan betokham ve-hemmah yihyu lo le-am ve-hu ha-Elohim yihyeh ittam [Eloheihem]* ("Behold, the dwelling of God is with the sons of man, and He will dwell among them, and they will be His people, and He, God, will be with them [their God]").
- **Note**: This verse is the consummation of the entire covenant trajectory:
  - *Mishkan* ("tabernacle/dwelling") — the Tabernacle of Ex 25:8-9 (*ve-asu li miqdash ve-shakhanti betokham*, "let them make me a sanctuary, that I may dwell among them")
  - *Ve-shakhan betokham* — verbatim from Ex 25:8, Ezek 43:7, 9, Zech 2:14-15
  - *Ve-hemmah yihyu li le-am* — the covenant formula of Jer 31:33, 32:38, Ezek 37:27, Zech 8:8
  - *Ve-hu yihyeh lahem le-Elohim* — Jer 31:33, Ezek 37:27
  - The bracketed *Eloheihem* ("their God") indicates a variant reading present in the Vulgate (*eorum Deus*) but uncertain in the Greek critical text.
- **Significance**: This single verse gathers the entire OT covenant tradition — from the Tabernacle to the prophetic new covenant — into its fulfillment. The Hebrew makes every allusion transparent.

### 21:4 — "u-machah Elohim kol dim'ah me-eineihem"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree. The Hebrew *u-machah Elohim kol dim'ah me-eineihem* ("and God will wipe away every tear from their eyes") is from Isa 25:8 (*u-machah Adonai YHVH dim'ah me-al kol panim*, "and the Lord GOD will wipe away tears from all faces").
- **Note**: Near-verbatim from Isaiah 25:8. The Isaiah passage continues with the abolition of death — *billa ha-mavet la-netzach* ("He will swallow up death forever") — which is alluded to in the next clause: *ve-ha-mavet lo yihyeh od* ("and death will be no more"). This combines Isa 25:8 with the eschatological promise.

### 21:5 — "hinneni oseh ha-kol chadash" — Divine speech
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Ecce nova facio omnia* ("Behold, I make all things new"), Greek *Idou kaina poio panta*. The Hebrew *hinneni oseh ha-kol chadash* ("Behold, I am making everything new").
- **Note**: *Hinneni oseh* uses the prophetic divine self-announcement formula (cf. Isa 43:19: *hinneni oseh chadashah*, "Behold, I am doing a new thing"). The speaker is God the Father seated on the throne. This is a direct divine declaration and a strong red-text candidate.

### 21:5 — "amittim ve-ne'emanim" for "fidelissima... et vera"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *fidelissima sunt et vera* ("most faithful and true"), Greek *pistoi kai alethinoi* ("faithful and true"). The Hebrew *amittim ve-ne'emanim* ("true and faithful") reverses the order from the Vulgate/Greek (faithful-and-true becomes true-and-faithful).
- **Note**: Minor stylistic reversal. Both adjectives are excellent biblical Hebrew. The reversal may be for euphony.

### 21:6 — "ha-yoh nihyatah" for "Factum est"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Factum est* ("It is done"), Greek *Gegonan* ("They have come to pass"). The Hebrew *ha-yoh nihyatah* ("it has indeed come to pass") uses the infinitive absolute construction (*ha-yoh* + finite verb) for emphasis.
- **Note**: The infinitive absolute construction is a hallmark of classical biblical Hebrew emphasis (cf. Gen 2:17: *mot tamut*, "you shall surely die"; Gen 18:18: *hayo yihyeh*, "shall surely become"). Good biblical style.

### 21:6 — "ma'yan mayim chayyim chinnam" for "fontem aquae vitae gratis"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree: the thirsty will drink from the fountain of living water freely. The Hebrew *mi-ma'yan mayim chayyim chinnam* ("from the spring of living waters, freely") echoes Jer 2:13 (*me-qor mayim chayyim*, "the fountain of living waters") and Isa 55:1 (*lekhu la-mayim... bilkhlu be-lo khesef u-velo mechir*, "come to the waters... buy without money and without price").
- **Note**: The *mayim chayyim* ("living waters") is a major OT motif: Gen 26:19, Jer 2:13, 17:13, Zech 14:8, Ezek 47:1-12. The Hebrew *chinnam* ("freely/gratis") echoes Isa 55:1. Excellent.

### 21:7 — "ehyeh lo le-Elohim ve-hu yihyeh li le-ven" — Adoption formula
- **Issue**: All three traditions present the divine adoption formula. The Hebrew *ehyeh lo le-Elohim ve-hu yihyeh li le-ven* ("I will be to him God, and he will be to me a son") echoes 2 Sam 7:14 (*ani ehyeh lo le-av ve-hu yihyeh li le-ven*, "I will be to him a father, and he will be to me a son") — the Davidic covenant promise to Solomon.
- **Note**: The 2 Samuel 7 allusion is precise. The Davidic covenant, originally given to Solomon, is now extended to every overcomer. The Hebrew preserves the covenantal *le-* construction (*le-Elohim*, *le-ven*) that marks the OT covenant formula.

### 21:8 — "rakkei ha-lev" for "timidis"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *timidis* ("the timid/cowardly"), Greek *tois deilois* ("the cowardly"). The Hebrew *rakkei ha-lev* ("the soft/tender of heart") echoes Deut 20:8 (*mi ha-ish ha-yare ve-rakh ha-levav*, "who is the man who is fearful and fainthearted?") — the dismissal of cowards before battle.
- **Note**: The Deuteronomy 20 military context is apt: those who lack courage for the spiritual battle are excluded from the city. Good OT allusion.

### 21:8 — Vice list alignment
- **Issue**: The Hebrew vice list: *rakkei ha-lev* (cowardly), *asher einam ma'aminim* (unbelieving), *ha-mego'alim* (the abominable), *ha-meratzhim* (murderers), *ha-zonim* (fornicators), *ha-mekhashfim* (sorcerers), *ovdei ha-elilim* (idolaters), *kol ha-meshaqrim* (all liars). This matches the Vulgate sequence and content.
- **Note**: All terms are good biblical or classical Hebrew. *Mekhashfim* (sorcerers, Ex 7:11, 22:17), *ovdei ha-elilim* (idol-worshippers, calque on numerous OT expressions).

### 21:10 — "ha-ir ha-qedoshah Yerushalayim ha-gedolah"
- **Issue**: The Hebrew adds *ha-gedolah* ("the great") to the city's name. The Vulgate has *civitatem sanctam Jerusalem* ("the holy city Jerusalem") without "great." The SBLGNT similarly has *ten polin ten hagian Ierousalem*. The Hebrew's *ha-gedolah* ("the great") is an addition not present in either source.
- **Note**: This is an expansion by Delitzsch. Neither the Vulgate nor the Greek include "great" here. For Vulgate conformity, *ha-gedolah* should be omitted.

### 21:12-13 — Gates and tribes
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree on the twelve gates with twelve angels and twelve tribal names, and the directional arrangement (three gates per side: east, north, south, west). The Hebrew follows Ezek 48:30-34, which lists the gates of the eschatological city by tribal assignment.
- **Note**: The gate arrangement matches Ezek 48:30-34 in having three gates per side, though the specific tribal assignments are not given here. The Hebrew uses the standard directional terms: *mizrach* (east), *tzafon* (north), *negev* (south), *ma'arav* (west) — all standard OT geography.

### 21:14 — "shelichei ha-seh" for "apostolorum Agni"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *apostolorum Agni* ("of the apostles of the Lamb"), Greek *ton dodeka apostolon tou arniou*. The Hebrew *shelichei ha-seh* ("the emissaries of the Lamb") uses *shaliach* ("emissary/sent one"), which is the Hebrew cognate of Greek *apostolos*.
- **Note**: *Shaliach* is attested in post-biblical Hebrew and Aramaic. The biblical equivalent would be the verb *sh-l-ch* ("send") in participial form, but *shaliach* as a noun meaning "emissary" does appear conceptually in OT passages like 1 Kgs 14:6 (Ahijah as God's messenger). Functional and clear.

### 21:14 — "ha-seh" for "Agni" (consistent throughout)
- **Issue**: The Hebrew consistently renders "the Lamb" as *ha-seh* ("the lamb/sheep") throughout Revelation. The Vulgate *Agnus* and Greek *to arnion* (diminutive: "little lamb") are both rendered by the single Hebrew term.
- **Note**: *Seh* is the term used for the Passover lamb in Ex 12:3-5 (*se le-veit avot seh la-bayit*, "a lamb for each household"). By using *seh* consistently, Delitzsch connects every Revelation reference to the Lamb back to the Passover. This is an excellent and consistent translation decision.

### 21:16 — "ris" for "stadia"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *stadia duodecim millia* ("twelve thousand stadia"), Greek *stadious dodeka chiliadon*. The Hebrew uses *ris* as a transliteration of the Greek measurement unit rather than converting to a biblical Hebrew measure like *ammah* (cubit) or *parasah* (parasang).
- **Note**: *Ris* is a Greco-Latin loanword (Greek *stadion* > *ris*). It appears in later rabbinic Hebrew. Using the foreign measurement preserves the scale without introducing conversion inaccuracies.

### 21:18-20 — Gemstone list
- **Issue**: The twelve foundation stones use a mix of biblical Hebrew gemstone names and transliterations. The Hebrew uses: *yashfeh* (jasper — Ex 28:20), *sappir* (sapphire — Ex 28:18), *shevo* (agate — Ex 28:19), *bareqet* (emerald — Ex 28:17), *yahalom* (diamond/onyx — Ex 28:18), *odem* (carnelian — Ex 28:17), *tarshish* (beryl/chrysolite — Ex 28:20), *shoham* (onyx — Gen 2:12, Ex 28:20), *pitdah* (topaz — Ex 28:17), *nofekh* (turquoise/carbuncle — Ex 28:18), *leshem* (jacinth — Ex 28:19), *achlamah* (amethyst — Ex 28:19).
- **Note**: Delitzsch maps the twelve foundation stones to the twelve stones of the high priest's breastplate (*choshen*, Ex 28:17-20). The identifications do not match one-to-one with modern gemological identifications, but the translation achieves something theologically important: the New Jerusalem's foundations are described using the vocabulary of the priestly breastplate, connecting the eschatological city to the Aaronic priesthood. This is a deliberate interpretive choice that highlights the priestly character of the New Jerusalem.

### 21:22 — "YHVH Elohim Tzevaot hu ve-ha-seh heikhalah"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Dominus enim Deus omnipotens templum illius est, et Agnus* ("For the Lord God Almighty is the temple of it, and the Lamb"). The Hebrew *YHVH Elohim Tzevaot hu ve-ha-seh heikhalah* ("the LORD God of hosts — He and the Lamb — is her temple").
- **Note**: The absence of a physical temple and the identification of God and the Lamb as the temple is theologically momentous. The Hebrew *heikhal* ("temple/palace") is the standard term for Solomon's Temple (1 Kgs 6:3, 5, 17, etc.) and the heavenly temple (Ps 11:4, Hab 2:20). Its replacement by the divine presence fulfills Jer 3:16-17 (the ark will no longer be needed, "they shall no more say, 'The ark of the covenant of the LORD'... Jerusalem shall be called the throne of the LORD").

### 21:23 — "kevod Elohim he'ir lah ve-nerah hu ha-seh"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree: the glory of God illuminates the city, and the Lamb is its lamp. The Hebrew *kevod Elohim he'ir lah* ("the glory of God illuminated her") echoes Isa 60:1 (*ki va orekh u-khvod YHVH alayikh zarach*, "for your light has come, and the glory of the LORD has risen upon you") and Isa 60:19 (*lo yihyeh lakh od ha-shemesh le-or yomam... ki YHVH yihyeh lakh le-or olam*, "the sun shall no longer be your light by day... for the LORD will be your everlasting light").
- **Note**: The entire section (vv. 23-26) is an extended fulfillment of Isaiah 60. The Hebrew vocabulary is shared with the Isaiah source text, making the connection explicit.

### 21:24 — "ve-ha-goyim yelkhu le-orah"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree: the nations walk by the city's light. The Hebrew echoes Isa 60:3 (*ve-halekhu goyim le-orekh*, "and nations shall walk by your light"). The Hebrew is nearly verbatim.
- **Note**: Outstanding alignment with the OT source text.

### 21:25 — "u-she'areha yomam lo yissageru ki laylah lo yihyeh sham"
- **Issue**: All three agree. The Hebrew echoes Isa 60:11 (*u-fittechu she'arayikh tamid yomam va-laylah lo yissageru*, "your gates shall be open continually, day and night they shall not be shut"). The Hebrew adaptation notes that there is no night at all (*laylah lo yihyeh sham*), going beyond Isaiah's "day and night" to eliminate night entirely.
- **Note**: Isaiah 60:11 is adapted: in Isaiah, the gates are open day and night; in the New Jerusalem, there is no night at all. The Hebrew preserves Isaiah's vocabulary while noting the escalation.

### 21:27 — "kol tamei" for "aliquod coinquinatum"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *aliquod coinquinatum* ("anything defiled"), Greek *pan koinon* ("anything common/unclean"). The Hebrew *kol tamei* ("anything unclean") uses the standard Levitical purity term *tamei* ("ritually unclean," Lev 11-15 passim).
- **Note**: The use of *tamei* frames entrance to the New Jerusalem in terms of Levitical purity. This connects to Isa 52:1 (*lo yosif yavo vakh od arel ve-tamei*, "the uncircumcised and the unclean shall enter you no more") and Ezek 44:9. Good.

### 21:27 — "shel ha-seh" — modern Hebrew construction
- **Issue**: The Hebrew *sefer ha-chayyim shel ha-seh* ("the book of life of the Lamb") uses the *shel* construction for possession. While *shel* appears in late biblical Hebrew (Song 1:1: *shir ha-shirim asher li-Shlomoh*; Eccl 1:1), the *shel* possessive particle is more characteristic of Mishnaic Hebrew than classical biblical usage. The standard biblical construction would be *sefer chayyei ha-seh* or *sefer ha-chayyim la-seh*.
- **Note**: Minor. This is one of several places where Delitzsch's Hebrew leans toward later usage.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 21:1 — shamayim chadashim ve-eretz chadashah
- **Hebrew**: *shamayim chadashim ve-eretz chadashah*
- **Note**: Verbatim from Isa 65:17, 66:22. See Content Alignment above. This is as close to a direct OT quotation as a translation can achieve.

### 21:2 — ha-ir ha-qedoshah Yerushalayim ha-chadashah
- **Hebrew**: *ha-ir ha-qedoshah Yerushalayim ha-chadashah*
- **Note**: *Ir ha-qodesh* / *ir ha-qedoshah* ("the holy city") appears in Isa 48:2, 52:1, Neh 11:1, 18, Dan 9:24. The New Jerusalem concept draws on all of these.

### 21:3 — mishkan / ve-shakhan
- **Hebrew**: *mishkan Elohim... ve-shakhan betokham*
- **Note**: The wordplay between *mishkan* (noun: "tabernacle/dwelling") and *shakhan* (verb: "dwell") exploits the shared root *sh-k-n*. God's *mishkan* is with humanity because He *shokhen* (dwells) among them. This root wordplay is foundational to the Tabernacle theology of Exodus and runs through Ezekiel's temple vision.

### 21:11 — mav'heqet ke-ein ha-qarach
- **Hebrew**: *ke-even yashfeh ha-mav'heqet ke-ein ha-qarach*
- **Note**: *Mav'heqet* ("gleaming/shining") from the root *b-h-q*, and *qarach* ("ice/crystal") from Ezek 1:22 (*ke-ein ha-qarach ha-nora*, "like the appearance of awesome crystal" — describing the expanse above the living creatures). Good Ezekiel theophany vocabulary.

### 21:15 — qeneh zahav
- **Hebrew**: *qeneh zahav* ("a golden reed")
- **Note**: *Qaneh* ("reed") as a measuring instrument echoes Ezek 40:3, 5 (*qaneh ha-middah*, "the measuring reed") — the angel who measures the eschatological temple. The golden version elevates the measurement from Ezekiel's earthly vision to the heavenly reality.

### 21:18 — zahav mufaz
- **Hebrew**: *zahav mufaz* ("refined gold")
- **Note**: *Zahav mufaz* appears in 1 Kgs 10:18 and is associated with Solomon's throne and temple furnishings. Also *paz* appears in Ps 19:11, 119:127, Prov 8:19. Good royal/temple vocabulary.

### 21:21 — margaliyyot
- **Hebrew**: *margaliyyot* ("pearls")
- **Note**: *Margalit* is a late Hebrew / Aramaic loanword (from Greek *margarites*), not biblical Hebrew. No biblical Hebrew word for "pearl" exists, so this is unavoidable.

## Other Notes

### Red-Text Candidates
- **21:3**: The voice from the throne: *hinneh mishkan Elohim im benei ha-adam...* This is spoken "from the throne" but may be an angelic herald rather than God directly. In Catholic interpretation, the voice from the throne announces God's covenant presence. Possible red-text candidate.
- **21:5-8**: *Va-yomer ha-yoshev al ha-kisse: hinneni oseh ha-kol chadash...* ("And He who sat on the throne said: Behold, I am making all things new..."). This is God the Father speaking directly from the throne. Strong red-text candidate through v. 8 (*ha-mavet ha-sheni*). This includes:
  - v. 5: "Behold, I make all things new" / "Write, for these words are true and faithful"
  - v. 6: "It is done. I am the Alpha and the Omega... I will give to the thirsty..."
  - v. 7: "He who overcomes shall inherit... I will be his God and he will be my son"
  - v. 8: "But the cowardly... their part shall be in the lake of fire..."

### Theological Note — 21:22 No Temple
The absence of a *heikhal* ("temple") in the New Jerusalem is one of the most theologically significant statements in Revelation. The entire OT trajectory — Tabernacle, Solomon's Temple, Second Temple, Ezekiel's eschatological Temple — is fulfilled not in a building but in the unmediated presence of God and the Lamb. Catholic theology sees this as the beatific vision: the direct encounter with God that the temple liturgy anticipated but could not fully achieve.

### Theological Note — 21:19-20 Breastplate Stones
The mapping of the twelve foundation stones to the priestly breastplate (Ex 28:17-20) suggests that the New Jerusalem is itself a priestly structure. The high priest bore the names of the twelve tribes on his chest when entering God's presence; now the city itself bears those names on its foundations (v. 14 has the apostles' names, while v. 12 has the tribes' names on the gates). The combination of tribal gates and apostolic foundations unites the Old and New Covenants in the structure of the city.

### OT Allusion Density
Chapter 21 has the highest concentration of Isaiah 60 and 65 allusions in Revelation:
- **Isa 65:17, 66:22**: New heavens and new earth — v. 1
- **Isa 61:10, 62:5**: Bride adorned — v. 2
- **Ex 25:8, Ezek 37:27**: Tabernacle/dwelling — v. 3
- **Isa 25:8**: Wiping tears, abolishing death — v. 4
- **Isa 43:19**: "I am doing a new thing" — v. 5
- **2 Sam 7:14**: Adoption formula — v. 7
- **Ezek 48:30-34**: Twelve gates — vv. 12-13
- **Ezek 40:3, 5**: Measuring reed — v. 15
- **Ex 28:17-20**: Breastplate gemstones — vv. 19-20
- **Isa 60:1-3, 19-20**: Glory of God as light, no need for sun — vv. 23-24
- **Isa 60:11**: Gates never shut — v. 25
- **Isa 52:1**: No unclean thing shall enter — v. 27
