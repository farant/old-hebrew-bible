# Revelation Chapter 16 — Translation Notes

## Versification

All 21 verses align across all three sources (Vulgate, SBLGNT, Delitzsch). No versification discrepancies.

## Content Alignment

### 16:1 — "Ite, et effundite" / "Hypagete kai ekcheete" / "lekhu hariqu"
- **Vulgate**: *Ite, et effundite septem phialas irae Dei in terram* ("Go and pour out the seven bowls of the wrath of God on the earth")
- **Greek**: *Hypagete kai ekcheete tas hepta phialas tou thymou tou theou eis ten gen*
- **Hebrew**: *lekhu hariqu et sheva qa'arot chamat ha-Elohim artzah* ("go, empty the seven bowls of the wrath of God earthward")
- **Note**: The Hebrew uses *hariqu* (hiphil of *r-y-q*, "to empty/pour out"), which is an excellent choice. *Hareq* appears in Gen 42:35 (emptying sacks) and Jer 48:12 (*ve-heriqu khelav*, "and they shall empty his vessels"). The imagery of emptying vessels of wrath echoes Jer 48:12 precisely — vessels being tipped over and emptied. The command *lekhu* ("go!") is the standard imperative plural.

### 16:2 — First bowl: "vulnus saevum et pessimum" / "helkos kakon kai poneron" / "shechin ra u-makh'iv"
- **Hebrew**: *va-yehi shechin ra u-makh'iv* ("and there was a sore, evil and painful")
- **Note**: *Shechin* ("boil/sore") is the term for the sixth Egyptian plague (Ex 9:9-11, *shechin avo'a'ot pore'ach ba-adam u-va-behemah*, "boils breaking out in sores on man and beast"). The parallel is exact — the first bowl plague directly recapitulates the Egyptian boils plague. *Shechin* also appears in Deut 28:27, 35 as a covenant curse (*yakkekha YHVH bi-shechin Mitzrayim*, "the LORD will strike you with the boils of Egypt"). All three sources align.

### 16:3 — "sanguis tamquam mortui" / "haima hos nekrou" / "dam ke-dam chalal"
- **Hebrew**: *va-yehi le-dam ke-dam chalal* ("and it became blood, like the blood of a slain one")
- **Note**: The Vulgate *tamquam mortui* ("as of a dead man") and Greek *hos nekrou* ("as of a dead person") are rendered *ke-dam chalal* ("like the blood of a slain one") in Hebrew. *Chalal* ("pierced one/slain") is the standard OT term for those killed in battle (Num 23:24, Deut 21:1, Isa 22:2, 34:3, Jer 51:4, Ezek 6:7, 30:11). It is more vivid than the Greek/Latin — not merely "dead" but "slain/pierced." This intensifies the image. The first Egyptian plague (Ex 7:17-21, water to blood) is the OT background.

### 16:4-7 — Third bowl and the angel of waters
- **Note**: All three sources align closely throughout this section. The dialogue between the angel of the waters and the altar is preserved.

### 16:5 — "qui es, et qui eras sanctus" / "ho on kai ho en, ho hosios" / "ha-hoveh ve-hayah ve-ha-qadosh"
- **Vulgate**: *Justus es, Domine, qui es, et qui eras sanctus, qui haec judicasti* ("You are just, Lord, who are and who were, the holy one, who have judged these things")
- **Greek**: *Dikaios ei, ho on kai ho en, ho hosios, hoti tauta ekrinas* ("Righteous are you, the one who is and who was, the holy one, because you judged these things")
- **Hebrew**: *tzaddiq attah ha-hoveh ve-hayah ve-ha-qadosh ki khen shafatta* ("righteous are you, the one who is and was, and the holy one, for thus you have judged")
- **Note**: The divine title *ha-hoveh ve-hayah* ("the one who is and was") notably omits the third member *ve-yavo* ("and will come") that appeared in earlier occurrences (1:4, 1:8, 4:8). Both the Greek and the Hebrew omit it here, following the SBLGNT. The Vulgate also omits the future element. This may be theologically significant: at this point in the judgment, the future coming is being realized — He no longer "is to come" because He is actively coming in judgment. All three agree on this omission.

### 16:6 — "quia sanguinem... dedisti bibere" — lex talionis
- **Hebrew**: *ki dam qedoshim u-nevi'im shafakhu ve-dam hishqitam ki gemul yadam hu* ("for the blood of saints and prophets they poured out, and blood you gave them to drink, for it is the recompense of their hands")
- **Note**: The phrase *gemul yadam* ("recompense of their hands") is not in the Vulgate (*digni enim sunt*, "for they are worthy") or the Greek (*axioi eisin*, "they are worthy"). The Hebrew replaces "they are worthy" with "it is the recompense of their hands," which echoes Isa 3:11 (*ki gemul yadav ye'aseh lo*, "for the recompense of his hands shall be done to him") and Joel 4:4 [3:4] (*gemulkhem ashiv be-roshkhem*, "I will return your recompense on your own head"). This is a substantive interpretive change.
- **Flag**: The Hebrew renders *digni enim sunt* / *axioi eisin* ("for they are worthy/deserving") as *ki gemul yadam hu* ("for it is the recompense of their hands"), departing from both Vulgate and Greek in wording though capturing a similar retributive meaning.

### 16:7 — "Etiam Domine Deus omnipotens" / "Nai, kyrie ho theos ho pantokrator"
- **Vulgate**: *Etiam Domine Deus omnipotens, vera et justa judicia tua*
- **Hebrew**: *omnam ken YHVH Elohim Tzeva'ot emet va-tzedeq mishpatekha*
- **Note**: The altar speaking (*va-eshma et ha-mizbe'ach omer*) personifies the altar, which represents the prayers and blood of the martyrs (6:9-10). *YHVH Elohim Tzeva'ot* renders *Domine Deus omnipotens*. *Emet va-tzedeq mishpatekha* ("true and righteous are your judgments") echoes Ps 19:10 (*mishpetei YHVH emet tzadequ yachdav*, "the judgments of the LORD are true, righteous altogether") and Ps 119:137 (*tzaddiq attah YHVH ve-yashar mishpatekha*, "righteous are you, O LORD, and upright are your judgments").

### 16:9 — "neque egerunt poenitentiam" / "ou metenoisan" / "ve-lo shavu"
- **Hebrew**: *ve-lo shavu latet lo ha-kavod* ("and they did not turn to give him glory")
- **Note**: The Hebrew uses *shavu* ("they returned/repented") from the root *sh-u-v*, the classic OT term for repentance/return (Jer 3:12, 14, 22, 4:1, 8:4-6, Hos 6:1, 14:2-3, Joel 2:12-13, Amos 4:6-11, Mal 3:7). This is more theologically resonant than the Greek *metanoeo* ("change of mind"), as *shuv* in the prophetic tradition connotes a full covenantal return to God, not merely cognitive change. Delitzsch renders *metanoeo* with *shuv* throughout Revelation, which is an excellent choice for biblical register.

### 16:10-11 — Fifth bowl: darkness
- **Hebrew**: *va-techshakh malkhutah* ("and her kingdom was darkened")
- **Note**: The darkness over the beast's kingdom echoes the ninth Egyptian plague (Ex 10:21-23, *choshekh afelah*, "thick darkness"). *Va-techshakh* (from *ch-sh-kh*, "to grow dark") appears in Isa 13:10, Jer 13:16, Ezek 30:18, Joel 2:10, 4:15 [3:15] for eschatological darkening. The tongue-biting (*va-yenashkhu et leshonam*, "they gnawed their tongues") is unique vivid imagery.

### 16:12 — "Euphraten" / "Euphrates" / "nehar Perat"
- **Hebrew**: *ha-nahar ha-gadol nehar Perat* ("the great river, the river Euphrates")
- **Note**: *Nehar Perat* is the standard OT name for the Euphrates (Gen 2:14, 15:18, Deut 1:7, 11:24, Josh 1:4, 2 Kgs 23:29, Jer 13:4-7, 46:2, 10, 51:63). The phrase *ha-nahar ha-gadol* ("the great river") is directly from Gen 15:18 (*mi-nehar Mitzrayim ad ha-nahar ha-gadol nehar Perat*, "from the river of Egypt to the great river, the river Euphrates") — God's covenantal land promise to Abraham. The drying of the Euphrates echoes Isa 11:15 (drying of the tongue of the Sea of Egypt) and Jer 50:38, 51:36 (drying up Babylon's waters).

### 16:12 — "regibus ab ortu solis" / "ton basileon ton apo anatoles heliou" / "melakhim ha-ba'im mi-mizrach shamesh"
- **Hebrew**: *la-melakhim ha-ba'im mi-mizrach shamesh* ("for the kings coming from the rising of the sun")
- **Note**: *Mi-mizrach shamesh* ("from the rising of the sun") echoes Isa 41:25 (*ha'iroti mi-tzafon va-yat mi-mizrach shemesh yiqra vi-shmi*, "I stirred up one from the north, and he has come, from the rising of the sun he shall call upon my name") — a reference to Cyrus as God's instrument. The kings from the east in Rev 16 may carry ironic Cyrus typology — Cyrus conquered Babylon by diverting the Euphrates (Herodotus 1.191), and here the Euphrates is dried to prepare the way for eastern kings.

### 16:13 — "pseudoprophetae" / "pseudoprophetou" / "nevi ha-sheqer"
- **Hebrew**: *nevi ha-sheqer* ("the prophet of falsehood")
- **Note**: This is the first appearance of the false prophet in Revelation. *Nevi ha-sheqer* echoes Jer 14:14 (*sheqer ha-nevi'im nibbe'im bi-shmi*, "falsehood the prophets prophesy in my name"), 23:25-26, 27:9, 29:8-9, Ezek 13:2-9. The false prophet forms the third member of the unholy trinity (dragon/beast/false prophet as parody of Father/Son/Spirit).

### 16:13 — "in modum ranarum" / "hos batrachoi" / "domot li-tzfarde'im"
- **Hebrew**: *domot li-tzfarde'im* ("resembling frogs")
- **Note**: *Tzfarde'im* ("frogs") directly evokes the second Egyptian plague (Ex 7:26-8:11 [8:1-15]). The unclean spirits as frogs reinforce the Exodus plague typology that runs throughout chapters 15-16. *Domot* ("resembling/similar to") is from *d-m-h*, used for visionary comparisons (Ezek 1:5, 10, 13, 22, 26, Dan 10:16).

### 16:14 — "ad diem magnum omnipotentis Dei" / "yom Elohei ha-Tzeva'ot"
- **Hebrew**: *le-milchemet ha-yom ha-hu ha-gadol yom Elohei ha-Tzeva'ot* ("for the battle of that great day, the day of the God of hosts")
- **Note**: *Yom Elohei ha-Tzeva'ot* echoes the Day of the LORD tradition: Joel 1:15, 2:1, 11, 31, Amos 5:18-20, Zeph 1:7, 14-16, Isa 2:12, 13:6, 9, Jer 46:10, Ezek 30:3, Mal 3:23 [4:5]. The phrase *ha-yom ha-hu ha-gadol* ("that great day") echoes Joel 3:4 [2:31] (*lifnei bo yom YHVH ha-gadol ve-ha-nora*, "before the great and awesome day of the LORD comes") and Mal 3:23 [4:5]. The battle assembled is the eschatological battle of the Day of the LORD.

### 16:15 — "Ecce venio sicut fur" — Red-text candidate
- **Vulgate**: *Ecce venio sicut fur. Beatus qui vigilat, et custodit vestimenta sua*
- **Greek**: *Idou erchomai hos kleptes. makarios ho gregoron kai teron ta himatia autou*
- **Hebrew**: *hineni va ke-gannav ashrei ha-shoqed ve-shomer et begadav pen yelekh arom ve-ra'u et ervatav*
- **Note**: This is an interjection by Christ himself, breaking into the narrative. The first person *hineni va* ("behold, I am coming") identifies the speaker as Christ (cf. 3:3, 1 Thess 5:2, 2 Pet 3:10). The thief metaphor echoes Mt 24:43-44, Lk 12:39-40, 1 Thess 5:2, 4. The beatitude about keeping garments echoes Rev 3:4, 18.
- The Hebrew *ervatav* ("his nakedness/shame") uses the standard OT term for sexual/shameful exposure (Gen 9:22-23, Lev 18:6-19, 20:17-21, Ezek 16:36-37). *Shoqed* ("one who watches/is alert") echoes Jer 1:12 (*ki shoqed ani al devari la'asoto*, "I am watching over my word to perform it") — a wordplay on the almond tree (*shaqed*).

### 16:16 — "Armagedon" / "Har Megiddon"
- **Vulgate**: *Armagedon*
- **Greek**: *Harmagedon*
- **Hebrew**: *har Megiddon* ("Mount Megiddo")
- **Note**: The Hebrew transparently renders the toponym as *har Megiddon*, revealing its etymology — *har* ("mountain") + *Megiddo* (the plain/city in northern Israel). Megiddo was the site of decisive battles: Judg 5:19 (Deborah and Barak vs. Sisera), 2 Kgs 23:29-30 (Josiah's death), 2 Chr 35:22. The plain of Megiddo is the site of Zech 12:11 (*misped Hadadrimmon be-viq'at Megiddon*, "the mourning of Hadadrimmon in the plain of Megiddo"). The Hebrew is more informative than the Greek/Latin transliterations.

### 16:17 — "Factum est" / "Gegonen" / "hayoh nihyatah"
- **Vulgate**: *Factum est* ("It is done")
- **Greek**: *Gegonen* ("It is done/has happened")
- **Hebrew**: *hayoh nihyatah* ("it has truly been accomplished")
- **Note**: The doubled form *hayoh nihyatah* (infinitive absolute + perfect) is an emphatic biblical Hebrew construction indicating certainty and completion. This grammatical pattern appears throughout the OT (Gen 2:17, *mot tamut*; Gen 18:18, *hayo yihyeh*; Num 15:35, *mot yumat*). The voice from the temple and the throne is a divine voice — either God the Father or Christ.

### 16:18-21 — Theophanic accompaniment and the great earthquake
- **Note**: The sequence *qolot u-re'amim u-veraqim* ("voices and thunders and lightnings") echoes Ex 19:16 (*qolot u-veraqim*, "thunders and lightnings") and Rev 4:5, 8:5, 11:19. This theophanic formula marks climactic divine action throughout Revelation. The earthquake that has no parallel since humanity began echoes Dan 12:1 (*ve-hayetah et tzarah asher lo nihyetah mi-heyot goy*, "there shall be a time of trouble such as never was since there was a nation").

### 16:19 — "Babylon magna venit in memoriam ante Deum"
- **Hebrew**: *va-tizzakher Bavel ha-gedolah lifnei Elohim latet lah kos yein chamat appo* ("and Babylon the great was remembered before God, to give her the cup of the wine of his fierce wrath")
- **Note**: *Va-tizzakher* ("was remembered") uses the niphal of *z-k-r*, which in the OT carries the sense of God remembering for the purpose of action — either blessing (Gen 8:1, *va-yizkor Elohim et Noach*; Gen 19:29, Ex 2:24) or judgment (Ps 137:7, Jer 14:10, Hos 8:13, 9:9). Here God "remembers" Babylon for judgment. *Kos yein chamat appo* ("cup of the wine of his fierce wrath") combines Jer 25:15 and Isa 51:17, 22. The construct *chamat appo* ("the fury of his anger/nostrils") is an OT idiom (Deut 29:23 [24], Jer 30:24, 32:37, Lam 4:11).

### 16:21 — "talentum" / "talantaia" / "ka-kikkar"
- **Hebrew**: *avnei varad ka-kikkar mishqalam* ("hailstones, about a talent in weight")
- **Note**: *Kikkar* ("talent") is the standard OT weight measure (Ex 25:39, 37:24, 38:24-29, 1 Kgs 9:14, 10:10). One talent is approximately 75 pounds / 34 kg. The hailstones of Rev 16:21 surpass the seventh Egyptian plague of hail (Ex 9:23-25, *va-yehi varad*, "and there was hail") and echo Josh 10:11 (*va-YHVH hishlikh aleihem avanim gedolot min ha-shamayim*, "and the LORD threw down great stones from heaven upon them"). The description *ki khavdah makkato me'od* ("for his plague was very heavy") uses *kavdah* ("heavy"), echoing Ex 9:3, 18, 24 (*kaved me'od*, "very heavy/severe").

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 16:1 — hariqu
- **Note**: *Hariqu* (hiphil imperative of *r-y-q*) for "pour out" is vivid — it connotes the complete emptying of a container, not a controlled pouring. This matches the totality of divine judgment. Cf. Jer 48:12 (Moab's vessels will be emptied).

### 16:5 — tzaddiq attah... ki khen shafatta
- **Note**: *Tzaddiq attah* ("righteous are you") echoes Ps 119:137. The divine attribute of justice affirmed even in the act of judgment is a prophetic theme (Jer 12:1, *tzaddiq attah YHVH ki ariv elekha*, "righteous are you, O LORD, when I plead with you").

### 16:9 — va-yelohatu
- **Hebrew**: *va-yelohatu venei adam be-chom gadol* ("and people were scorched with great heat")
- **Note**: *Va-yelohatu* (from *l-h-t*, "to burn/flame") is rare and vivid. The root appears in Deut 32:22 (*ki esh qadechah be-appi va-tiqad ad she'ol tachtit*, "for a fire is kindled in my anger and it burns to the depths of Sheol"), where it describes divine fire burning downward. Also related to *lahat* in Gen 3:24 (*lahat ha-cherev ha-mithappekhet*, "the flaming sword that turned every way"). Strong biblical register.

### 16:12 — va-yechrevu meimav
- **Hebrew**: *va-yechrevu meimav* ("and its waters dried up")
- **Note**: *Charav* ("to be dry/desolate") is the Exodus vocabulary for the drying of waters: Ex 14:21 (*va-yasem et ha-yam le-charavah*, "and made the sea into dry ground"), Josh 2:10, 4:23, 2 Kgs 19:24, Isa 44:27 (*ha-omer la-tzulah choravi ve-naharotayikh ovish*, "who says to the deep, 'Be dry,' and I will dry up your rivers"). Using *charav* for the Euphrates drying explicitly frames it as a new Exodus/conquest event.

### 16:15 — ashrei ha-shoqed
- **Note**: The beatitude *ashrei ha-shoqed* ("blessed is the one who watches") follows the OT *ashrei* formula. *Shoqed* is used for vigilant watchfulness in Jer 1:12 and Prov 8:34 (*ashrei adam shome'a li lishqod al daltotai yom yom*, "blessed is the one who listens to me, watching daily at my gates"). The Proverbs passage is particularly apt — Wisdom's call to watchfulness parallels Christ's call here.

## Other Notes

### Red-Text Candidates
- **16:1**: The voice from the temple commanding the seven angels (*lekhu hariqu et sheva qa'arot chamat ha-Elohim artzah*) — this is a divine voice from the temple/throne. Since no one can enter the temple (15:8), the speaker must be God himself. This is a red-text candidate for divine speech.
- **16:15**: *Hineni va ke-gannav ashrei ha-shoqed ve-shomer et begadav pen yelekh arom ve-ra'u et ervatav* — Christ speaks in the first person. Red-text candidate.
- **16:17**: *Hayoh nihyatah* ("It is done") — the voice from the temple and the throne. Divine speech. Red-text candidate.

### Exodus Plague Typology
Chapter 16 systematically recapitulates the Egyptian plagues in escalated, universal form:
| Bowl | Plague | Egyptian Parallel |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| 1st | Sores (*shechin*) | 6th plague: boils (Ex 9:8-12) |
| 2nd | Sea to blood | 1st plague: Nile to blood (Ex 7:17-21) |
| 3rd | Rivers to blood | 1st plague continued |
| 4th | Scorching sun | No direct parallel (cf. Deut 32:22) |
| 5th | Darkness | 9th plague: darkness (Ex 10:21-23) |
| 6th | Euphrates dried | Red Sea drying (Ex 14:21) |
| 7th | Hail/earthquake | 7th plague: hail (Ex 9:22-26) |

### OT Allusion Density
- **Ex 7-12**: Egyptian plagues throughout
- **Ex 14:21**: Drying of waters — v. 12
- **Ex 19:16**: Theophanic thunder and lightning — v. 18
- **Gen 15:18**: The great river Euphrates — v. 12
- **Josh 10:11**: Great stones from heaven — v. 21
- **Isa 51:17, 22**: Cup of wrath — v. 19
- **Jer 10:7, 25:15**: Fear of God, cup of wrath — vv. 7, 19
- **Joel 2:31, 4:14 [3:14]**: Day of the LORD — v. 14
- **Zech 12:11**: Mourning of Megiddo — v. 16
- **Dan 12:1**: Unprecedented tribulation — v. 18
