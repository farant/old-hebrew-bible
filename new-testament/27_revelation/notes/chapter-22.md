# Revelation Chapter 22 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 21 verses align across all three sources (Vulgate, SBLGNT, Delitzsch). No versification discrepancies.

## Content Alignment

### 22:1 — "nachal shel mayim chayyim" for "fluvium aquae vitae"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *fluvium aquae vitae* ("a river of the water of life"), Greek *potamon hydatos zoes*. The Hebrew *nachal shel mayim chayyim* ("a stream/river of living waters") uses *nachal* ("stream/wadi/river"), which in the OT typically refers to seasonal watercourses.
- **Note**: A stronger OT term might be *nahar* ("river," as in Gen 2:10: *ve-nahar yotze me-Eden*, "a river went out from Eden") or the *nachal* of Ezek 47:5-12 (the eschatological temple river). In fact, *nachal* does appear in Ezek 47:5 (*nachal asher lo ukhal la'avor*, "a river I could not cross"), so the choice is defensible. The *shel* construction (see 21:27 note) is again Mishnaic rather than classical. The phrase *mayim chayyim* echoes Zech 14:8 (*yetze'u mayim chayyim mi-Yerushalayim*, "living waters shall go out from Jerusalem") and Jer 2:13.

### 22:1 — "yotze mi-kisse ha-Elohim ve-ha-seh" — Source of the river
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree: the river proceeds from the throne of God and of the Lamb. The Hebrew *yotze mi-kisse ha-Elohim ve-ha-seh* ("going out from the throne of God and the Lamb") echoes Ezek 47:1 (*ve-hinneh mayim yotzim mittachtat miftan ha-bayit qadimah*, "and behold, water was issuing from below the threshold of the temple toward the east"). In Ezekiel, the water comes from the temple; here it comes from the throne, since God and the Lamb are the temple (21:22).
- **Note**: The transition from temple-source (Ezekiel) to throne-source (Revelation) is theologically coherent: what the temple mediated is now directly present.

### 22:2 — "etz chayyim" for "lignum vitae"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree. The Hebrew *etz chayyim* ("tree of life") is from Gen 2:9, 3:22, 24 (*ve-etz ha-chayyim betokh ha-gan*, "and the tree of life in the midst of the garden") and Prov 3:18, 11:30, 13:12, 15:4.
- **Note**: Outstanding. The tree of life, barred since Gen 3:24 (*et ha-keruvim ve-et lahat ha-cherev ha-mithappekhet lishmor et derekh etz ha-chayyim*, "the cherubim and the flaming sword to guard the way to the tree of life"), is now accessible again. The Hebrew *etz chayyim* creates a direct inclusio between Genesis and Revelation.

### 22:2 — "li-trufat ha-goyim" for "ad sanitatem gentium"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *ad sanitatem gentium* ("for the healing of the nations"), Greek *eis therapeian ton ethnon*. The Hebrew *li-trufat ha-goyim* ("for the healing of the nations") uses *terufah* ("healing/medicine"), found in Jer 30:13, 33:6, 46:11, 2 Chr 21:18, Prov 3:8.
- **Note**: *Terufah* is good biblical Hebrew. The healing of the nations by the leaves of the tree connects to Ezek 47:12 (*ve-aleihu li-terufah*, "and its leaves for healing"). The Hebrew of Rev 22:2 and Ezek 47:12 share the same key phrase: *aleh... li-terufah*. Excellent.

### 22:3 — "kol cherem lo yihyeh od" for "omne maledictum non erit amplius"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *omne maledictum non erit amplius* ("every curse shall be no more"), Greek *pan katathema ouk estai eti*. The Hebrew *kol cherem lo yihyeh od* ("every ban/curse will be no more") uses *cherem* ("the devoted thing/ban," from the root *ch-r-m*).
- **Note**: *Cherem* is a powerful OT term for the sacred ban — total destruction or devotion to God (Josh 6:17-18, 7:1, 11-12, Lev 27:28-29, Deut 7:26, Zech 14:11). The last OT use of this word is Zech 14:11 (*ve-cherem lo yihyeh od*, "and there shall be no more ban/curse"), which is virtually verbatim with this verse. The Hebrew of Rev 22:3 is a direct quotation of Zech 14:11. Outstanding.

### 22:3 — "yeshartuhu" for "servient illi"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *servi ejus servient illi* ("his servants shall serve him"), Greek *hoi douloi autou latreusousin auto*. The Hebrew *avadav yeshartuhu* ("his servants shall minister to him") uses *sheret* ("minister/serve in a priestly capacity") rather than *avad* ("serve/work").
- **Note**: *Sheret* is the specific term for Levitical/priestly service (Ex 28:35, 43, 29:30, Num 1:50, 3:6, 8:26, Deut 10:8, 1 Sam 2:11, 3:1, Ezek 44:11-12). By using *yeshartuhu*, Delitzsch indicates that the saints serve God with priestly ministry, not mere labor. This connects to the earlier declaration that they are *kohanim* ("priests," 20:6). Excellent theological precision.

### 22:4 — "ve-hemmah yir'u et panav"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree: they shall see His face. The Hebrew *yir'u et panav* ("they shall see His face") is the consummation of the OT longing: Ps 17:15 (*ani be-tzedeq echezeh fanekha*, "in righteousness I shall behold your face"), Ps 42:3 (*matai avo ve-era'eh penei Elohim*, "when shall I come and appear before God"), Ex 33:20 (*lo yukhal ha-adam lir'oti va-chai*, "no man shall see me and live"). What was impossible under the old dispensation (Ex 33:20) is now granted.
- **Note**: This is the beatific vision — the direct sight of God's face, which Catholic theology identifies as the essence of heavenly blessedness. The Hebrew makes the Exodus contrast explicit.

### 22:5 — "YHVH Elohim hu ya'ir lahem"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Dominus Deus illuminabit illos* ("the Lord God will illuminate them"), Greek *kyrios ho theos photisei ep' autous*. The Hebrew *YHVH Elohim hu ya'ir lahem* ("the LORD God — He will give them light").
- **Note**: The emphatic pronoun *hu* ("He") stresses that God Himself, not a created light, is the source. Echoes Isa 60:19 and Ps 36:10 (*ki immekha meqor chayyim be-orkha nir'eh or*, "for with you is the fountain of life; in your light we see light").

### 22:6 — "YHVH Elohei ruchot ha-nevi'im" for "Dominus Deus spirituum prophetarum"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Dominus Deus spirituum prophetarum* ("the Lord God of the spirits of the prophets"), Greek *ho kyrios ho theos ton pneumaton ton propheton*. The Hebrew *YHVH Elohei ruchot ha-nevi'im* ("the LORD God of the spirits of the prophets").
- **Note**: The phrase *Elohei ha-ruchot* echoes Num 16:22, 27:16 (*Elohei ha-ruchot le-khol basar*, "the God of the spirits of all flesh"). Here it is narrowed to the prophets. The Hebrew Tetragrammaton follows the Vulgate *Dominus*.

### 22:7 — "hinneni va maher" — Christ's self-announcement
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *ecce venio velociter* ("behold, I come quickly"), Greek *idou erchomai tachy*. The Hebrew *hinneni va maher* ("behold, I am coming quickly").
- **Note**: *Hinneni* is the prophetic/divine self-announcement formula (Gen 22:1, Ex 3:4, 1 Sam 3:4, Isa 6:8, 58:9, 65:1). The speaker is Christ. *Maher* ("quickly") echoes Isa 5:26 (*ve-hinneh meherah qal yavo*, "and behold, swiftly, speedily it comes") and Mal 3:1 (*yavo el heikhalo*, "he will come to his temple").

### 22:10 — "al tachtom" for "Ne signaveris"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Ne signaveris verba prophetiae* ("Do not seal the words of the prophecy"), Greek *me sphragises*. The Hebrew *al tachtom et divrei nevu'at ha-sefer ha-zeh* ("do not seal the words of the prophecy of this book") uses *chatam* ("seal"), the same root as Dan 12:4 (*chatom ha-sefer ad et qetz*, "seal the book until the time of the end").
- **Note**: The deliberate contrast with Daniel is crucial. Daniel was told to seal; John is told not to seal. The Hebrew uses the identical verb *chatam* from Daniel, making the contrast with Dan 12:4 unmistakable. Excellent.

### 22:11 — Fourfold "yosif" construction
- **Issue**: The Vulgate uses *noceat adhuc... sordescat adhuc... justificetur adhuc... sanctificetur adhuc* (four imperative-like constructions with *adhuc*, "still/yet"). The Hebrew uses *yosif* ("let him continue/add") four times: *ha-chomes yosif lachmos... ve-ha-tamei yosif lehittamei... ve-ha-tzaddiq yosif lehitztaddeq... ve-ha-qadosh yosif lehitqaddesh*.
- **Note**: The *yosif* + infinitive construction is good biblical Hebrew for continued action (Gen 4:12, 8:21, 38:26, Judg 13:21). The four categories form a chiasm: wicked/impure // righteous/holy. The Hebrew verbal forms are well-crafted.

### 22:12 — "sekhari itti" for "merces mea mecum est"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *merces mea mecum est* ("my reward is with me"), Greek *ho misthos mou met emou*. The Hebrew *u-sekhari itti* ("and my reward is with me") echoes Isa 40:10 (*hinneh sekharo itto u-fe'ullato lefanav*, "behold, his reward is with him and his recompense before him") and Isa 62:11 (*hinneh sekharo itto*, "behold, his reward is with him").
- **Note**: Near-verbatim from Isa 40:10 and 62:11. The speaker is Christ, and the Isaiah passages describe the coming of YHVH/the LORD. The Hebrew makes the identification of Christ with YHVH's coming explicit.

### 22:13 — Triple self-identification
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *ego sum alpha et omega, primus et novissimus, principium et finis* ("I am the Alpha and Omega, the first and the last, the beginning and the end"). The Hebrew *ani ha-Alef ve-ha-Tav ha-rosh ve-ha-sof ha-rishon ve-ha-acharon* ("I am the Aleph and the Tav, the beginning and the end, the first and the last").
- **Note**: The triple self-identification combines Isa 44:6 (*ani rishon va-ani acharon*, "I am the first and I am the last") with Isa 41:4 (*ani YHVH rishon ve-et acharonim ani hu*, "I the LORD, the first, and with the last, I am He"). These are YHVH's exclusive self-identifications in Isaiah. Christ claims them for Himself. The order differs slightly from the Vulgate (Hebrew: Alpha-Omega, beginning-end, first-last; Vulgate: Alpha-Omega, first-last, beginning-end), but all three pairs are present.

### 22:14 — "ha-mekhabsim et salmoteihem" for "qui lavant stolas suas"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads *qui lavant stolas suas in sanguine Agni* ("who wash their robes in the blood of the Lamb"), following the TR. The SBLGNT reads *hoi plynontes tas stolas auton* ("those who wash their robes") without "in the blood of the Lamb." The Hebrew *ha-mekhabsim et salmoteihem* ("those who wash their garments") follows the SBLGNT, omitting "in the blood of the Lamb."
- **Vulgate**: *qui lavant stolas suas in sanguine Agni*
- **Hebrew**: *ha-mekhabsim et salmoteihem*
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the Greek critical text against the Vulgate here. The Vulgate's *in sanguine Agni* ("in the blood of the Lamb") is a significant addition from the TR tradition (cf. Rev 7:14). For strict Vulgate conformity, the Hebrew should include *be-dam ha-seh*. This is a notable divergence.
- **Note on vocabulary**: *Mekhabsim* ("washing/laundering") from *k-b-s* is the standard OT term for washing garments (Ex 19:10, 14, Lev 11:25, 40, 13:6, 34, Num 19:7, 8, 10, 19, 2 Sam 19:25, Mal 3:2). *Salmot* ("garments") appears in Ex 22:8, Ruth 3:3, Mic 2:8. Good biblical vocabulary.

### 22:14 — "rishyon be-etz ha-chayyim" for "potestas eorum in ligno vitae"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *ut sit potestas eorum in ligno vitae* ("that they may have the right to the tree of life"), Greek *hina estai he exousia auton epi to xylon tes zoes*. The Hebrew *lema'an yihyeh lahem rishyon be-etz ha-chayyim* ("so that they may have permission/right to the tree of life") uses *rishyon* ("license/permission"), found in Ezra 3:7.
- **Note**: *Rishyon* is rare, appearing only in Ezra 3:7 in the OT (*rishyon... mi-Koresh melekh Paras*, "authorization from Cyrus king of Persia"). A more common biblical expression would be *reshut* or *zekhut*. The tree of life connection to Genesis is maintained.

### 22:15 — "ha-kelavim" for "canes"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Foris canes* ("Outside are dogs"), Greek *exo hoi kynes* ("outside are the dogs"). The Hebrew *u-mi-chutz la-ir ha-kelavim* ("and outside the city are the dogs").
- **Note**: *Kelavim* ("dogs") in OT usage denotes the morally impure and idolaters (Deut 23:19 where *kelev* is a cultic prostitute; 1 Sam 17:43, 24:15, 2 Sam 3:8, 9:8, 16:9, 2 Kgs 8:13, Ps 22:17, 21). The exclusion of "dogs" from the holy city echoes Deut 23:18-19.

### 22:16 — "shoresh David ve-toladto" for "radix et genus David"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *radix et genus David* ("the root and offspring of David"), Greek *he rhiza kai to genos Dauid*. The Hebrew *shoresh David ve-toladto* ("the root of David and his offspring") uses *shoresh* ("root," Isa 11:10: *shoresh Yishai*, "the root of Jesse") and *toladto* ("his offspring/generation").
- **Note**: *Shoresh* echoes Isa 11:1, 10 (*ve-yatza choter mi-geza Yishai ve-netzer mi-shorashav yifreh*, "a shoot from the stump of Jesse, a branch from his roots shall bear fruit"). *Toladto* from the root *y-l-d* ("beget") connects to the genealogical tradition. The paradox is central: Christ is both David's root (preexistent source) and David's offspring (incarnate descendant).

### 22:16 — "kokhav nogah ha-shachar" for "stella splendida et matutina"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *stella splendida et matutina* ("the bright morning star"), Greek *ho aster ho lampros ho proinos*. The Hebrew *kokhav nogah ha-shachar* ("the bright star of the dawn").
- **Note**: *Kokhav* is the standard OT star (Gen 1:16, Num 24:17). The Num 24:17 Balaam oracle is the primary background: *darakh kokhav mi-Ya'aqov* ("a star shall come out of Jacob"). *Nogah* ("brightness/splendor") appears in 2 Sam 22:13, Isa 4:5, 60:3, Ezek 1:4, 27, 28, 10:4, Joel 2:10, 4:15, Hab 3:4. *Ha-shachar* ("the dawn") echoes Isa 14:12 (*helel ben shachar*, "morning star, son of the dawn") — the fallen star of Babylon, now contrasted with Christ as the true morning star.

### 22:17 — "ve-ha-ruach ve-ha-kallah omrim bo"
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree: the Spirit and the Bride say "Come." The Hebrew *ve-ha-ruach ve-ha-kallah omrim bo* ("and the Spirit and the Bride say, 'Come'").
- **Note**: The final liturgical cry of the book. *Bo* ("come") is the simplest and most direct Hebrew imperative. The Spirit (*ha-ruach*) and the Bride (*ha-kallah*) together invite the coming of Christ, echoing the *Maranatha* of 1 Cor 16:22.

### 22:18-19 — "yosif/yigra" — Addition/subtraction warning
- **Issue**: All three traditions agree. The Hebrew warning against adding (*yosif*) or subtracting (*yigra*) from the book echoes Deut 4:2 (*lo tosifu al ha-davar asher anokhi metzavveh etkhem ve-lo tigre'u mimmennu*, "you shall not add to the word that I command you, nor take from it") and Deut 13:1.
- **Note**: The verbal parallels to Deuteronomy are exact: *yosif/tosifu* and *yigra/tigre'u* share the same roots. The canonical warning that closes Revelation mirrors the canonical warning that governs the Torah. This frames Revelation as having the same divine authority as the Mosaic law.

### 22:19 — "etz ha-chayyim" vs. Vulgate "libro vitae"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads *auferet Deus partem ejus de libro vitae* ("God will take away his part from the book of life"), following the TR. The SBLGNT reads *apo tou xylou tes zoes* ("from the tree of life"). The Hebrew *me-etz ha-chayyim* ("from the tree of life") follows the Greek critical text against the Vulgate.
- **Vulgate**: *de libro vitae* ("from the book of life")
- **Hebrew**: *me-etz ha-chayyim* ("from the tree of life")
- **Note**: This is a significant textual variant. The TR/Vulgate has "book of life" (*biblou/libro*), while the critical text has "tree of life" (*xylou/ligno*). The Hebrew follows the critical Greek text against the Vulgate. For Vulgate conformity, the Hebrew should read *mi-sefer ha-chayyim* ("from the book of life"). This should be flagged.

### 22:20 — "bo'ah na ha-Adon Yeshua" — Maranatha
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Veni, Domine Jesu* ("Come, Lord Jesus"), Greek *erchou kyrie Iesou* ("Come, Lord Jesus"). The Hebrew *bo'ah na ha-Adon Yeshua* ("Come, please, Lord Jesus") adds the particle *na* ("please/I pray"), which is not in the Vulgate or Greek.
- **Note**: The addition of *na* is a Delitzsch choice that adds warmth and longing. The *na* particle appears frequently in biblical prayers and supplications (Gen 12:11, 13:8, 18:3, Ps 118:25, 2 Kgs 20:3). The phrase echoes the Aramaic *Maranatha* ("Our Lord, come!" — 1 Cor 16:22), which is the earliest Christian prayer formula. *Bo'ah* (feminine imperative form) is used here, which is grammatically unusual for a masculine addressee — possibly a scribal/printing convention in Delitzsch.

### 22:21 — "chesed Adoneinu Yeshua ha-Mashiach im kol ha-qedoshim"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate *Gratia Domini nostri Jesu Christi cum omnibus vobis* ("The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all"), Greek *He charis tou kyriou Iesou meta panton* ("The grace of the Lord Jesus be with all"). The Hebrew adds *ha-Mashiach* ("the Christ/Messiah") and *ha-qedoshim* ("the saints") where the SBLGNT has only *Iesou* and *panton*.
- **Vulgate**: *Domini nostri Jesu Christi cum omnibus vobis*
- **Hebrew**: *Adoneinu Yeshua ha-Mashiach im kol ha-qedoshim*
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the Vulgate/TR in including "Christ" (*ha-Mashiach*) and expands *vobis* ("you") to *ha-qedoshim* ("the saints"), which follows a TR variant. The closing *Amen* is present in all three traditions.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 22:1 — mav'hiq ke-ein ha-qarach
- **Hebrew**: *mav'hiq ke-ein ha-qarach*
- **Note**: The description of the river as *mav'hiq ke-ein ha-qarach* ("gleaming like crystal") uses the same vocabulary as 21:11. *Qarach* ("ice/crystal") is from Ezek 1:22. Good consistency.

### 22:2 — etz chayyim oseh peri
- **Hebrew**: *etz chayyim oseh peri shtem esreh*
- **Note**: *Etz chayyim* ("tree of life") is the direct Genesis connection (Gen 2:9). The phrase *oseh peri* ("bearing fruit") echoes Gen 1:11-12 (*etz peri oseh peri le-mino*, "fruit trees bearing fruit according to their kinds"). The creation language returns in the new creation.

### 22:2 — midei chodesh be-chodsho
- **Hebrew**: *midei chodesh be-chodsho yitten et piryo*
- **Note**: *Midei chodesh be-chodsho* ("every month in its month") is excellent biblical prose style. Cf. 1 Chr 27:1 (*midei chodesh be-chodesh*, "month by month"), Isa 66:23 (*midei chodesh be-chodsho*, "from new moon to new moon"). The monthly cycle of fruit connects to the liturgical calendar and the new creation's order.

### 22:3 — cherem
- **Hebrew**: *kol cherem lo yihyeh od*
- **Note**: Verbatim from Zech 14:11. See Content Alignment above. This is one of the most precisely targeted OT quotations in the entire translation.

### 22:11 — chomes/tamei/tzaddiq/qadosh
- **Hebrew**: The four categories: *ha-chomes* (the violent/unjust), *ha-tamei* (the impure), *ha-tzaddiq* (the righteous), *ha-qadosh* (the holy).
- **Note**: *Chomes* (from *ch-m-s*, "violence/wrong") is classic prophetic vocabulary (Gen 6:11, Hab 1:2-3, Jer 6:7, Ezek 45:9). *Tamei/tahor* ("impure/pure") is Levitical. *Tzaddiq* is the foundational OT moral category (Gen 6:9, 18:23-26, Ps 1:6, Hab 2:4). *Qadosh* is the priestly/divine category (Lev 11:44-45, 19:2, Isa 6:3). Together the four terms span the OT moral vocabulary: prophetic, priestly, wisdom, and holiness traditions.

### 22:16 — anokhi shoresh David
- **Hebrew**: *anokhi shoresh David ve-toladto*
- **Note**: The use of *anokhi* (the emphatic first-person pronoun) rather than *ani* adds solemnity to Christ's self-identification. *Anokhi* is the form used in the divine self-revelations: Ex 20:2 (*anokhi YHVH Elohekha*, "I am the LORD your God"), Isa 43:11 (*anokhi anokhi YHVH*, "I, I am the LORD"). Its use here by Christ is a subtle but significant claim.

## Other Notes

### Red-Text Candidates
Chapter 22 contains multiple instances of direct divine/Christological speech:
- **22:7**: *Hinneni va maher* ("Behold, I am coming quickly") — Christ speaking. Red text.
- **22:12-13**: *Ve-hinneni va maher u-sekhari itti... ani ha-Alef ve-ha-Tav...* ("Behold, I am coming quickly, and my reward is with me... I am the Alpha and the Omega...") — Christ speaking. Red text.
- **22:16**: *Ani Yeshua shalachti et mal'akhi le-ha'id lakhem et eleh bifnei ha-qehillot anokhi shoresh David ve-toladto kokhav nogah ha-shachar* ("I Jesus have sent my angel to testify to you these things for the churches. I am the root and the offspring of David, the bright morning star.") — Christ speaking. Red text.
- **22:20a**: *Ha-me'id ha-edut ha-zot omer amnam ken ani va maher* ("He who testifies these things says, 'Indeed, yes, I am coming quickly'") — Christ speaking. The inner quotation is red text.

### Theological Note — 22:1-5 The Restored Eden
The closing vision of Revelation (22:1-5) systematically reverses the curses and losses of Genesis 3:
- **Gen 3:24** (barred from the tree of life) is reversed in **22:2** (the tree of life is accessible, bearing fruit perpetually)
- **Gen 3:17-19** (cursed ground, toil) is reversed in **22:3** (*kol cherem lo yihyeh od*, "no more curse")
- **Gen 3:8** (hiding from God's presence) is reversed in **22:4** (*yir'u et panav*, "they shall see His face")
- **Gen 3:23** (exile from Eden) is reversed in **22:5** (reigning forever in God's presence)

The Hebrew translation makes all four reversals explicit through shared vocabulary with Genesis 2-3. The canonical arc from creation to new creation is closed.

### Theological Note — 22:13 Christ and YHVH
The triple self-identification (*ha-Alef ve-ha-Tav, ha-rosh ve-ha-sof, ha-rishon ve-ha-acharon*) is spoken by Christ (v. 16 confirms the speaker as Jesus). Yet *ha-rishon ve-ha-acharon* is YHVH's exclusive title in Isa 44:6 and 48:12. The Hebrew translation preserves this identification with YHVH's own language, making the Christological claim unmistakable: Christ speaks with the voice and titles of YHVH.

### Theological Note — 22:17 The Great Invitation
The final invitation (*ve-ha-ruach ve-ha-kallah omrim bo... ve-ha-tzame yavo ve-he-chafetz yiqqach mayim chayyim chinnam*, "the Spirit and the Bride say Come... and let the thirsty come, and whoever desires, let him take the water of life freely") echoes Isa 55:1 (*hoy kol tzame lekhu la-mayim*, "Ho, everyone who thirsts, come to the waters"). This is the eschatological fulfillment of Isaiah's invitation. The Hebrew *chinnam* ("freely/gratis") matches Isaiah's *be-lo khesef u-velo mechir* ("without money and without price").
