# Luke Chapter 19 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 48 verses align across all three sources. No versification discrepancies.

## Content Alignment
### 19:26 — "et abundabit" in Vulgate
- **Issue**: The Vulgate adds "et abundabit" (and he will abound) which is not in the Greek text. The Greek says simply δοθήσεται (will be given). The Hebrew follows the Greek without the addition.
- **Vulgate**: omni habenti dabitur, et abundabit
- **Greek**: παντὶ τῷ ἔχοντι δοθήσεται
- **Hebrew**: כָּל-אִישׁ שֶׁיֶּשׁ-לוֹ יִנָּתֵן לוֹ
- **Note**: The Vulgate's "et abundabit" is likely a harmonization with Matthew 25:29. A Vulgate-following text should include this addition.

### 19:45 — "vendentes et ementes" vs. τοὺς πωλοῦντας
- **Issue**: The Vulgate mentions both "vendentes" (those selling) and "ementes" (those buying), while the Greek has only τοὺς πωλοῦντας (those selling). The Hebrew includes both: הַמֹּכְרִים וְאֵת הַקּוֹנִים (the sellers and the buyers), matching the Vulgate.
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly follows the Vulgate here.

## Hebrew Translation Quality
### 19:4 — Sycamore tree
- **Hebrew**: שִׁקְמָה (sycamore)
- **Note**: The Greek συκομορέαν and Vulgate "arborem sycomorum" refer to the sycamore-fig tree (Ficus sycomorus), which is precisely the biblical שִׁקְמָה. Unlike the disputed tree in 17:6, this identification is exact (cf. 1 Kings 10:27, 1 Chronicles 27:28, Amos 7:14).

### 19:8 — Fourfold restitution
- **Hebrew**: אַרְבַּעְתָּיִם (fourfold)
- **Note**: This is the exact word used in the law of restitution for stolen sheep in Exodus 21:37 (MT) / 22:1 (English): אַרְבַּע-צֹאן. The form אַרְבַּעְתָּיִם (fourfold) appears in 2 Samuel 12:6. Excellent biblical diction matching the legal context Zacchaeus invokes.

### 19:13 — Mina / mnas
- **Hebrew**: מָנִים (minas)
- **Note**: מָנֶה (singular) / מָנִים (plural) is attested in biblical Hebrew at Ezekiel 45:12, 1 Kings 10:17, Ezra 2:69, and Nehemiah 7:71-72. This is the correct biblical term for the Greek μνᾶ / Latin "mna."

### 19:29 — Place names
- **Hebrew**: בֵּית-פַּגֵּי וּבֵית-הִינִי (Bethphage and Bethany)
- **Note**: The Hebraization of Bethphage as בֵּית-פַּגֵּי (house of unripe figs) reflects the likely Aramaic/Hebrew etymology. בֵּית-הִינִי for Bethany is less standard; the more common etymology connects to בֵּית-עַנְיָה (house of affliction/poverty) or בֵּית-עֲנָנְיָה (house of Ananiah, Nehemiah 11:32).

## Other Notes
### 19:38 — Psalm 118 citation
- The Hebrew בָּרוּך הַמֶּלֶךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוָֹה closely follows Psalm 118:26 with the addition of הַמֶּלֶךְ (the king), matching the Vulgate's "qui venit rex" and Greek ὁ ἐρχόμενος βασιλεύς. The addition of "king" distinguishes Luke's account from the base Psalm text.

### 19:40 — Stones crying out
- The Hebrew הָאֲבָנִים תִּזְעָקְנָה is strong biblical diction. The verb זָעַק (cry out) is used for anguished or urgent cries in the Tanakh (cf. Genesis 4:10 where Abel's blood cries out). The feminine plural form matches the grammatical gender of אֲבָנִים.
