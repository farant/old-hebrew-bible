# Luke Chapter 16 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 31 verses align across all three sources.

## Content Alignment
### 16:9 — "When you fail" vs. "when it fails"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "cum defeceritis" (when you fail/die), second person plural. The Greek ἐκλίπῃ is third person singular (when it [mammon] fails). The Hebrew בְּעֵת כְּלֹתוֹ (when it is consumed) follows the Greek reading with a third person singular reference.
- **Vulgate**: ut, cum defeceritis, recipiant vos
- **Greek**: ἵνα ὅταν ἐκλίπῃ δέξωνται ὑμᾶς
- **Hebrew**: לְמַעַן יַאַסְפוּ אֶתְכֶם בְּעֵת כְּלֹתוֹ
- **Note**: The Vulgate's "defeceritis" (when you die) has strong patristic support and is theologically significant. The Hebrew should follow the Vulgate here.

### 16:22 — "sepultus est in inferno"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate adds "in inferno" (in hell) to the rich man's burial, which is not present in the Greek text. The Greek simply says ἐτάφη (was buried). The Hebrew וַיִּקָּבַר (and was buried) follows the Greek, omitting the Vulgate's "in inferno."
- **Vulgate**: Mortuus est autem et dives, et sepultus est in inferno.
- **Greek**: ἀπέθανεν δὲ καὶ ὁ πλούσιος καὶ ἐτάφη
- **Hebrew**: וְגַם-הֶעָשִׁיר מֵת וַיִּקָּבַר
- **Note**: The Vulgate's "in inferno" is a significant addition, connecting the burial directly to hell. This is a Vulgate-specific reading that should be reflected in a Vulgate-following text.

### 16:23 — Hades/Sheol/Inferno
- **Issue**: The Hebrew renders ᾅδῃ/inferno as שְׁאוֹל, which is the standard biblical underworld. The Vulgate's previous verse uses "inferno" for the burial, and this verse describes the rich man lifting his eyes in torments.
- **Note**: שְׁאוֹל is the appropriate biblical Hebrew term and adequately corresponds to both the Greek ᾅδης and the Vulgate's "inferno" in this context.

## Hebrew Translation Quality
### 16:1 — Steward vocabulary
- **Hebrew**: סֹכֵן עַל-בֵּיתוֹ
- **Note**: סֹכֵן is a rare but genuinely biblical word (Isaiah 22:15, where Shebna is called הַסֹּכֵן). Excellent choice over more common alternatives.

### 16:8 — "Sons of this world" / "sons of light"
- **Hebrew**: בְנֵי הָעוֹלָם הַזֶּה... מִבְּנֵי הָאוֹר
- **Note**: בְּנֵי אוֹר is well attested in the Dead Sea Scrolls (1QS 1:9, 3:13, etc.) and represents authentic late Second Temple Hebrew. The phrase also appears in the War Scroll. This Hebrew rendering connects well with its Jewish literary milieu.

### 16:19 — Purple and fine linen
- **Hebrew**: אַרְגָּמָן וָשֵׁשׁ
- **Note**: Excellent biblical pair. Both words appear together in descriptions of the Tabernacle (Exodus 25:4, 26:1) and in Proverbs 31:22. This is precisely how a biblical author would describe luxury clothing.

### 16:26 — The great gulf
- **Hebrew**: גֵּיא גָדוֹל מַפְרִיד
- **Note**: גֵּיא (valley/chasm) is biblical (e.g., Joel 4:2, 4:12). The rendering captures the Vulgate's "chaos magnum" and Greek χάσμα μέγα effectively, though גֵּיא typically refers to a geographical valley rather than an abyss.

## Other Notes
### 16:17 — Law and prophets
- The Hebrew קוֹץ for κεραίαν/apicem ("tittle/serif") is noteworthy. קוֹץ means "thorn" in biblical Hebrew but was used in later Hebrew for the ornamental strokes on Hebrew letters. This is a natural semantic development.

### 16:29 — Moses and the Prophets
- The Hebrew משֶׁה (without the matres lectionis מֹשֶׁה) appears here and at 16:31. This is a variant spelling found in some Hebrew traditions.
