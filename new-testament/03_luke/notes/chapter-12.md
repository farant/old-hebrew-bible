# Luke Chapter 12 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 59 verses align across all three sources.

## Content Alignment

### 12:31 — "primum" and "justitiam ejus"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Verumtamen quaerite primum regnum Dei, et justitiam ejus" (seek first the kingdom of God, and his justice). The SBLGNT reads πλὴν ζητεῖτε τὴν βασιλείαν αὐτοῦ (but seek his kingdom), lacking both "first" and "and his justice." The Hebrew has אַךְ דִּרְשׁוּ אֶת-מַלְכוּת הָאֱלֹהִים (but seek the kingdom of God), following the SBLGNT in omitting "first" and "his justice."
- **Vulgate**: Verumtamen quaerite primum regnum Dei, et justitiam ejus : et haec omnia adjicientur vobis
- **Greek**: πλὴν ζητεῖτε τὴν βασιλείαν αὐτοῦ, καὶ ταῦτα προστεθήσεται ὑμῖν
- **Hebrew**: אַךְ דִּרְשׁוּ אֶת-מַלְכוּת הָאֱלֹהִים וְנוֹסַף לָכֶם כָּל-אֵלֶּה
- **Note**: The Vulgate adds "primum" (first) and "et justitiam ejus" (and his justice), harmonizing with Matt 6:33. The Hebrew omits both additions. To follow the Vulgate, the Hebrew should include בָּרִאשׁוֹנָה (first) and וְאֶת-צִדְקָתוֹ (and his righteousness).

### 12:38 — Watch divisions
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Et si venerit in secunda vigilia, et si in tertia vigilia venerit" (if he comes in the second watch or the third watch). The SBLGNT has κἂν ἐν τῇ δευτέρᾳ κἂν ἐν τῇ τρίτῃ φυλακῇ (whether in the second or third watch). The Hebrew matches: בָּאַשְׁמֻרָה הַשֵּׁנִית אוֹ בָּאַשְׁמֻרָה הַשְּׁלִישִׁית.
- **Note**: Alignment is good. The Hebrew אַשְׁמוּרָה (watch) is proper biblical Hebrew for a night watch (Judg 7:19, Exod 14:24, Ps 90:4).

### 12:46 — "infidelibus" vs. "ἀπίστων"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "partemque ejus cum infidelibus ponet" (and will place his portion with the unfaithful). The SBLGNT has μετὰ τῶν ἀπίστων (with the unfaithful). The Hebrew has עִם-הַסּוֹרֲרִים (with the rebellious/stubborn).
- **Vulgate**: partemque ejus cum infidelibus ponet
- **Hebrew**: וְשָׂם אֶת-חֶלְקוֹ עִם-הַסּוֹרֲרִים
- **Note**: הַסּוֹרֲרִים (the rebellious) shifts the meaning from "unfaithful/unbelieving" to "rebellious/stubborn." The Vulgate "infidelibus" means faithless ones. A closer Hebrew equivalent would be חֲסְרֵי אֱמוּנָה or with the root כחש/מרד. However, סוֹרֲרִים has strong biblical resonance (Deut 21:18, Ps 78:8, Isa 30:1) and captures moral rebelliousness.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 12:6-7 — Sparrows and providence
- **Hebrew**: הֲלֹא תִמָּכַרְנָה חָמֵשׁ צִפֳּרִים בִּשְׁנֵי אִסָּרִים... גַּם-שַׂעֲרוֹת רֹאשְׁכֶם נִמְנוֹת כֻּלָּן
- **Note**: The word צִפֳּרִים (birds/sparrows) is good biblical Hebrew (Ps 84:4, Prov 26:2). אִסָּרִים (assarii, a Roman copper coin) is a necessary loanword with no biblical equivalent. The hair-counting imagery echoes 1 Sam 14:45 and 2 Sam 14:11, where "not a hair shall fall" signifies divine protection.

### 12:19 — Rich fool's soliloquy
- **Hebrew**: נַפְשִׁי יֶשׁ-לָךְ טוֹבָה הַרְבֵּה לְשָׁנִים רַבּוֹת הִנָּפְשִׁי אִכְלִי שְׁתִי וְשִׂישִׂי
- **Note**: The self-address to the soul (נַפְשִׁי) echoes Ps 42:6,12; 43:5 (מַה-תִּשְׁתּוֹחֲחִי נַפְשִׁי). The sequence אִכְלִי שְׁתִי וְשִׂישִׂי (eat, drink, and be merry) echoes Eccl 8:15 (לֶאֱכוֹל וְלִשְׁתּוֹת וְלִשְׂמוֹחַ). The imperative forms addressed to a feminine noun (נֶפֶשׁ is feminine) are correctly formed.

### 12:20 — "Stulte" / "Fool"
- **Hebrew**: אַתָּה הַכְּסִיל
- **Note**: The Vulgate "Stulte" (Fool!) is rendered הַכְּסִיל (the fool), which is the standard biblical wisdom-literature term for a fool (Prov 1:22, 10:1, 26:1, Eccl 2:14). The direct address echoes Ps 14:1 / 53:2 (אָמַר נָבָל בְּלִבּוֹ, the fool says in his heart), though Delitzsch uses כְּסִיל rather than נָבָל here.

### 12:24 — Ravens
- **Hebrew**: הִתְבּוֹנֲנוּ אֶל-הָעֹרְבִים
- **Note**: Luke uniquely specifies κόρακας / "corvos" (ravens), not generic birds. The Hebrew עֹרְבִים (ravens) connects to the OT tradition where ravens are provided for by God (Job 38:41, Ps 147:9) and serve as God's agents to feed Elijah (1 Kings 17:4-6). The verb הִתְבּוֹנֲנוּ (consider/contemplate) is appropriately weighty biblical Hebrew.

### 12:27 — Lilies
- **Hebrew**: הִתְבּוֹנֲנוּ אֶל-הַשּׁוֹשַׁנִּים הַצֹּמְחוֹת וְאֵינָן טֹוֹת וְאֵינָן אֹרְגוֹת
- **Note**: שׁוֹשַׁנִּים (lilies) echoes Song of Songs (2:1-2, 4:5, 6:3). The verbs טוה (to spin) and ארג (to weave) are both well-attested biblical vocabulary (Exod 35:25-26, Judg 16:13, Isa 59:5). The comparison with Solomon בְּכָל-הֲדָרוֹ (in all his splendor) is an effective choice; הָדָר (splendor/glory) appears in Ps 104:1, 111:3.

### 12:35 — Girded loins
- **Hebrew**: מָתְנֵיכֶם יִהְיוּ חֲגוּרִים
- **Note**: The image of girded loins for readiness is deeply biblical (Exod 12:11 for Passover readiness, 1 Kings 18:46 for Elijah, 2 Kings 4:29 for Gehazi). The Hebrew is concise and authentic.

### 12:49 — Fire on earth
- **Hebrew**: אֲנִי בָּאתִי לְשַׁלַּח אֵשׁ בָּאָרֶץ
- **Note**: The prophetic fire imagery resonates with Jer 5:14 (הִנְנִי נֹתֵן דְּבָרַי בְּפִיךָ לְאֵשׁ) and Mal 3:2-3 (כְּאֵשׁ מְצָרֵף). The construction is clean and forceful.

## Other Notes

### 12:10 — Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit
The Hebrew distinction between speaking against בֶּן-הָאָדָם (Son of Man) as forgivable, and blaspheming רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ (the Holy Spirit) as unforgivable, is clearly rendered. The verb מְגַדֵּף (to blaspheme) is post-biblical in this specific sense, though the root גדף appears in biblical Hebrew with the sense of reviling (Num 15:30, 2 Kings 19:6, 22).

### 12:50 — Baptism of suffering
The Hebrew טְבִילָה (immersion/baptism) for the Vulgate's "baptismo" in the context of Christ's passion is a significant theological image. The word טְבִילָה is post-biblical; biblical Hebrew uses the root טבל in its basic sense of dipping/immersing (2 Kings 5:14, Ruth 2:14).

### 12:53 — Micah 7:6 allusion
The Hebrew division of household members against each other echoes Micah 7:6 (כִּי-בֵן מְנַבֵּל אָב בַּת קָמָה בְאִמָּהּ כַּלָּה בַּחֲמֹתָהּ). Delitzsch's rendering captures the same familial pairs, making the prophetic connection clear.
