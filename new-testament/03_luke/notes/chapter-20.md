# Luke Chapter 20 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 47 verses align across all three sources. No versification discrepancies.

## Content Alignment
### 20:23 — "Quid me tentatis?"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate includes "Quid me tentatis?" (Why do you tempt me?) which is absent from the SBLGNT Greek. The Hebrew includes this phrase: מַה-תְּנַסּוּנִי (why do you test me?), but places it at the start of v. 24 rather than v. 23.
- **Vulgate**: Considerans autem dolum illorum, dixit ad eos : Quid me tentatis?
- **Greek**: κατανοήσας δὲ αὐτῶν τὴν πανουργίαν εἶπεν πρὸς αὐτούς
- **Hebrew**: ...וַיֹּאמֶר לָהֶם מַה-תְּנַסּוּנִי (in v. 24)
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly preserves the Vulgate's addition but splits it across the verse boundary. Acceptable.

### 20:24 — "panourgia" / "dolum"
- **Issue**: The Greek πανουργίαν means "craftiness/trickery." The Vulgate uses "dolum" (deceit). The Hebrew נִכְלֵיהֶם (their deceits/plots) captures the Vulgate's "dolum" well, using a strong biblical term (cf. Genesis 27:35, Numbers 25:18).
- **Note**: Good correspondence with the Vulgate.

### 20:42-43 — Psalm 110:1 citation
- **Issue**: This is a critical Christological text. The Hebrew renders it as נְאֻם-יְהוָֹה לַאדֹנִי שֵׁב לִימִינִי עַד-אָשִׁית אֹיְבֶיךָ הֲדֹם לְרַגְלֶיךָ, directly quoting the Hebrew of Psalm 110:1 (MT). Both the Vulgate ("Dixit Dominus Domino meo") and the Greek (Εἶπεν κύριος τῷ κυρίῳ μου) translate from this psalm.
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly returns to the source psalm. The use of the original Hebrew rather than a back-translation from Greek/Latin is appropriate.

## Hebrew Translation Quality
### 20:17 — Psalm 118:22 citation
- **Hebrew**: אֶבֶן מָאֲסוּ הַבּוֹנִים הָיְתָה לְרֹאשׁ פִּנָּה
- **Note**: This is an exact quotation of Psalm 118:22 in the Masoretic Text. The Hebrew correctly returns to the source psalm rather than back-translating from Greek or Latin.

### 20:18 — Crushed and scattered
- **Hebrew**: יִשָּׁבֵר (will be broken) ... תִּשְׁחָקֵהוּ (will grind him)
- **Note**: The verb שָׁחַק (to grind, pulverize) appears in Exodus 32:20, Deuteronomy 9:21, and 2 Chronicles 34:7. This is the correct biblical verb for the Vulgate's "comminuet" (will crush to powder) and Greek λικμήσει (will winnow/scatter).

### 20:36 — "Equal to the angels"
- **Hebrew**: שָׁוִים הֵם לַמַּלְאָכִים (they are equal to the angels)
- **Note**: שָׁוֶה (equal) is biblical (Esther 3:8, 5:13, 7:4). The Greek ἰσάγγελοι (equal to angels) is a unique compound word. The Hebrew periphrastic construction is an appropriate rendering since Hebrew lacks compound adjectives of this type.

### 20:46 — "Stolas" / "Tallitot"
- **Hebrew**: עֲטוּפֵי טַלִּית (wrapped in tallitot)
- **Note**: The rendering of στολαῖς / "stolis" (long robes) as טַלִּית (prayer shawl) is an interpretive choice that Judaizes the text. The Greek and Latin refer to impressive outer garments in general, not specifically to prayer shawls. A more neutral rendering would use a term like בְּגָדִים or מְעִילִים.

## Other Notes
### 20:37 — "At the Bush"
- The Hebrew בַּסְּנֶה (at the bush/in the bush passage) correctly identifies the reference to the burning bush narrative (Exodus 3). This matches both the Vulgate "secus rubum" and the Greek ἐπὶ τῆς βάτου. The word סְנֶה is the exact term used in Exodus 3:2-4.
