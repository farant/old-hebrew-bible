# Luke Chapter 14 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 35 verses align across all three sources.

## Content Alignment
### 14:5 — "Son or ox" vs. "donkey or ox"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "asinus, aut bos" (donkey or ox). The Greek has υἱὸς ἢ βοῦς (son or ox). The Hebrew has חֲמֹרוֹ אוֹ שׁוֹרוֹ (his donkey or his ox), following the Vulgate.
- **Vulgate**: Cujus vestrum asinus, aut bos in puteum cadet
- **Greek**: Τίνος ὑμῶν υἱὸς ἢ βοῦς (son or ox)
- **Hebrew**: אֲשֶׁר חֲמֹרוֹ אוֹ שׁוֹרוֹ (his donkey or his ox)
- **Note**: The Vulgate and Hebrew agree against the Greek here. This is a well-known textual variant. The Vulgate reading is to be followed.

### 14:3 — Split verse content
- **Issue**: In the Greek text, verse 3 ends with "ἢ οὔ" (or not) and the response "they were silent" begins verse 4. The Hebrew combines the question and the silence in verse 3 (וַיַּחֲרִישׁוּ), leaving verse 4 with only the action of healing. The Vulgate similarly places "At illi tacuerunt" at the start of verse 4.
- **Note**: Minor rearrangement. The Vulgate's division should be followed: the silence belongs at the beginning of verse 4.

## Hebrew Translation Quality
### 14:8 — Wedding feast vocabulary
- **Hebrew**: כִּי-יִקְרָא אֹתְךָ אִישׁ אֶל-הַחֲתֻנָּה
- **Note**: חֲתֻנָּה is post-biblical (Mishnaic). Biblical Hebrew uses מִשְׁתֶּה (feast/banquet) for wedding celebrations. The Latin "nuptias" and Greek γάμους both mean wedding, but the Hebrew vocabulary choice is anachronistic.

### 14:11 — Excellent proverbial form
- **Hebrew**: כִּי כָּל-הַמְרוֹמֵם עַצְמוֹ יִשָּׁפֵל וְהַמַּשְׁפִּיל אֶת עַצְמוֹ יְרוֹמָם
- **Note**: Fine biblical Hebrew with good use of hif'il and nif'al stems. Echoes Ezekiel 21:31 (הַשָּׁפָלָה הַגְבֵּהַ וְהַגָּבֹהַ הַשְׁפִּיל) and Proverbs 29:23.

### 14:26 — Hate one's own soul
- **Hebrew**: וְאַף גַּם-אֶת-נַפְשׁוֹ
- **Note**: Good rendering of ψυχήν/animam as נֶפֶשׁ, the standard biblical equivalent with the full semantic range of "life/soul/self."

### 14:34 — Salt metaphor
- **Hebrew**: טוֹב הַמֶּלַח וְאִם הַמֶּלַח הָיָה תָפֵל
- **Note**: Excellent. תָּפֵל (tasteless, unseasoned) is a genuine biblical word (Job 6:6, Ezekiel 13:10-15). This is a better choice than any Mishnaic alternative.

## Other Notes
### 14:27 — Cross-bearing
- The Hebrew צְלוּב for σταυρόν/crucem is a post-biblical coinage but is the standard term in Delitzsch. Biblical Hebrew has no native word for cross.
