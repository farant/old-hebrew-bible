# Luke Chapter 23 — Translation Notes

## Versification
### 23:17 — Vulgate includes, SBLGNT omits
- The Vulgate includes verse 17: "Necesse autem habebat dimittere eis per diem festum unum" (Now he had to release one to them at the festival). The SBLGNT has no Greek text for this verse (empty field). The Hebrew provides a corresponding text: וְלוֹ הָיָה לְהַתִּיר לָהֶם חָבוּשׁ אֶחָד בִּימֵי הֶחָג (and it was for him to release to them one prisoner on the days of the festival).
- **Note**: This verse should be retained as Vulgate text. It explains the Barabbas custom and is necessary for narrative coherence with vv. 18-25.

## Content Alignment
### 23:11 — "veste alba" vs. ἐσθῆτα λαμπράν
- **Issue**: The Vulgate says "veste alba" (white garment), while the Greek has ἐσθῆτα λαμπράν (shining/bright garment). The Hebrew בֶּגֶד זְהוֹרִית follows neither precisely: זְהוֹרִית means "scarlet/crimson" (cf. Genesis 38:28, Joshua 2:18), suggesting a scarlet garment.
- **Vulgate**: indutum veste alba
- **Greek**: ἐσθῆτα λαμπράν
- **Hebrew**: בֶּגֶד זְהוֹרִית
- **Note**: The Hebrew renders this as a scarlet garment, which matches neither the Vulgate's white nor the Greek's bright/shining. This may be a confusion with the scarlet robe in Matthew 27:28. A Vulgate-following text should use בֶּגֶד לָבָן (white garment).

### 23:23 — "et voces eorum et principum sacerdotum"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate simply says "et invalescebant voces eorum" (and their voices grew strong). The Hebrew adds וְקוֹל רָאשֵׁי הַכֹּהֲנִים (and the voice of the chief priests), which is not present in the Vulgate or the SBLGNT Greek for this verse. This appears to be a Delitzsch addition, possibly harmonizing with manuscript variants.
- **Note**: This addition does not follow the Vulgate and should be noted.

### 23:34 — "Pater, dimitte illis"
- **Issue**: The words of Jesus on the cross "Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do" are in the Vulgate. The SBLGNT brackets them (⸂...⸃) indicating manuscript uncertainty. The Hebrew includes them: אָבִי סְלַח לָהֶם כִּי אֵינָם יֹדְעִים מָה הֵם עֹשִׂים.
- **Note**: These words must be retained as Vulgate text. The Hebrew rendering is accurate.

### 23:38 — Trilingual inscription
- **Issue**: The Vulgate specifies the inscription was "litteris græcis, et latinis, et hebraicis" (in Greek, Latin, and Hebrew letters). The SBLGNT Greek omits the trilingual detail. The Hebrew includes it: בִּכְתָב יְוָנִי וְרוֹמִי וְעִבְרִי (in Greek, Roman, and Hebrew writing), matching the Vulgate.
- **Note**: Good alignment with the Vulgate on this significant detail.

### 23:42 — "Domine" / Ἰησοῦ
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Domine, memento mei" (Lord, remember me). The Greek reads Ἰησοῦ, μνήσθητί μου (Jesus, remember me). The Hebrew combines both concepts: אֲדֹנִי (my Lord) for the address, aligning with the Vulgate.
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the Vulgate's "Domine" here rather than the Greek's direct name "Jesus."

### 23:43 — Paradise
- **Hebrew**: בְּגַן-עֵדֶן (in the Garden of Eden)
- **Note**: The Greek παραδείσῳ and Vulgate "paradiso" both derive from the Persian/Hebrew term for an enclosed garden. The Hebrew rendering as גַּן-עֵדֶן explicitly identifies paradise with the Garden of Eden from Genesis 2-3, which is an interpretive choice that goes beyond the source texts. The Greek παράδεισος in this context refers to a heavenly dwelling, not necessarily the primordial garden.

### 23:50-51 — Joseph of Arimathea description
- **Issue**: The Hebrew adds מִן-הָרָמָתַיִם עִיר הַיְּהוּדִים (from Ramathaim, a city of the Jews) in v. 50, but the Vulgate places this detail in v. 51: "ab Arimathæa civitate Judææ." The Hebrew shifts the geographical information one verse earlier.
- **Note**: Minor verse boundary difference.

## Hebrew Translation Quality
### 23:31 — Green wood and dry
- **Hebrew**: בָּעֵץ הַלָּח... בַּיָּבֵשׁ (in the green wood... in the dry)
- **Note**: Excellent biblical diction. The contrast between לַח (moist/green) and יָבֵשׁ (dry) for wood is attested in Ezekiel 17:24 (עֵץ לַח / עֵץ יָבֵשׁ) and Ezekiel 21:3 (עֵץ-לַח וְעֵץ-יָבֵשׁ). The Delitzsch translation matches the Ezekiel phraseology exactly.

### 23:33 — Calvary / Golgotha
- **Hebrew**: גֻּלְגָּלְתָּא (Golgotha)
- **Note**: The Hebrew uses the Aramaic form גֻּלְגָּלְתָּא rather than the Hebrew גֻּלְגֹּלֶת (skull, cf. Judges 9:53, 2 Kings 9:35). The Vulgate identifies the place as "Calvariæ" (of the skull), and the Greek as Κρανίον (skull). The Aramaic form is appropriate here as it reflects the spoken language of the region.

### 23:45 — Temple veil
- **Hebrew**: פָרֹכֶת הַהֵיכָל (the veil of the temple)
- **Note**: פָּרֹכֶת is the exact biblical term for the inner veil of the tabernacle/temple (Exodus 26:31-33, 2 Chronicles 3:14). הֵיכָל for ναός / "templi" is correct as it specifically denotes the inner sanctuary rather than the temple complex as a whole.

### 23:46 — "In manus tuas"
- **Hebrew**: בְּיָדְךָ אַפְקִיד רוּחִי (into your hand I commit my spirit)
- **Note**: This is a close paraphrase of Psalm 31:6 (MT): בְּיָדְךָ אַפְקִיד רוּחִי. The Hebrew correctly returns to the psalm source. The verb פָּקַד in the hiphil means "to entrust, deposit, commit." Both the Vulgate "commendo" and the Greek παρατίθεμαι correspond to this Hebrew verb.

## Other Notes
### 23:44-45 — Darkness and veil
- The sequence of darkness (sixth to ninth hour) followed by the tearing of the veil is rendered faithfully. The Hebrew וַיֶּחְשַׁךְ הַשָּׁמֶשׁ (and the sun was darkened) matches the Vulgate's "obscuratus est sol" while the Greek reads τοῦ ἡλίου ἐκλιπόντος (the sun failing/being eclipsed). The Hebrew verb חָשַׁךְ is neutral between an eclipse and miraculous darkening, matching the Vulgate's neutrality.
