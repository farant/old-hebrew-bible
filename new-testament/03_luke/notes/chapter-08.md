# Luke Chapter 8 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 56 verses align across all three sources.

## Content Alignment

### 8:3 — "ministrabant ei" vs. "ministrabant eis"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "ministrabant ei" (ministered to him, singular). The SBLGNT has αὐτοῖς (to them, plural) in the critical text, though some manuscripts read αὐτῷ (to him). The Hebrew שֵׁרְתֻהוּ (they served him) follows the Vulgate singular.
- **Vulgate**: quæ ministrabant ei de facultatibus suis
- **Greek**: αἵτινες διηκόνουν αὐτοῖς ἐκ τῶν ὑπαρχόντων αὐταῖς
- **Hebrew**: אֲשֶׁר שֵׁרְתֻהוּ מִנִּכְסֵיהֶן
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly follows the Vulgate reading. The plural "them" (referring to Jesus and the twelve) is the SBLGNT preference.

### 8:26 — "Gerasenorum" vs. "Gadarenes"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Gerasenorum" (Gerasenes). The Hebrew has הַגַּדְרִיִּים (Gadarenes). The SBLGNT has Γερασηνῶν (Gerasenes).
- **Vulgate**: ad regionem Gerasenorum
- **Greek**: εἰς τὴν χώραν τῶν Γερασηνῶν
- **Hebrew**: אֶל-אֶרֶץ הַגַּדְרִיִּים
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the Matthean parallel (Matt 8:28, Gadarenes) rather than the Vulgate/Lukan reading. This is a significant departure: the Hebrew should read הַגְּרָסִיִּים to follow the Vulgate.

### 8:43 — Vulgate includes physician clause
- **Issue**: The Vulgate includes "quæ in medicos erogaverat omnem substantiam suam" (who had spent all her substance on physicians). The SBLGNT includes this in its critical text but marks it with a variant marker. The Hebrew includes the clause: אֲשֶׁר הוֹצִיאָה כָל-מִחְיָתָהּ לָרֹפְאִים.
- **Note**: All three texts align. Worth noting because some critical editions of the Greek omit this clause under influence from the Western text (Codex D), but the Vulgate and Hebrew both include it.

### 8:45 — Expanded text in Vulgate and Hebrew
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "dixit Petrus, et qui cum illo erant" (Peter said, and those who were with him). The SBLGNT reads simply "Peter said" (εἶπεν ὁ Πέτρος). The Hebrew has an expanded reading: פֶּטְרוֹס וְהָעֹמְדִים אֶצְלוֹ (Peter and those standing beside him).
- **Vulgate**: dixit Petrus, et qui cum illo erant
- **Greek**: εἶπεν ὁ Πέτρος
- **Hebrew**: וַיֹּאמֶר פֶּטְרוֹס וְהָעֹמְדִים אֶצְלוֹ
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the Vulgate's expanded reading, which reflects the Byzantine text tradition.

### 8:48 — "Filia" / "Daughter"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "Filia" (Daughter). The SBLGNT has Θυγάτηρ. The Hebrew has [חִזְקִי] בִּתִּי, where חִזְקִי ("be strong") is in brackets, and בִּתִּי (my daughter) follows.
- **Vulgate**: Filia, fides tua salvam te fecit
- **Hebrew**: [חִזְקִי] בִּתִּי אֱמוּנָתֵךְ הוֹשִׁיעָה לָּךְ
- **Note**: The bracketed חִזְקִי (be strong/take courage) has no equivalent in the Vulgate or SBLGNT. It may be Delitzsch's exegetical addition or reflect a variant reading. It should be noted as extraneous to the Vulgate.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 8:5 — Parable of the Sower
- **Hebrew**: הַזּוֹרֵעַ יָצָא לִזְרֹעַ אֶת-זַרְעוֹ
- **Note**: Clean biblical Hebrew using the participial construction followed by a narrative verb. The pattern echoes agricultural language throughout the OT (cf. Isa 55:10, Eccl 11:6).

### 8:10 — Isaiah 6:9 allusion
- **Hebrew**: לְמַעַן בִּרְאֹתָם לֹא יִרְאוּ וּבְשָׁמְעָם לֹא יָבִינוּ
- **Note**: Directly echoes Isaiah 6:9 (שִׁמְעוּ שָׁמוֹעַ וְאַל-תָּבִינוּ וּרְאוּ רָאוֹ וְאַל-תֵּדָעוּ). Delitzsch captures the paradox well, though the Hebrew construction differs somewhat from the Isaianic original.

### 8:12 — הַשָּׂטָן for "diabolus"
- **Hebrew**: בָּא הַשָּׂטָן
- **Note**: The Vulgate has "diabolus" and the Greek has ὁ διάβολος. Delitzsch uses הַשָּׂטָן (the Satan/Adversary), which is the standard biblical Hebrew term (Job 1:6-12, Zech 3:1-2). This is a sound translation choice that roots the NT figure in OT language.

### 8:22 — הַיָּם for "stagnum"
- **Hebrew**: נַעְבְּרָה אֶל-עֵבֶר הַיָּם
- **Note**: The Vulgate has "stagnum" (lake) and the Greek has λίμνη (lake). Delitzsch uses הַיָּם (the sea), following the convention of the Sea of Galilee (יָם כִּנֶּרֶת, Num 34:11). This is the correct biblical convention.

## Other Notes

### 8:31 — הַתְּהוֹם for "abyssum"
The Hebrew הַתְּהוֹם (the deep/abyss) for the Vulgate's "abyssum" and Greek ἄβυσσον is an excellent choice. תְּהוֹם is a primal biblical term (Gen 1:2, Gen 7:11, Ps 71:20) that carries far more resonance than a transliteration would.

### 8:54 — הַיַּלְדָּה קוּמִי
The Hebrew הַיַּלְדָּה קוּמִי (Girl, arise!) recalls the Aramaic Talitha koum preserved in Mark 5:41. Delitzsch renders in Hebrew rather than preserving the Aramaic, which is appropriate for a Hebrew translation.
