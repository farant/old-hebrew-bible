# Luke Chapter 13 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 35 verses align across all three sources.

## Content Alignment
### 13:15 — Singular vs. Plural "Hypocrites"
- **Issue**: Greek has plural Ὑποκριταί and Vulgate has plural "Hypocritae," but Hebrew has singular הֶחָנֵף.
- **Vulgate**: Hypocritæ
- **Hebrew**: הֶחָנֵף (singular)
- **Note**: Delitzsch singularized the address. The Vulgate's plural should be followed; the Hebrew should read הַחֲנֵפִים.

### 13:25 — "When he shall have entered" vs. "From the day when he rises"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Cum autem intraverit paterfamilias" (when the master of the house shall have entered), while the Greek has ἀφʼ οὗ ἂν ἐγερθῇ (from the time when he rises). The Hebrew מִיּוֹם אֲשֶׁר יָקוּם follows the Greek ("from the day when he rises"), not the Vulgate ("when he enters").
- **Vulgate**: Cum autem intraverit paterfamilias, et clauserit ostium
- **Greek**: ἀφʼ οὗ ἂν ἐγερθῇ ὁ οἰκοδεσπότης καὶ ἀποκλείσῃ τὴν θύραν
- **Hebrew**: מִיּוֹם אֲשֶׁר יָקוּם בַּעַל הַבַּיִת וְסָגַר אֶת-הַדָּלֶת
- **Note**: The Vulgate's "intraverit" (shall have entered) differs significantly from the Greek "ἐγερθῇ" (rises up). The Hebrew follows the Greek rather than the Vulgate here.

### 13:35 — "deserta" (desolate)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate includes "deserta" (desolate) — "relinquetur vobis domus vestra deserta." The Hebrew includes שָׁמֵם in brackets, suggesting awareness that this word is textually uncertain. The SBLGNT omits the word.
- **Vulgate**: Ecce relinquetur vobis domus vestra deserta.
- **Greek**: ἰδοὺ ἀφίεται ὑμῖν ὁ οἶκος ὑμῶν (no "deserted")
- **Hebrew**: הִנֵּה בֵיתְכֶם יֵעָזֵב לָכֶם [שָׁמֵם]
- **Note**: The Hebrew brackets indicate uncertainty. The Vulgate reading with "deserta" should be followed per our methodology. The brackets in the Hebrew are a useful editorial indicator.

## Hebrew Translation Quality
### 13:7 — Excellent biblical Hebrew in the fig tree parable
- **Hebrew**: הִנֵּה-זֶה שָׁלשׁ שָׁנִים אָנֹכִי בָא לְבַקֵּשׁ פְּרִי
- **Note**: Excellent use of אָנֹכִי (the older/more literary first person pronoun) and classical narrative construction. The vocabulary (כֹּרֵם for vinedresser, דֹּמֶן for dung) is solidly biblical.

### 13:19 — Mishnaic שֶׁל construction
- **Hebrew**: לְגַרְגַּר שֶׁל-חַרְדָּל
- **Note**: The שֶׁל construction is post-biblical/Mishnaic. Biblical Hebrew would use a construct chain or a relative clause. However, חַרְדָּל (mustard) itself only appears in post-biblical Hebrew, so some accommodation is unavoidable.

### 13:27 — Excellent OT echo
- **Hebrew**: סוּרוּ מִמֶּנִּי כָּל-פֹּעֲלֵי הָאָוֶן
- **Note**: Direct echo of Psalm 6:9 (סוּרוּ מִמֶּנִּי כָּל-פֹּעֲלֵי אָוֶן), which is the LXX text Jesus appears to be quoting. Excellent choice by Delitzsch.

### 13:34 — Jerusalem lament
- **Hebrew**: יְרוּשָׁלַיִם יְרוּשָׁלַיִם הַהֹרֶגֶת אֶת-הַנְּבִיאִים
- **Note**: The doubling of Jerusalem and the participial construction are authentically biblical. The word תַּרְנְגֹלֶת (hen) is post-biblical; biblical Hebrew lacks a specific term for hen, so this is an unavoidable modernism.

## Other Notes
### 13:28 — Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob
- The patriarchal triad echoes Exodus 3:6 and many other OT passages. The Hebrew renders this perfectly with the standard biblical names.

### 13:35 — Psalm 118:26 quotation
- Hebrew correctly renders בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוָֹה, matching the Hebrew Psalter exactly.
