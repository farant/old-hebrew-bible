# Luke Chapter 1 -- Translation Notes

## Versification
All 80 verses align across all three sources.

## Content Alignment

### 1:28 -- Ave gratia plena
- **Issue**: The SBLGNT omits "benedicta tu in mulieribus" (blessed art thou among women). The Vulgate includes it. The Delitzsch Hebrew brackets it: [בְּרוּכָה אַתְּ בַּנָּשִׁים].
- **Vulgate**: "Ave gratia plena : Dominus tecum : benedicta tu in mulieribus."
- **Greek**: Χαῖρε, κεχαριτωμένη, ὁ κύριος μετὰ σοῦ. (SBLGNT omits the clause.)
- **Hebrew**: שָׁלוֹם לָךְ אֵשֶׁת-חֵן יְהוָֹה עִמָּךְ [בְּרוּכָה אַתְּ בַּנָּשִׁים]
- **Note**: The brackets in the Hebrew correctly indicate the phrase is textually disputed. The Vulgate reading should be followed. The phrase is attested in the Byzantine tradition and the parallel at 1:42. "Ave gratia plena" is rendered as שָׁלוֹם לָךְ אֵשֶׁת-חֵן, which translates the greeting as "peace to you, woman of grace" rather than the richer Latin sense of "full of grace" (plena gratia). A more precise Hebrew rendering might be מְלֵאַת-חֵן.

### 1:29 -- Quae cum audisset
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "quae cum audisset" (when she had heard), while the Greek has ἐπὶ τῷ λόγῳ (at the word) and the Hebrew has בִּרְאוֹתָהּ (when she saw), which is bracketed. The Vulgate emphasizes hearing, the Greek emphasizes the word itself, and the Hebrew introduces seeing.
- **Vulgate**: "Quae cum audisset, turbata est in sermone ejus"
- **Greek**: ἡ δὲ ἐπὶ τῷ λόγῳ διεταράχθη
- **Hebrew**: וְהִיא [בִּרְאוֹתָהּ] נִבְהֲלָה לִדְבָרוֹ
- **Note**: The Hebrew introduces a visual element not in either the Vulgate or SBLGNT. The brackets suggest awareness of the problem.

### 1:35 -- Quod nascetur ex te sanctum
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "quod nascetur ex te sanctum" (that which shall be born of thee, the Holy One), with "ex te" (from you). The SBLGNT lacks the equivalent of "ex te." The Hebrew has לַיִּלוֹד (to the one born) without "from you."
- **Vulgate**: "Ideoque et quod nascetur ex te sanctum, vocabitur Filius Dei."
- **Greek**: διὸ καὶ τὸ γεννώμενον ἅγιον κληθήσεται, υἱὸς θεοῦ
- **Hebrew**: עַל-כֵּן קָדוֹשׁ יֵאָמֵר לַיִּלוֹד בֶּן-הָאֱלֹהִים
- **Note**: The Vulgate "ex te" is theologically significant, affirming the birth from Mary. The Hebrew omits this. The Hebrew יֵאָמֵר (it will be said) is weaker than the Vulgate/Greek "vocabitur/kληθήσεται" (he shall be called).

### 1:46 -- Magnificat
- **Issue**: The Vulgate brackets the Magnificat (1:46-55) and Benedictus (1:68-79) as canticles. No textual divergence, but worth noting the liturgical framing.
- **Note**: The Hebrew translations of both canticles are notably strong, drawing on biblical psalm language throughout.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 1:1 -- Prologue vocabulary
- **Hebrew**: אַחֲרֵי אֲשֶׁר רַבִּים הוֹאִילוּ לְחַבֵּר סִפּוּר
- **Note**: סִפּוּר (story/account) is a post-biblical/Mishnaic term. Biblical Hebrew would use something like דִּבְרֵי or סֵפֶר. The verb הוֹאִילוּ (undertook/ventured) is excellent biblical Hebrew (cf. Deut 1:5, Judg 17:11).

### 1:3 -- הָאַדִּיר for "optime"
- **Hebrew**: הָאַדִּיר תְּאוֹפִילוֹס
- **Note**: הָאַדִּיר (the mighty/noble) is good biblical Hebrew (Judg 5:13, Ps 8:2) but renders κράτιστε/optime (most excellent) as "the mighty one," which slightly shifts the register from honorific address to a description of power. A more precise equivalent might be הַנִּכְבָּד.

### 1:5 -- מִמִּשְׁמֶרֶת אֲבִיָּה
- **Hebrew**: מִמִּשְׁמֶרֶת אֲבִיָּה
- **Note**: Excellent. מִשְׁמֶרֶת directly parallels the priestly course terminology in 1 Chr 24:10, where Abijah's division is listed eighth. This is precisely the biblical Hebrew term for the priestly courses.

### 1:7 -- וָלָד for "filius"
- **Hebrew**: וְלָהֶם אֵין וָלָד
- **Note**: The Vulgate has "filius" (son), and the Greek has τέκνον (child). The Hebrew וָלָד (offspring) is rare in biblical Hebrew (only Job 14:21 in some reckonings), though not strictly post-biblical. It captures the generic sense of τέκνον well.

### 1:15 -- Nazirite allusion
- **Hebrew**: וְיַיִן וְשֵׁכָר לֹא יִשְׁתֶּה
- **Note**: Directly echoes the Nazirite vow formula from Num 6:3 (יַיִן וְשֵׁכָר לֹא יִשְׁתֶּה) and the angelic announcement to Manoah's wife regarding Samson in Judg 13:4. The Hebrew perfectly captures this OT allusion present in all three texts.

### 1:37 -- כִּי לֹא-יִפָּלֵא מֵאֱלֹהִים כָּל-דָּבָר
- **Hebrew**: כִּי לֹא-יִפָּלֵא מֵאֱלֹהִים כָּל-דָּבָר
- **Note**: Outstanding. A near-direct quotation of Gen 18:14 (הֲיִפָּלֵא מֵיְהוָה דָּבָר), linking Gabriel's promise to Mary with God's promise to Sarah. The LXX of Gen 18:14 uses the same μὴ ἀδυνατήσει that Luke employs.

### 1:46-55 -- The Magnificat
- **Hebrew**: רוֹמֲמָה נַפְשִׁי אֶת-יְהוָֹה (1:46), שַׁדָּי (1:49), גֵּאִים (1:51)
- **Note**: The Magnificat translation is excellent throughout. שַׁדָּי for ὁ δυνατός/potens captures divine power with a patriarchal-era divine name. The vocabulary consistently draws from the Song of Hannah (1 Sam 2:1-10) and the Psalms. The phrase רְעֵבִים מִלֵּא-טוֹב (1:53) echoes Ps 107:9.

### 1:68-79 -- The Benedictus
- **Hebrew**: קֶרֶן יְשׁוּעָה (1:69), צַלְמָוֶת (1:79)
- **Note**: קֶרֶן יְשׁוּעָה (horn of salvation) directly echoes Ps 18:3 / 2 Sam 22:3. The phrase לְישְׁבֵי חשֶׁךְ וְצַלְמָוֶת (1:79) echoes Ps 107:10 and Isa 9:1. הַנֹּגַהּ מִמָּרוֹם (1:78) for "oriens ex alto" is poetic and effective.

## Other Notes

### 1:17 -- Malachi 3:24 / 4:6 allusion
The entire verse alludes to Mal 3:23-24 (ET 4:5-6). The Hebrew לְהָשִׁיב לֵב אָבוֹת עַל-בָּנִים closely follows the Malachi text. This is a key LXX connection since Luke follows the LXX form of the Malachi prophecy.

### 1:32-33 -- Davidic covenant
The Hebrew וַיהוָֹה אֱלֹהִים יִתֶּן-לוֹ אֶת-כִּסֵּא דָּוִד אָבִיו echoes 2 Sam 7:12-16 and Isa 9:6 (the throne of David). The phrase וּלְמַלְכוּתוֹ אֵין קֵץ closely parallels Dan 7:14 (מַלְכוּתֵהּ... לָא תִתְחַבַּל, his kingdom shall not be destroyed).

### 1:38 -- הִנְנִי שִׁפְחַת יְהוָֹה
The Hebrew הִנְנִי (here I am) carries strong patriarchal resonance (Gen 22:1, 1 Sam 3:4). שִׁפְחָה (handmaid) for δούλη/ancilla is appropriate and echoes Hannah's self-description in 1 Sam 1:11.
