# Mark Chapter 11 — Translation Notes

## Versification

The Vulgate includes 11:26 ("Quod si vos non dimiseritis : nec Pater vester, qui in caelis est, dimittet vobis peccata vestra"), which is absent from the SBLGNT (the Greek field is empty). The Delitzsch Hebrew includes the verse: וְאַתֶּם אִם-לֹא תִמְחֲלוּ אַף-אֲבִיכֶם שֶׁבַּשָּׁמַיִם לֹא-יִסְלַח לְפִשְׁעֵיכֶם. Follow the Vulgate: this verse is retained. It is a complement to 11:25 (on forgiveness in prayer), harmonizing with Matthew 6:15.

## Content Alignment

### 11:1 — "Bethphage and Bethany" vs "Bethany" only
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Jerosolymae et Bethaniae" (Jerusalem and Bethany), omitting Bethphage. The SBLGNT includes both: "eis Bethphage kai Bethanian." The Hebrew includes both: אֶל-בֵּית-פַּגֵּי וּבֵית-הִינִי.
- **Vulgate**: *Jerosolymae et Bethaniae* (no Bethphage)
- **Greek**: *eis Bethphage kai Bethanian*
- **Hebrew**: אֶל-בֵּית-פַּגֵּי וּבֵית-הִינִי (Bethphage and Bethany)
- **Note**: Follow the Vulgate. The Hebrew includes Bethphage, which the Vulgate omits. Strictly following the Vulgate, בֵּית-פַּגֵּי should be removed and the text should read only אֶל-בֵּית-הִינִי. However, this is a minor geographical detail where the broader manuscript tradition supports both names.

### 11:8 — "De arboribus" (from the trees) vs "from the fields"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "frondes caedebant de arboribus" (branches cut from the trees). The SBLGNT reads "stibadas kopsantes ek ton agron" (leafy branches cut from the fields). The Hebrew follows the Vulgate with מִן-הָעֵצִים (from the trees).
- **Vulgate**: *de arboribus* (from the trees)
- **Greek**: *ek ton agron* (from the fields)
- **Hebrew**: מִן-הָעֵצִים (from the trees)
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly aligns with the Vulgate. No change needed.

### 10 — "[בְּשֵׁם יְהוֹה]" bracket notation
- **Issue**: The Hebrew text for 11:10 includes the phrase בְּשֵׁם יְהוֹה in brackets: בְּרוּכָה מַלְכוּת דָּוִד אָבִינוּ הַבָּאָה [בְּשֵׁם יְהוֹה]. Neither the Vulgate nor the SBLGNT includes "in the name of the Lord" in this verse (it belongs only to 11:9). The brackets indicate Delitzsch considered this an addition.
- **Vulgate**: *benedictum quod venit regnum patris nostri David* (no "in the name of the Lord")
- **Greek**: No *en onomati kyriou* in v.10
- **Hebrew**: [בְּשֵׁם יְהוֹה] — bracketed
- **Note**: Follow the Vulgate. The bracketed phrase should be removed, as it is not in the Vulgate and Delitzsch himself flagged it as uncertain.

### 11:26 — Vulgate-only verse on unforgiveness
- **Issue**: See Versification above. This verse is the negative complement of 11:25. It is absent from the SBLGNT but present in the Vulgate and the Hebrew.
- **Vulgate**: *Quod si vos non dimiseritis : nec Pater vester, qui in caelis est, dimittet vobis peccata vestra.*
- **Greek**: (absent)
- **Hebrew**: וְאַתֶּם אִם-לֹא תִמְחֲלוּ אַף-אֲבִיכֶם שֶׁבַּשָּׁמַיִם לֹא-יִסְלַח לְפִשְׁעֵיכֶם
- **Note**: Follow the Vulgate. Retain this verse. The Hebrew rendering is correct and mirrors the structure of 11:25.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 11:9 — הוֹשַׁע-נָא (Hosanna)
- **Hebrew**: הוֹשַׁע-נָא בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוֹה
- **Note**: Excellent. The Hebrew renders "Hosanna" as הוֹשַׁע-נָא (save now, please), which is the original Hebrew behind the Greek/Latin transliteration, from Psalm 118:25. The following בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוֹה quotes Psalm 118:26 verbatim from the Masoretic text. This is precisely how the crowd's acclamation would have sounded in Hebrew.

### 11:17 — Isaiah 56:7 and Jeremiah 7:11 composite quotation
- **Hebrew**: כִּי בֵיתִי בֵּית תְּפִלָּה יִקָּרֵא לְכָל-הָעַמִּים וְאַתֶּם עֲשִׂיתֶם אֹתוֹ מְעָרַת פָּרִיצִים
- **Note**: The first half closely follows Isaiah 56:7 (כִּי בֵיתִי בֵּית-תְּפִלָּה יִקָּרֵא לְכָל-הָעַמִּים). The second half echoes Jeremiah 7:11 (הַמְעָרַת פָּרִצִים). Delitzsch uses the MT source text for both quotations, which is the correct approach for direct OT citations. The word פָּרִיצִים (robbers/violent men) matches the Jeremiah passage.

### 11:1 — בֵּית-הִינִי (Bethany)
- **Hebrew**: בֵּית-הִינִי for Bethania
- **Note**: Delitzsch renders Bethany as בֵּית-הִינִי rather than the more common בֵּית-עַנְיָה (house of affliction) that appears in some Hebrew traditions. The form בֵּית-הִינִי is a phonetic transliteration rather than an etymological reconstruction. This is acceptable, though בֵּית-עַנְיָה has stronger Hebrew etymological support.

### 11:22 — אֱמוּנַת אֱלֹהִים (faith of God)
- **Hebrew**: תְּהִי-נָא בָכֶם אֱמוּנַת אֱלֹהִים
- **Note**: The construct אֱמוּנַת אֱלֹהִים (faith of God) mirrors the Greek *pistin theou* and Latin *fidem Dei*. In biblical Hebrew, אֱמוּנָה primarily means "faithfulness/steadfastness" (as a divine attribute). The NT sense of "faith in God" is a different semantic range. The Hebrew is grammatically correct but carries the dual resonance of both "faith in God" and "God's own faithfulness."

## Other Notes

### 11:9-10 — Psalm 118 at the triumphal entry
The crowd's acclamation combines Psalm 118:25-26 with a Davidic/messianic expansion. The Hebrew rendering allows a reader to see the Psalm source directly, without the veil of Greek transliteration. This is one of the passages where Hebrew most powerfully reveals the OT substrate of the NT text.

### 11:15-17 — Temple cleansing as prophetic act
Jesus's words in 11:17 combine two prophetic texts (Isaiah 56:7 on the temple as a house of prayer for all nations, and Jeremiah 7:11 on the den of robbers). Both are rendered in Hebrew that tracks the Masoretic originals, which means a Hebrew reader would immediately recognize the prophetic quotations in their original form.
