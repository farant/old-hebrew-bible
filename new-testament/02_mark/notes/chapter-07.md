# Mark Chapter 7 — Translation Notes

## Versification

### 7:16 — Absent from SBLGNT, present in Vulgate and Hebrew
- **Issue**: Mark 7:16 ("Si quis habet aures audiendi, audiat" / "If anyone has ears to hear, let him hear") is present in the Vulgate and Hebrew but entirely absent from the SBLGNT. The Greek field in our data is empty.
- **Vulgate**: *Si quis habet aures audiendi, audiat.*
- **Greek**: (empty)
- **Hebrew**: כָּל-אֲשֶׁר אָזְנַיִם לוֹ לִשְׁמֹע יִשְׁמָע
- **Note**: This verse is omitted by Sinaiticus, Vaticanus, and other major uncials, and is therefore excluded from the SBLGNT. The Vulgate includes it, and we follow the Vulgate. The Hebrew correctly includes it. The verse is a recurring Markan refrain (cf. 4:9, 4:23). Its inclusion is required.

## Content Alignment

### 7:4 — "lectorum" (beds) — bracketed in Hebrew
- **Issue**: The Vulgate includes "et lectorum" (and beds/couches) among the items ritually washed. The SBLGNT also has "kai klinon" in the apparatus. The Hebrew includes [וּמִטּוֹת] (and beds) in brackets, indicating uncertainty.
- **Vulgate**: *baptismata calicum, et urceorum, et aeramentorum, et lectorum*
- **Greek**: *baptismous poterion kai xeston kai chalkion kai klinon* (with apparatus marks on "kai klinon")
- **Hebrew**: כְּמוֹ טְבִילַת כֹּסוֹת וְכַדִּים וְיוֹרוֹת [וּמִטּוֹת]
- **Note**: Since the Vulgate includes "lectorum," the brackets should be removed from the Hebrew. The word וּמִטּוֹת should be included without brackets.

### 7:8 — Bracketed addition on washings
- **Issue**: The Hebrew contains a bracketed addition: [טְבִילוֹת כַּדִּים וְכֹסוֹת וְכָאֵלֶּה רַבּוֹת אַתֶּם עֹשִׂים] ("washings of jars and cups, and many such things you do"). The Vulgate includes equivalent content: "baptismata urceorum et calicum: et alia similia his facitis multa."
- **Vulgate**: *baptismata urceorum et calicum: et alia similia his facitis multa*
- **Hebrew**: [טְבִילוֹת כַּדִּים וְכֹסוֹת וְכָאֵלֶּה רַבּוֹת אַתֶּם עֹשִׂים]
- **Note**: The Vulgate includes this material. The brackets should be removed from the Hebrew since it matches the Vulgate.

### 7:9 — "servetis" (keep) vs "stesetе" (establish)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "ut traditionem vestram servetis" (that you may keep your tradition). The SBLGNT reads "teresete" with apparatus mark; some witnesses read "stesete" (establish). The Hebrew reads שֶׁתִּשְׁמְרוּ (that you may keep), using the root שׁ-מ-ר which matches the Vulgate "servetis."
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly follows the Vulgate. No change needed.

### 7:19 — "purgans omnes escas" (purging all foods)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "purgans omnes escas" (purging all foods), which can be read as a parenthetical editorial comment (Mark declares all foods clean) or as part of the digestive process description. The Greek "katharizon panta ta bromata" is similarly ambiguous. The Hebrew reads הַמְנַקֶּה כָל-הַנֶּאֱכָל ("that which cleanses all that is eaten"), attaching the participle to the digestive process.
- **Vulgate**: *in secessum exit, purgans omnes escas*
- **Hebrew**: וְיֵצֵא אֶל-בֵּית הַכִּסֵּא הַמְנַקֶּה כָל-הַנֶּאֱכָל
- **Note**: The Hebrew interpretation follows the Vulgate's grammatical structure, reading "purgans" as modifying the process of excretion rather than as an authorial declaration. This is the traditional Catholic reading.

### 7:24 — "Tyri et Sidonis" (Tyre and Sidon) vs "Tyri" alone
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "in fines Tyri et Sidonis" (into the borders of Tyre and Sidon). The SBLGNT reads only "Tyrou" (Tyre), with Sidon as a variant. The Hebrew includes both: צוֹר וְצִידוֹן (Tyre and Sidon).
- **Vulgate**: *abiit in fines Tyri et Sidonis*
- **Greek**: *eis ta horia Tyrou* (Tyre only)
- **Hebrew**: אֶל-גְּבוּלוֹת צוֹר וְצִידוֹן (Tyre and Sidon)
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly follows the Vulgate in including both cities. No change needed.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 7:6-7 — Isaiah 29:13 quotation
- **Hebrew**: הָעָם הַזֶּה בִּשְׂפָתָיו כִּבְּדוּנִי וְלִבּוֹ רִחַק מִמֶּנִּי וְתֹהוּ יִרְאָתָם אֹתִי מִצְוֹת אֲנָשִׁים מְלַמְּדִים
- **Note**: Delitzsch closely tracks the Masoretic text of Isaiah 29:13, adapting it to fit the NT context. The MT reads: נִגַּשׁ הָעָם הַזֶּה בְּפִיו וּבִשְׂפָתָיו כִּבְּדוּנִי וְלִבּוֹ רִחַק מִמֶּנִּי וַתְּהִי יִרְאָתָם אֹתִי מִצְוַת אֲנָשִׁים מְלֻמָּדָה. Delitzsch adapts the last phrase to a participle (מְלַמְּדִים, "teaching") to fit the Gospel syntax while preserving the Isaianic language. The LXX version (which the Vulgate follows) differs from the MT, particularly in "in vain do they worship me." Delitzsch here follows the MT wording rather than the LXX.

### 7:10 — כַּבֵּד אֶת-אָבִיךָ וְאֶת-אִמֶּךָ (Honor your father and your mother)
- **Hebrew**: כַּבֵּד אֶת-אָבִיךָ וְאֶת-אִמֶּךָ
- **Note**: Verbatim from Exodus 20:12 / Deuteronomy 5:16. The second quotation, מוֹת יוּמָת (shall surely die), reproduces the standard judicial death formula from Exodus 21:17. Excellent direct OT quotation.

### 7:11 — קָרְבָּן (Corban)
- **Hebrew**: קָרְבָּן פֵּרוּשׁוֹ מַתָּנָה לֵאלֹהִים
- **Note**: The Hebrew retains the Aramaic/Hebrew term קָרְבָּן and glosses it as מַתָּנָה לֵאלֹהִים (a gift to God), matching the Vulgate "quod est donum" and Greek "ho estin Doron." The root ק-ר-ב is thoroughly biblical (Leviticus 1:2 etc.). The gloss uses the פֵּרוּשׁוֹ ("its interpretation") formula, consistent with how Delitzsch handles Aramaic terms (cf. 5:41).

### 7:32 — חֵרֵשׁ וְאִלֵּם (deaf and mute)
- **Hebrew**: אִישׁ אֲשֶׁר הָיָה חֵרֵשׁ וְאִלֵּם
- **Note**: The Greek uses the rare word "mogilalon" (having a speech impediment), which appears in the LXX only at Isaiah 35:6. The Vulgate translates simply as "mutum" (mute). The Hebrew uses אִלֵּם, matching the Vulgate. The Isaiah 35:6 connection (where the tongue of the mute sings for joy) is a key LXX allusion that the crowd recognizes in 7:37.

### 7:34 — אִפַּתַּח (Ephphatha)
- **Hebrew**: אִפַּתַּח וּפֵרוּשׁוֹ הִתְפַּתֵּחַ
- **Note**: The Aramaic imperative is retained and glossed with the Hebrew hitpael הִתְפַּתֵּחַ (be opened). The root פ-ת-ח is thoroughly biblical. The Vulgate's "Adaperire" is accurately rendered.

## Other Notes

### 7:6-7 — Isaiah 29:13 (MT vs LXX)
The quotation of Isaiah 29:13 in Mark 7:6-7 follows the LXX/Vulgate tradition more closely than the MT. The key divergence: the LXX/Vulgate reads "in vain do they worship me, teaching as doctrines the commandments of men" (a two-clause structure), whereas the MT reads this differently. Delitzsch's Hebrew steers closer to the MT wording while accommodating the NT context, creating a slight tension. For our purposes following the Vulgate, the rendering is acceptable.

### 7:32-37 — Isaiah 35:5-6 fulfillment
The healing of the deaf-mute deliberately echoes Isaiah 35:5-6: "Then the ears of the deaf shall be unstopped, and the tongue of the mute shall sing for joy." The crowd's reaction in 7:37 ("He has done all things well; he makes the deaf hear and the mute speak") explicitly recognizes this fulfillment. The Hebrew vocabulary (חֵרֵשׁ, אִלֵּם, נִפְתְּחוּ) connects directly to the Isaianic prophecy.
