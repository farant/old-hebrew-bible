# Mark Chapter 10 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 52 verses align across sources. No numbering mismatches or absent verses.

## Content Alignment

### 10:6 — "Deus" (God as subject of creation)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate explicitly names God as the creator: "masculum et feminam fecit eos Deus." The SBLGNT has "arsen kai thelu epoiesen autous" with no explicit subject "God" (it is implied). The Hebrew adds אֱלֹהִים explicitly: זָכָר וּנְקֵבָה בָּרָא אֹתָם אֱלֹהִים.
- **Vulgate**: *...fecit eos Deus.*
- **Greek**: *epoiesen autous* (he made them — no explicit "God")
- **Hebrew**: בָּרָא אֹתָם אֱלֹהִים (God created them)
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly follows the Vulgate. The verb בָּרָא (created) also matches Genesis 1:27 more closely than the Greek's *epoiesen* (made), which is a strong touch.

### 10:21 — "Take up the cross" (שָׂא אֶת-הַצְּלוּב)
- **Issue**: The SBLGNT does not include "take up the cross" (aras ton stauron) in this verse. The Vulgate also omits it: "vade, quaecumque habes vende, et da pauperibus..." with no cross reference. However, the Hebrew inserts וּבוֹא שָׂא אֶת-הַצְּלוּב וְלֵךְ אַחֲרָי (come, take up the cross, and follow me).
- **Vulgate**: *...et veni, sequere me.* (no cross)
- **Greek**: No *aras ton stauron* in SBLGNT
- **Hebrew**: שָׂא אֶת-הַצְּלוּב (take up the cross — added)
- **Note**: The Hebrew diverges from both the Vulgate and SBLGNT by inserting "take up the cross," likely harmonizing with Mark 8:34. Since we follow the Vulgate, the phrase שָׂא אֶת-הַצְּלוּב should be removed. The Hebrew should read וּבוֹא וְלֵךְ אַחֲרָי.

### 10:24 — "Confidentes in pecuniis" (those trusting in riches)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "confidentes in pecuniis" (those trusting in money). The SBLGNT omits this qualifier — the critical text reads simply "how difficult it is to enter the kingdom of God." The Hebrew includes it: לַבֹּטְחִים עַל-חֵילָם (those trusting in their wealth).
- **Vulgate**: *quam difficile est, confidentes in pecuniis, in regnum Dei introire*
- **Greek**: *pos dyskolon estin eis ten basileian tou theou eiselthein* (no "trusting in riches")
- **Hebrew**: לַבֹּטְחִים עַל-חֵילָם
- **Note**: Follow the Vulgate. The Hebrew correctly includes this qualifier. The phrase חֵילָם (their wealth/strength) for "pecuniis" is good biblical Hebrew (cf. Psalm 62:11).

### 10:29 — "Aut uxorem" (or wife) absent from Vulgate
- **Issue**: The Vulgate list of what one might leave for Christ's sake is: "domum, aut fratres, aut sorores, aut patrem, aut matrem, aut filios, aut agros" — no mention of wife. The Hebrew includes אוֹ אֶת-אִשְׁתּוֹ (or his wife). The SBLGNT likewise omits "wife."
- **Vulgate**: No "uxorem" (wife) in the list
- **Greek**: No *gynaika* (wife)
- **Hebrew**: Includes אוֹ אֶת-אִשְׁתּוֹ (or his wife)
- **Note**: The Hebrew adds "wife" beyond both the Vulgate and SBLGNT, likely harmonizing with Luke 18:29. Since we follow the Vulgate, אוֹ אֶת-אִשְׁתּוֹ should be removed.

### 10:33 — "Et senioribus" (and elders)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate adds "et senioribus" (and elders) to the passion prediction: "tradetur principibus sacerdotum, et scribis, et senioribus." The SBLGNT does not include "elders." The Hebrew does not include elders either: לְרָאשֵׁי הַכֹּהֲנִים וְלַסּוֹפְרִים.
- **Vulgate**: *principibus sacerdotum, et scribis, et senioribus*
- **Greek**: *tois archiereusin kai tois grammateusin* (chief priests and scribes only)
- **Hebrew**: לְרָאשֵׁי הַכֹּהֲנִים וְלַסּוֹפְרִים (chief priests and scribes)
- **Note**: Follow the Vulgate. The Hebrew should add וְלַזְּקֵנִים (and to the elders) to match. This three-part formula (priests, scribes, elders) reflects the composition of the Sanhedrin and appears in the Vulgate's passion predictions consistently.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 10:4 — סֵפֶר כְּרִיתֻת (certificate of divorce)
- **Hebrew**: משֶׁה הִתִּיר לִכְתֹּב סֵפֶר כְּרִיתֻת וּלְשַׁלֵּחַ
- **Note**: Excellent. The phrase סֵפֶר כְּרִיתֻת is taken directly from Deuteronomy 24:1,3 (the Torah passage under discussion). Delitzsch uses the exact Mosaic terminology rather than coining something new, making the intertextual connection explicit.

### 10:9 — לֹא יַפְרִידֶנּוּ אָדָם (let not man separate)
- **Hebrew**: אֵת אֲשֶׁר-חִבַּר אֱלֹהִים לֹא יַפְרִידֶנּוּ אָדָם
- **Note**: Clean biblical syntax with a good chiastic structure: God joined / man shall not separate. The verb חִבַּר is post-biblical (more common in Mishnaic Hebrew), but serves well here. A more biblical alternative might be צָמַד (yoke together) but חִבַּר is clear and functional.

### 10:25 — גָּמָל / מַחַט (camel / needle)
- **Hebrew**: נָקֵל לַגָּמָל לַעֲבֹר בְּנֶקֶב הַמַּחַט
- **Note**: The camel/needle image is rendered naturally. The word מַחַט (needle) does not appear in the MT but is attested in Mishnaic Hebrew. There is no biblical alternative. The construction נָקֵל (it is easier) is clean.

### 10:45 — כֹּפֶר תַּחַת רַבִּים (ransom for many)
- **Hebrew**: וְלָתֵת אֶת-נַפְשׁוֹ כֹּפֶר תַּחַת רַבִּים
- **Note**: Outstanding. The term כֹּפֶר (ransom) is deeply biblical (Exodus 21:30, 30:12; Psalm 49:8) and carries strong atonement theology. The phrase תַּחַת רַבִּים (in place of many) echoes Isaiah 53:11-12. This is one of the most theologically loaded phrases in Mark, and the Hebrew renders it with maximum OT resonance.

### 10:51 — רַבּוּנִי (Rabboni)
- **Hebrew**: וַיֹּאמֶר אֵלָיו הָעִוֵּר רַבּוּנִי
- **Note**: The Aramaic honorific רַבּוּנִי is preserved untranslated, matching both the Greek Rabbouni and the Vulgate Rabboni. This is correct — the word is a direct address in the original languages and should be transliterated, not translated.

## Other Notes

### 10:6-9 — Genesis creation order quotation
Jesus quotes Genesis 1:27 (male and female) and Genesis 2:24 (a man shall leave his father and mother). The Hebrew of both quotations closely follows the Masoretic text, as expected for direct Torah citations. The verb בָּרָא in 10:6 precisely matches Genesis 1:27.

### 10:34 — Passion prediction details
The sequence of verbs in the Hebrew — וִיהָתֵלּוּ (mock) / וְיַכֻּהוּ בַשׂוֹטִים (scourge) / וְיָרֹקּוּ (spit) / וִימִיתֻהוּ (kill) — follows the Vulgate order: illudent, flagellabunt, conspuent, interficient. The Greek has a different order (mock, spit, scourge, kill). The Hebrew tracks the Vulgate sequence correctly.
