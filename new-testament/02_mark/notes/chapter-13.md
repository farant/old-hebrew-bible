# Mark Chapter 13 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 37 verses align across all three sources. No numbering mismatches.

## Content Alignment

### 13:8 — "Initium dolorum haec" (These are the beginning of sorrows)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate places "Initium dolorum haec" at the end of verse 8 as a complete clause. The Hebrew places the equivalent phrase at the beginning of verse 9: אֵלֶּה רֵאשִׁית הַחֲבָלִים. The Greek also places it at the end of verse 8: arche odinon tauta. The Hebrew shifts the clause boundary.
- **Vulgate**: *...et fames. Initium dolorum haec.*
- **Hebrew**: v.8 ends with וּמְהוּמָה; v.9 begins with אֵלֶּה רֵאשִׁית הַחֲבָלִים
- **Note**: The Hebrew has moved "the beginning of sorrows" to the start of verse 9. It should remain at the end of verse 8 to follow the Vulgate.

### 13:8 — "et fames" only vs "fames et perturbationes"
- **Issue**: The Hebrew adds וּמְהוּמָה (and tumult/confusion) at the end of the verse after "famine." The Vulgate reads only "et fames" (and famines). The SBLGNT also lacks the addition. This extra word follows the Byzantine/TR reading.
- **Vulgate**: *et erunt terraemotus per loca, et fames*
- **Hebrew**: וְהָיָה רַעַשׁ כֹּה וָכֹה וְהָיָה רָעָב וּמְהוּמָה (and earthquake here and there and famine and tumult)
- **Note**: The word וּמְהוּמָה should be removed to follow the Vulgate.

### 13:14 — Addition of "Daniel the prophet"
- **Issue**: The Hebrew inserts אֲשֶׁר אָמַר דָּנִיֵּאל הַנָּבִיא (which Daniel the prophet spoke). Neither the Vulgate nor the SBLGNT contain this phrase in Mark 13:14. It is imported from the Matthean parallel (Matthew 24:15).
- **Vulgate**: *Cum autem videritis abominationem desolationis stantem, ubi non debet*
- **Hebrew**: אֶת-שִׁקּוּץ מְשֹׁמֵם אֲשֶׁר אָמַר דָּנִיֵּאל הַנָּבִיא עֹמֵד
- **Note**: The phrase about Daniel is a harmonization with Matthew. It should be removed from the Hebrew to follow the Vulgate.

### 13:33 — "Vigilate, et orate" (Watch and pray)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Videte, vigilate, et orate" (See, watch, and pray). The Greek reads "blepete agrypneite" (look, be alert) without "pray" in the main SBLGNT text. The Hebrew includes prayer: שִׁקְדוּ וְהִתְפַּלֵּלוּ (watch and pray). The Hebrew aligns with the Vulgate here.
- **Vulgate**: *Videte, vigilate, et orate*
- **Hebrew**: רְאוּ שִׁקְדוּ וְהִתְפַּלֵּלוּ
- **Note**: Hebrew correctly includes prayer, following the Vulgate against the SBLGNT critical text. No change needed.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 13:14 — שִׁקּוּץ מְשֹׁמֵם (abomination of desolation)
- **Hebrew**: שִׁקּוּץ מְשֹׁמֵם
- **Note**: Excellent. This is taken directly from Daniel 12:11 and 11:31 (cf. Daniel 9:27). The term שִׁקּוּץ מְשֹׁמֵם is the exact Masoretic phrase. Delitzsch perfectly captures the prophetic allusion.

### 13:19 — עֵת צָרָה (time of tribulation)
- **Hebrew**: כִּי הַיָּמִים הָהֵם יִהְיוּ עֵת צָרָה אֲשֶׁר לֹא-נִהְיְתָה כָמוֹהָ מֵרֵאשִׁית הַבְּרִיאָה אֲשֶׁר בָּרָא אֱלֹהִים
- **Note**: Very strong. The phrase עֵת צָרָה directly echoes Daniel 12:1, which uses the identical expression. The construction אֲשֶׁר לֹא-נִהְיְתָה כָמוֹהָ mirrors the Danielic language. The Hebrew root ב-ר-א for "creation" is the Genesis 1:1 verb, theologically appropriate.

### 13:20 — קִצֵּר (shortened)
- **Hebrew**: וְלוּלֵי קִצֵּר יְהוָֹה אֶת-הַיָּמִים
- **Note**: The verb קִצֵּר ("cut short/shortened") is well chosen. The conditional construction with לוּלֵי is good classical Hebrew. The use of the Tetragrammaton for "Dominus/Kyrios" maintains the OT convention that Delitzsch follows throughout.

### 13:24-25 — Prophetic cosmic imagery
- **Hebrew**: תֶּחְשַׁךְ הַשֶּׁמֶשׁ וְהַיָּרֵחַ לֹא-יַגִּיהַּ אוֹרוֹ ... וְהַכּוֹכָבִים יִפְּלוּ מִן-הַשָּׁמַיִם וַחֲיָלֵי הַשָּׁמַיִם יִתְמוֹטָטוּ
- **Note**: The vocabulary draws from Isaiah 13:10 (the sun darkened, the moon not giving light) and Isaiah 34:4 (host of heaven falling). The word חֲיָלֵי (host/armies) for "virtutes" (powers) uses the OT term for the heavenly host, echoing צְבָא הַשָּׁמַיִם. Good prophetic register.

### 13:34 — שׂוֹעֵר (doorkeeper/watchman)
- **Hebrew**: וְאֶת-הַשׂוֹעֵר צִוָּה לִשְׁקֹד
- **Note**: The term שׂוֹעֵר is the biblical word for gatekeeper (2 Samuel 18:26, 2 Kings 7:10-11). It aptly renders the Vulgate "janitori" and evokes the Israelite role of the temple or city gatekeeper. Appropriate biblical register.

## Other Notes

### 13:32 — "Neque Filius" (nor the Son)
The Vulgate retains "neque Filius" (nor the Son), matching the Greek. The Hebrew renders this as גַּם-לֹא הַבֵּן (also not the Son). This verse is theologically sensitive because it attributes a limitation of knowledge to the Son. The Hebrew faithfully preserves the statement without softening.

### 13:26-27 — Danielic Son of Man
The language of the Son of Man coming in clouds (בֶּן-הָאָדָם בָּא בַעֲנָנִים) directly echoes Daniel 7:13 (עִם-עֲנָנֵי שְׁמַיָּא). The Hebrew captures the intended allusion well, though Daniel's Aramaic uses עִם while the Hebrew uses the preposition בְּ. The Vulgate "in nubibus" aligns with this.
