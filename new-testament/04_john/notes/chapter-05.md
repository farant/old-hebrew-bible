# John Chapter 5 — Translation Notes

## Versification

### 5:2 — "Bethsaida" vs "Bethesda"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Bethsaida." The SBLGNT reads "Bethesda." The Hebrew has "Beit-Chasda." These reflect textual variants in the manuscript traditions (Bethesda, Bethsaida, Bethzatha). The Hebrew "Beit-Chasda" (House of Mercy/Grace) follows the "Bethesda" tradition, not the Vulgate's "Bethsaida."
- **Vulgate**: quæ cognominatur hebraice Bethsaida
- **Greek**: he epilegomene Hebraisti Bethesda
- **Hebrew**: asher yikre'u lah be-lashon ivrit Beit-Chasda
- **Note**: To strictly follow the Vulgate, the Hebrew should render "Beit-Tzaida" or equivalent.

### 5:3-4 — Angel stirring the water (Vulgate canonical)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate includes 5:3b-4 (the sick waiting for the moving of the water; an angel descending and stirring the pool). The SBLGNT omits verse 4 entirely and lacks the latter part of v3. The Hebrew includes the full text in brackets, indicating Delitzsch recognized it as secondary to the critical Greek text but present in the received tradition.
- **Vulgate**: 5:3 "expectantium aquae motum." 5:4 "Angelus autem Domini descendebat secundum tempus in piscinam..."
- **Greek**: 5:3 ends after "xeron" (withered). 5:4 is absent.
- **Hebrew**: Both 5:3b and 5:4 present in brackets.
- **Note**: Per our Catholic-tradition approach, 5:4 is canonical. The brackets in the Hebrew are acceptable as an editorial convention but the text must be included.

### 5:16 — Additional clause
- **Issue**: The Hebrew includes "va-yevakshu hamito" (and they sought to kill him) in brackets. The Vulgate does not include this clause in v16 (it appears in v18 instead). The SBLGNT also lacks it here.
- **Vulgate**: Propterea persequebantur Judæi Jesum, quia hæc faciebat in sabbato.
- **Greek**: kai dia touto ediokon hoi Ioudaioi ton Iesoun hoti tauta epoiei en sabbato.
- **Hebrew**: ...radfu ha-Yehudim et-Yeshua [va-yevakshu hamito] ki-asah eleh ba-Shabbat.
- **Note**: The bracketed addition in the Hebrew goes beyond the Vulgate. It should be removed or clearly marked as not from the Vulgate tradition.

## Content Alignment

### 5:22-23 — Verse boundary shift
- **Issue**: The Vulgate places "ut omnes honorificent Filium, sicut honorificant Patrem" (that all may honor the Son as they honor the Father) in verse 23. The Hebrew combines this clause with verse 22: "le-ma'an yekhabdu khulam et-ha-Ben ka-asher yekhabdu et-ha-Av" appears in the Hebrew of v22, and v23 contains only the second half ("whoever does not honor the Son does not honor the Father who sent him").
- **Vulgate**: v22 ends "omne judicium dedit Filio"; v23 begins "ut omnes honorificent Filium"
- **Hebrew**: v22 includes both the judgment clause and the honor clause; v23 starts at "mi asher lo-yekhabbed"
- **Note**: The verse boundary grouping differs but no content is lost. This is a display/formatting issue rather than a textual one.

### 5:39 — Imperative or indicative?
- **Issue**: The Vulgate "Scrutamini Scripturas" is ambiguous between imperative (Search the Scriptures!) and indicative (You search the Scriptures). The Greek "Eraunate" is likewise ambiguous. The Hebrew "dirshu ba-ketavim" uses the imperative form, reading it as a command.
- **Vulgate**: Scrutamini Scripturas, quia vos putatis in ipsis vitam aeternam habere
- **Greek**: Eraunate tas graphas, hoti hymeis dokeite en autais zoen aionion echein
- **Hebrew**: dirshu ba-ketavim asher tomru she-yesh lakhem chayyei olam ba-hem
- **Note**: Most patristic commentators read this as indicative (a reproach: "You search the Scriptures [and yet]..."). The imperative reading is defensible but the indicative is more common in Catholic tradition.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 5:2 — "al-yad sha'ar ha-tzon"
- **Hebrew**: u-vi-Yerushalayim berekhah al-yad sha'ar ha-tzon
- **Note**: "sha'ar ha-tzon" (Sheep Gate) accurately reflects the Vulgate's "probatica" (pertaining to sheep) and echoes Nehemiah 3:1,32; 12:39 where the Sheep Gate of Jerusalem is mentioned. Excellent biblical vocabulary.

### 5:4 — Angel passage
- **Hebrew**: ki mal'akh yarad be-mo'ado el-ha-berekhah va-yar'esh et-meimeiha
- **Note**: "mal'akh" (angel/messenger), "yarad be-mo'ado" (descended at his appointed time), "va-yar'esh et-meimeiha" (and stirred its waters) -- all excellent biblical Hebrew. "mo'ed" (appointed time/season) echoes the Levitical festival vocabulary and adds liturgical resonance.

### 5:17 — "Avi po'el pe'ullato ad-attah"
- **Hebrew**: Avi po'el pe'ullato ad-attah ve-gam-anokhi po'el
- **Note**: "po'el pe'ullato" (works his work) uses the cognate accusative construction, which is characteristically biblical. The statement that the Father "works until now" is one of the most theologically charged moments in John. The Hebrew's "ad-attah" (until now) is good biblical temporal language (cf. Genesis 32:5).

### 5:35 — "ha-ner ha-doleq ve-ha-me'ir"
- **Hebrew**: hu hayah ha-ner ha-doleq ve-ha-me'ir
- **Note**: "ha-ner ha-doleq ve-ha-me'ir" (the lamp that burns and shines) for "lucerna ardens et lucens." The distinction between "ner" (lamp, as here for John the Baptist) and "or" (light, used of Christ in ch. 1 and 8) is theologically significant and correctly maintained. Cf. Psalm 132:17 where God prepares a "ner" for His anointed.

### 5:46 — "ki hu katav alai"
- **Hebrew**: ki hu katav alai
- **Note**: "he wrote about me" -- the claim that Moses wrote about Christ. The Hebrew is simple and direct. This verse is foundational for Christian typological reading of the Torah.

## Other Notes

### 5:18 — "va-yiddameh le-Elohim"
The Hebrew's "va-yiddameh le-Elohim" (and made himself like God) for "aequalem se faciens Deo" (making himself equal to God). The root d-m-h (to be like/resemble) is weaker than the Vulgate's "aequalem" (equal). The Greek "ison" clearly means "equal." The Hebrew could have used "va-yashev et-atzmo shaveh le-Elohim" for a more precise rendering of equality. This is theologically significant as it concerns Christ's divine nature.
