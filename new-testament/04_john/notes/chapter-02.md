# John Chapter 2 — Translation Notes

## Versification
All 25 verses align across all three sources. No numbering discrepancies.

## Content Alignment

### 2:4 — "Quid mihi et tibi est, mulier?"
- **Issue**: All three sources align well here. The Greek "ti emoi kai soi, gynai" matches the Vulgate "Quid mihi et tibi est, mulier?" The Hebrew "mah-li va-lakh ishah" is an exact biblical idiom (cf. Judges 11:12, 2 Samuel 16:10, 1 Kings 17:18).
- **Note**: No divergence. The Hebrew idiom is perfect biblical Hebrew.

### 2:12 — "fratres ejus"
- **Issue**: All three sources include "his brothers" (fratres/adelphoi/echav). This is theologically significant in Catholic tradition regarding the "brethren of the Lord." No divergence to flag.
- **Note**: Aligned across all sources.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 2:1 — "chatunnah" for "nuptiae"
- **Hebrew**: u-va-yom ha-shelishi hayetah chatunnah be-Qanah asher ba-Galil
- **Note**: "chatunnah" (wedding) is post-biblical; the word is not found in the Hebrew Bible, where "mishteh" (feast/banquet) is the standard term for wedding celebrations (cf. Judges 14:10, 12). However, "chatunnah" is attested in Mishnaic Hebrew and is immediately clear. A more biblical rendering might use "mishteh chatunnah" or simply "mishteh."

### 2:4 — "mah-li va-lakh ishah"
- **Hebrew**: mah-li va-lakh ishah itti adayin lo va'ah
- **Note**: Excellent biblical idiom. "mah-li va-lakh" is exactly the phrase used in Judges 11:12 and 2 Kings 3:13. "Itti adayin lo va'ah" (my time has not yet come) uses "et" (time/season) in a biblical manner reminiscent of Ecclesiastes 3:1.

### 2:6 — "kaddei-even" and measures
- **Hebrew**: shisha kaddei-even arukhim ke-mishpat ha-Yehudim le-tahoratam
- **Note**: "kaddei-even" (stone jars) is acceptable but "kad" typically refers to a water pitcher in biblical Hebrew (cf. Genesis 24:14-20, Judges 7:16). The term works well. "ke-mishpat" (according to the custom/law of) is good biblical Hebrew.

### 2:15 — Temple cleansing vocabulary
- **Hebrew**: va-yiqqach chavalim va-ye'abbetem le-shot va-yegaresh kullam min-ha-miqdash
- **Note**: "va-ye'abbetem le-shot" (braided them into a whip) — "shot" (whip) is biblical (Proverbs 26:3, Nahum 3:2). "va-yefazzer et me'ot ha-shulchanim" (scattered the coins of the money-changers) — "pizzer" is biblical (Psalm 68:31, Proverbs 11:24). Good vocabulary choices throughout.

### 2:17 — Psalm 69:10 quotation
- **Hebrew**: ki qin'at beitekha akhalatni
- **Note**: Directly matches the MT of Psalm 69:10 (ki qin'at beitekha akhalatni). The Vulgate has the future tense "comedit me" while the Hebrew uses the perfect "akhalatni" as in the Psalm. The LXX also has the future (katephagetai), matching the Vulgate. However, since the Vulgate itself has the present/past "comedit," the Hebrew's perfect tense actually aligns well.

### 2:19 — "heirasu/hirsu"
- **Hebrew**: hirsu et ha-heikhal ha-zeh u-vi-shloshah yamim aqimennu
- **Note**: "hirsu" (destroy) from the root h-r-s is good biblical Hebrew (cf. Judges 6:25, 2 Kings 23:7). "Heikhal" (temple/palace) is the appropriate biblical term for the sanctuary proper, as distinct from "miqdash" (the broader temple complex), matching the Greek "naos" vs "hieron" distinction.

## Other Notes

### 2:17 — Psalm 69 connection
The quotation of Psalm 69:10 is the first explicit OT citation in John's Gospel after the Prologue. The Hebrew faithfully preserves the MT wording, creating a seamless connection to the source text.

### 2:19-21 — Temple/Body typology
The word "heikhal" (palace/temple) is used in both the literal sense (the Jerusalem temple) and the figurative sense (Christ's body), which is theologically significant. In 2:21, the Hebrew uses "heikhal geviyyato" (the temple of his body), where "geviyyah" is a somewhat elevated/literary term for body found in the Hebrew Bible (cf. 1 Samuel 31:10, 12; Nahum 3:3).
