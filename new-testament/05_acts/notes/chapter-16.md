# Acts Chapter 16 — Translation Notes

## Versification

All 40 verses align between the Vulgate, SBLGNT, and Hebrew text.

## Content Alignment

### 16:7 — "Spiritus Jesu" vs. "Spiritus Sanctus"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Spiritus Jesu" (the Spirit of Jesus), matching the Greek "pneuma Iesou." The Hebrew reads "ruach Yeshua" (the Spirit of Jesus), which correctly aligns with both.
- **Note**: Good alignment across all three texts. This is a theologically significant phrase — "the Spirit of Jesus" rather than simply "the Holy Spirit" — and the Hebrew preserves it.

### 16:10 — "Deus" vs. "theos" — first person plural shift
- **Issue**: This verse marks the beginning of the first "we" passage in Acts. All three texts agree on the shift to first person plural. The Vulgate "certi facti quod vocasset nos Deus" and the Greek "symbibazontes hoti proskekleitai hemas ho theos" both use "God." The Hebrew uses "haElohim."
- **Note**: Good alignment. The "we" section begins here in all traditions.

### 16:12 — "prima partis Macedoniae civitas, colonia"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate "prima partis Macedoniae civitas" is rendered in Hebrew as "harishonah le'arei hapelekh shel-Maqdonia" (the first of the cities of the district of Macedonia). The term "pelekh" (district) is a good choice, echoing Nehemiah 3:9 usage.
- **Note**: The Hebrew adds "uqlonia hayetah" for "colonia," transliterating the Latin administrative term. Adequate rendering.

### 16:16 — "spiritum pythonem" — divination spirit
- **Issue**: The Vulgate "spiritum pythonem" and Greek "pneuma pythona" describe a python-spirit (oracular spirit). The Hebrew renders this as "ba'alat ov" (a mistress of a necromantic spirit), using the biblical term from Lev 20:27 and 1 Sam 28:7.
- **Note**: Excellent translation choice. Rather than transliterating "python," Delitzsch uses the biblical Hebrew term for divination spirits (ov), connecting this directly to the Torah prohibition.

### 16:17 — "Dei excelsi" — God Most High
- **Issue**: The Vulgate "Dei excelsi" and Greek "theou tou hypsistou" are both rendered "El Elyon" in Hebrew. This is the precise biblical Hebrew title (Gen 14:18-20, Ps 78:35).
- **Note**: Excellent alignment. The use of "El Elyon" creates a direct link to the patriarchal narratives and Melchizedek tradition.

### 16:31 — "Dominum Jesum" — Vulgate adds "Christum"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "Crede in Dominum Jesum" while the SBLGNT reads "Pisteuson epi ton kyrion Iesoun" (without Christon). The Hebrew adds "haMashiach" (the Messiah/Christ), matching the Vulgate fuller reading.
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly follows the Vulgate tradition with the fuller title.

### 16:40 — "consolati sunt" vs. "vayyazhirum" (warned)
- **Issue**: The Vulgate "consolati sunt eos" (comforted/encouraged them) and the Greek "parekalesan tous adelphous" (exhorted the brothers). The Hebrew uses "vayyazhirum" (and they warned them), which has a different nuance — warning rather than comforting.
- **Vulgate**: "consolati sunt eos"
- **Hebrew**: "vayyazhirum" (warned/admonished them)
- **Note**: The Hebrew shifts from encouragement/consolation to warning/admonishment. This does not accurately represent either the Vulgate or the Greek. A better choice would be "vayenachamu" or "vaye'amtzum."

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 16:14 — "Lydia" and "purpuraria"
- **Hebrew**: "mokhereth argaman" (seller of purple)
- **Note**: Good biblical Hebrew. "Argaman" is the biblical term for purple (Exod 25:4, Judg 8:26). The construction echoes the mercantile language of Proverbs 31.

### 16:25 — "kachatzsot halaylah" (about midnight)
- **Hebrew**: "vayehi kachatzsot halaylah uPolos veSila mitpalelim umezamrim l'Elohim"
- **Note**: "Kachatzsot halaylah" is the precise biblical idiom from Exodus 11:4 and Ruth 3:8 (about midnight). The waw-consecutive narrative style here is excellent biblical Hebrew prose.

### 16:26 — Earthquake description
- **Hebrew**: "vehineh ra'ash gadol pit'om vayyazu'u mosdot beit hakele"
- **Note**: "Ra'ash gadol" (great earthquake) and "mosdot" (foundations) are good biblical vocabulary. Cf. Isa 24:18, Ps 82:5. The construction "vayyazu'u mosdot" (the foundations shook) mirrors prophetic earthquake language.

### 16:37 — Roman citizenship declaration
- **Hebrew**: "hakkeh hikkunu neged kol ha'am belo-din umishpat va'anachnu anashim romi'im"
- **Note**: The phrase "belo-din umishpat" (without judgment and justice) is good legal Hebrew but somewhat Mishnaic in register. Biblical Hebrew might prefer something like "beli mishpat" or parallel to Jer 22:13 constructions.

## Other Notes

### 16:3 — Timothy's circumcision
Paul circumcises Timothy despite the Jerusalem Council's decision that Gentile converts need not be circumcised. The reason given (Timothy's Jewish mother, Greek father) shows that the issue was Timothy's Jewish identity, not imposing Torah on Gentiles. This is well preserved in all three texts.

### 16:9-10 — Macedonian call and "we" passage
The shift to first person plural in 16:10 is one of the most discussed features of Acts. The Hebrew preserves this shift naturally with the first person plural verb forms.
