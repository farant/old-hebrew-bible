# Acts Chapter 24 -- Translation Notes

## Versification

The Vulgate contains 27 verses. Verse 24:7 is Vulgate-only (no Greek in SBLGNT). The Hebrew text includes 24:7 in brackets as part of the 24:6-8 complex. This is correct and follows the Vulgate.

### 24:6-8 -- Vulgate's extended Tertullus speech

- **Issue**: The Vulgate's 24:6-8 contains the extended Tertullus speech found in the Western/Byzantine text. The SBLGNT ends 24:6 at ekratesamen ("we seized him") and 24:7 is entirely absent. The Vulgate includes 24:7 ("Superveniens autem tribunus Lysias, cum vi magna eripuit eum de manibus nostris") and the beginning of 24:8 ("jubens accusatores ejus ad te venire"). The Hebrew text places 24:6b-8a in brackets, indicating awareness of the textual variant.
- **Vulgate**: 24:7 -- "Superveniens autem tribunus Lysias..."
- **Hebrew**: brackets from vayyavo aleinu Lusias through va-yetzav et sotnav asher yavo'u lefanekha
- **Note**: The bracketed material in the Hebrew correctly follows the Vulgate's longer reading. The brackets appropriately signal the textual status without omitting the Vulgate content.

## Content Alignment

### 24:2-3 -- Tertullus's flattery split across verses

- **Issue**: The Hebrew merges the content of 24:2 and 24:3 in a way that moves significant material. The Vulgate's 24:2 says "in multa pace agamus per te, et multa corrigantur per tuam providentiam" (we enjoy much peace through you and many things are corrected by your providence). The Hebrew places this content largely in v. 3. The Hebrew's v. 2 is abbreviated to merely "he began to accuse him and said" (vayyahel Tartullos lehayev oto vayyomar), deferring the actual speech content to v. 3.
- **Vulgate**: 24:2 -- "Cum in multa pace agamus per te..."
- **Hebrew**: 24:2 omits the substance of Tertullus's opening flattery, placing it in v. 3
- **Note**: The verse boundary shift does not lose content but may cause confusion when cross-referencing.

### 24:14 -- "Patri et Deo meo" vs. "Elohei avoteinu"

- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "Patri et Deo meo" ("the Father and my God," or more likely "the God of my fathers"). The Greek has to patroio theo ("the ancestral God"). The Hebrew has Elohei avoteinu ("the God of our fathers"), using the first person plural rather than a singular possessive. This is a common biblical formula (cf. Exod 3:13, 2 Chr 20:6).
- **Vulgate**: "Patri et Deo meo" -- the paternal/ancestral God
- **Hebrew**: Elohei avoteinu -- "the God of our fathers"
- **Note**: The Hebrew's plural "our fathers" is a natural biblical Hebrew rendering and effectively communicates the same sense as the Latin "Patri." No correction needed.

### 24:25 -- "Castitate" vs. "perishut"

- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "castitate" (chastity/self-control). The Greek has egkrateias (self-control/continence). The Hebrew has ha-perishut ("abstinence/separation"), from the root p-r-sh. This is a reasonable equivalent but carries a slightly different connotation: perishut in rabbinic Hebrew implies ascetic separation rather than the moral self-mastery of castitas.
- **Vulgate**: "de justitia, et castitate, et de judicio futuro"
- **Hebrew**: al ha-tzedeq ve-al ha-perishut ve-al ha-din he-atid lavo
- **Note**: The Hebrew perishut is acceptable but carries more ascetic overtones than the Latin castitas. The word tzeni'ut (modesty) or kibush ha-yetzer (mastery of impulse) might be closer to the Vulgate's sense, though perishut is defensible.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 24:5 -- "Pestiferum" / "ke-qetev"

- **Hebrew**: ki matzanu et ha-ish ha-zeh ke-qetev -- "we found this man like a pestilence"
- **Note**: Excellent. The word qetev (pestilence/destruction) is strong biblical Hebrew (cf. Deut 32:24, Hos 13:14, Ps 91:6). The simile particle ke- softens it slightly compared to the Vulgate's direct "pestiferum," but this is effective biblical style.

### 24:16 -- "Temim-lev" (integrity of heart)

- **Hebrew**: lakhen ani amel lihyot temim-lev im Elohim ve-adam tamid
- **Note**: The phrase temim-lev ("perfect/whole of heart") is excellent biblical Hebrew, echoing Gen 17:1 (hithallekh lefanai ve-heyeh tamim). The word amel ("toiling/laboring") for the Vulgate's "studeo" (I strive) is a good dynamic equivalent.

### 24:27 -- "Mi-qetz shenatayim yamim"

- **Hebrew**: va-yehi mi-qetz shenatayim yamim
- **Note**: The temporal construction mi-qetz shenatayim yamim ("at the end of two years' time") is an exact biblical parallel to Gen 41:1 (va-yehi mi-qetz shenatayim yamim). This is excellent stylistic alignment with biblical narrative.

## Other Notes

### 24:14 -- "Sectam / haeresim" and "kat"

The Vulgate's double term "sectam quam dicunt haeresim" (the sect which they call a heresy) is rendered in Hebrew simply as kat (sect/faction). The nuance of the Latin -- where the prosecution calls it a hairesis (heresy) but Paul accepts the term "way" (via/ha-derekh) -- is partially preserved in the Hebrew's ha-derekh ha-hi asher yiqra'uha be-shem kat ("that way which they call by the name of a sect").

### 24:15 -- Resurrection of just and unjust

Paul's statement about the resurrection of both the just and the unjust (justorum et iniquorum / la-tzaddiqim ve-la-resha'im) is a significant theological declaration. The Hebrew la-resha'im ("the wicked") for "iniquorum" is standard biblical language.
