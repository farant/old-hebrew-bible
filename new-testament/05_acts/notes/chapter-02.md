# Acts Chapter 2 — Translation Notes

## Versification

All 47 verses align with Vulgate numbering.

## Content Alignment

### 2:1 — "dies Pentecostes" vs "seven weeks"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "dies Pentecostes" (the day of Pentecost), and the Greek "ten hemeran tes pentekostes." The Hebrew renders this as "the day of the completion of seven weeks" (יום מלאת שבעת השבעות), using the Hebrew Feast of Weeks (Shavuot) terminology rather than the Hellenistic "Pentecost."
- **Vulgate**: "Et cum complerentur dies Pentecostes"
- **Note**: The Hebrew rendering is idiomatic and accurate for a Jewish audience. Not a content deviation but a cultural translation choice worth noting.

### 2:17 — "in novissimis diebus" / Joel quotation opening
- **Issue**: The Joel quotation (Joel 3:1-5 LXX / 2:28-32 MT) opens differently across traditions. The Vulgate has "in novissimis diebus, dicit Dominus" (in the last days, says the Lord). The Greek has "en tais eschatais hemerais, legei ho theos" (in the last days, says God). The MT of Joel has "acharei khen" (after this). The Hebrew NT renders "in the last days, says God" (באחרית הימים נאם אלהים), following the Greek/Vulgate "last days" expansion but using "Elohim" rather than "the Lord" (Dominus/YHVH).
- **Vulgate**: "dicit Dominus"
- **Hebrew**: "ne'um Elohim"
- **Note**: The Vulgate's "Dominus" should be reflected in the Hebrew, potentially as "YHVH" or "Adonai." "Elohim" follows the Greek but not the Vulgate.

### 2:20 — "manifestus" vs "epiphane"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "magnus et manifestus" (great and manifest/glorious). The Greek has "megalen kai epiphane" (great and manifest/glorious). The MT Joel has "hagadol vehanora" (the great and terrible). The Hebrew follows the MT with "hagadol vehanora" (הגדול והנורא).
- **Vulgate**: "dies Domini magnus et manifestus"
- **Hebrew**: "yom YHVH hagadol vehanora"
- **Note**: This is significant. The Vulgate/Greek say "great and manifest" while the Hebrew OT says "great and terrible." The Hebrew NT follows the OT Hebrew rather than the Vulgate. Since this is a Septuagint quotation in Acts, the Vulgate's "manifestus" (following the LXX "epiphane") should be preferred.

### 2:24 — "doloribus inferni" vs "pains of death"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "doloribus inferni" (pains of hell/the underworld). The Greek has "tas odinas tou thanatou" (the birth-pains of death). The Hebrew renders "chevlei hamavet" (חבלי המות, the pains/pangs of death), following the Greek.
- **Vulgate**: "solutis doloribus inferni"
- **Greek/Hebrew**: pains of death
- **Note**: The Vulgate's "inferni" rather than "mortis" is a significant theological difference. The Vulgate reading connects to Christ's descent into hell (Apostles' Creed: "descendit ad inferos"). The Hebrew should reflect this: "chevlei she'ol" (חבלי שאול) would match the Vulgate.

### 2:30 — Vulgate Christological expansion
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads simply "de fructu lumbi ejus sedere super sedem ejus" (from the fruit of his loins to sit upon his throne). The Greek is similar. However, the Hebrew contains a bracketed insertion: "[להקים את המשיח כפי הבשר]" ([to raise up the Messiah according to the flesh]). This appears to be a Byzantine/TR addition not present in the Vulgate.
- **Vulgate**: "de fructu lumbi ejus sedere super sedem ejus"
- **Note**: The bracketed material in the Hebrew follows some Greek manuscripts but not the Vulgate. It should be flagged.

### 2:33 — "promissione... accepta a Patre"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate reads "promissione Spiritus Sancti accepta a Patre" (having received the promise of the Holy Spirit from the Father). The Hebrew faithfully renders this: "laqach me'et Aviv et-havtachat Ruach haQodesh."
- **Note**: Good alignment. No issue.

### 2:43 — "in Jerusalem" and Vulgate expansion
- **Issue**: The Vulgate adds "in Jerusalem" and "et metus erat magnus in universis" (and great fear was upon all). The Hebrew includes the bracketed expansion "[ואימה גדולה היתה על כלם]" matching the Vulgate's "metus erat magnus in universis." The "in Jerusalem" also appears.
- **Vulgate**: "multa quoque prodigia et signa per Apostolos in Jerusalem fiebant, et metus erat magnus in universis"
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly includes the Vulgate's longer reading in brackets, indicating awareness of the textual variant. Good practice.

### 2:46-47 — Verse boundary
- **Issue**: The Hebrew splits the content differently from the Vulgate. In the Hebrew, 2:46 ends after "in the houses" and the material about eating food with gladness appears in 2:47. The Vulgate places "sumebant cibum cum exsultatione et simplicitate cordis" in v.46.
- **Note**: The Hebrew rearranges the content across the verse boundary. While the verse numbers match, the distribution of content within verses 46-47 differs slightly.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 2:2 — "ruach se'arah" for "spiritus vehementis"
- **Hebrew**: "כקול רוח סערה" (like the sound of a storm wind)
- **Note**: Excellent biblical parallel. "Ruach se'arah" appears in Ezekiel 1:4, 13:11, 13:13; Psalm 107:25, 148:8; Job 38:1. This evokes the theophanic wind of the prophets. The Vulgate's "spiritus vehementis" (violent wind/spirit) is well served by this choice.

### 2:3 — "leshonot mitpardot" for "dispertitae linguae"
- **Hebrew**: "לשנות מתפרדות" (tongues dividing/separating)
- **Note**: Good rendering. "Mitpardot" from the root p-r-d (separate) is classical biblical Hebrew (cf. Genesis 2:10 where the river "divides").

### 2:25-28 — Psalm 16 quotation
- **Hebrew**: Closely follows MT Psalm 16:8-11.
- **Note**: The Hebrew NT naturally reverts to the MT text of Psalm 16 for this quotation, which Peter cites from the LXX. There are differences: the LXX/Vulgate v.26 has "my tongue" (he glossa mou / lingua mea) while the MT has "my glory" (kevodi). The Hebrew NT uses "kevodi" (כבודי), following the MT rather than the LXX/Vulgate. Since we follow the Vulgate, "leshoni" (my tongue) would be more appropriate here, as this is the Apostolic text quoting the LXX.

### 2:34-35 — Psalm 110:1 quotation
- **Hebrew**: "נאם יהוה לאדני שב לימיני עד אשית איביך הדם לרגליך"
- **Note**: Properly renders the crucial Psalm 110:1. "Ne'um YHVH la-Adoni" (oracle of YHVH to my lord) correctly captures the distinction between Dominus (YHVH) and Domino meo (Adoni).

### 2:38 — "shuvu midarkeikhem" for "poenitentiam agite"
- **Hebrew**: "שובו מדרכיכם" (return from your ways)
- **Note**: The Hebrew expands "poenitentiam agite" (do penance/repent) with "from your ways," which is not in the Vulgate, Greek, or any standard text. The addition "midarkeikhem" is an interpretive expansion. Simple "shuvu" would be more faithful.

## Other Notes

### 2:17-21 — Joel quotation and the Septuagint
This is one of the most important LXX quotations in Acts. The text follows Joel 3:1-5 LXX (= Joel 2:28-32 MT). Key divergences from the MT:
- "In the last days" (LXX/Acts) vs. "after this" (MT)
- "manifest/glorious" (LXX epiphane) vs. "terrible" (MT nora)
The Hebrew NT tends to revert to the MT form of the OT quotation rather than translating the LXX form back into Hebrew. This is a systematic issue for the project.

### 2:36 — Christological climax
"quia et Dominum eum et Christum fecit Deus hunc Jesum" — God has made this Jesus both Lord and Christ. The Hebrew renders "Adon" (אדון) and "Mashiach" (משיח). This verse is central to Petrine Christology in Acts.

### 2:42 — "fractio panis"
The Vulgate's "fractionis panis" (breaking of bread) is rendered "betziat halechem" (בציעת הלחם) — proper biblical Hebrew for the Eucharistic act. Cf. the language in the institution narratives.
