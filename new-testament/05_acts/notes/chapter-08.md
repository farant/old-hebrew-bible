# Acts Chapter 8 — Translation Notes

## Versification

### 8:1 — Saul's consent placed differently
- **Issue**: The SBLGNT and Delitzsch Hebrew include "Saul was consenting to his death" at the start of 8:1. The Vulgate begins 8:1 at "Facta est autem in illa die persecutio magna..." with no mention of Saul's consent here. The Vulgate places the Saul consent clause at the end of 7:60 (some editions) or treats it as implicit from the preceding narrative.
- **Vulgate**: "Facta est autem in illa die persecutio magna in ecclesia..."
- **Greek/Hebrew**: Both begin with Saul consenting to Stephen's death before the persecution statement.
- **Note**: The Hebrew follows the SBLGNT structure. For Vulgate alignment, the Saul clause should either be removed from 8:1 or flagged as belonging to the end of chapter 7.

### 8:37 — The Ethiopian's confession of faith
- **Issue**: Acts 8:37 is absent from the SBLGNT (and most critical Greek texts) but present in the Vulgate. The Hebrew includes it in brackets. This is one of the most significant textual variants in Acts.
- **Vulgate**: "Dixit autem Philippus: Si credis ex toto corde, licet. Et respondens ait: Credo Filium Dei esse Jesum Christum."
- **Greek**: Absent (the SBLGNT skips from 8:36 to 8:38).
- **Hebrew**: Present in brackets: Philip asks if he believes with his whole heart, the eunuch confesses Jesus Christ as Son of God.
- **Note**: The Vulgate authoritatively includes this verse. The Hebrew bracketing reflects modern critical doubt, but from the Vulgate perspective this verse must be retained as canonical. This confession ("Credo Filium Dei esse Jesum Christum") is theologically significant as a baptismal formula.

## Content Alignment

### 8:5 — "the city" vs. "a city"
- **Issue**: The SBLGNT has a textual variant marker on the article before "city." The Vulgate reads "in civitatem Samariae" (the city of Samaria). The Hebrew has "a city of Samaria" without the definite article emphasis.
- **Vulgate**: "in civitatem Samariæ" — the city of Samaria
- **Hebrew**: עִיר שֹׁמְרוֹן — a city of Samaria (indefinite)
- **Note**: The Vulgate implies the principal city. Minor issue but the Hebrew should use הָעִיר for closer alignment.

### 8:10 — "called Great"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "quae vocatur magna" (which is called Great), explicitly stating the title is given to him. The Greek matches. The Hebrew omits the "called" element.
- **Vulgate**: "Hic est virtus Dei, quae vocatur magna"
- **Hebrew**: זֶה הוּא גְּבוּרַת הָאֱלֹהִים הַגְּדוֹלָה — "This is the great power of God" (no "called")
- **Note**: The Hebrew simplifies. The Vulgate's "vocatur" is significant because it indicates a title conferred by the people, not an objective description.

### 8:19 — Split verse in Vulgate
- **Issue**: The Vulgate appends "Petrus autem dixit ad eum:" (But Peter said to him:) at the end of verse 19, bridging into Peter's response. The Greek and Hebrew place Peter's response entirely in verse 20.
- **Vulgate**: 8:19 ends with "Petrus autem dixit ad eum:"
- **Greek/Hebrew**: Peter's reply begins at 8:20.
- **Note**: This is a minor versification difference; the content is the same but the verse boundary differs.

### 8:20 — "existimasti" vs. "said"
- **Issue**: The Vulgate has "donum Dei existimasti pecunia possideri" (you thought the gift of God could be purchased with money). The Hebrew renders this as "you said to purchase" (אָמַרְתָּ לִקְנוֹת), which weakens the Vulgate/Greek sense of "thinking/supposing."
- **Vulgate**: "existimasti" — you supposed/reckoned
- **Greek**: ἐνόμισας — you thought/supposed
- **Hebrew**: אָמַרְתָּ — you said
- **Note**: The Hebrew should use a verb of thinking (חָשַׁבְתָּ or דִּמִּיתָ) rather than speaking to match the Vulgate and Greek.

## Hebrew Translation Quality

### 8:1 — Excellent biblical narrative style
- **Hebrew**: וַתְּהִי בָּיּוֹם הַהוּא רְדִיפָה גְדוֹלָה
- **Note**: The wayyiqtol chain and vocabulary here are good biblical Hebrew. רְדִיפָה is a natural choice for "persecution."

### 8:21 — Excellent OT echo
- **Hebrew**: אֵין-לְךָ חֵלֶק וְגוֹרָל בַּדָּבָר הַזֶּה כִּי לְבָבְךָ אֵינֶנּוּ יָשָׁר לִפְנֵי הָאֱלֹהִים
- **Note**: Superb. חֵלֶק וְגוֹרָל echoes the Levitical language of inheritance portions (cf. Deut 10:9, 12:12). לְבָבְךָ אֵינֶנּוּ יָשָׁר לִפְנֵי הָאֱלֹהִים echoes 2 Kgs 10:15. The Delitzsch rendering here preserves the Hebraic register of Peter's rebuke.

### 8:23 — Outstanding OT allusion
- **Hebrew**: כִּי-רֹאֶה אָנֹכִי כִּי-בָאתָ בִּידֵי מְרוֹרַת רוֹשׁ וְחַרְצֻבּוֹת רָשַׁע
- **Note**: מְרוֹרַת רוֹשׁ alludes to Deut 29:17 (LXX 29:18) "root bearing gall and wormwood." חַרְצֻבּוֹת echoes Isa 58:6. This is excellent biblical Hebrew that preserves the OT allusive texture of the original.

### 8:32-33 — Isaiah 53 quotation
- **Hebrew**: כַּשֶּׂה לַטֶּבַח יוּבָל וּכְרָחֵל לִפְנֵי גּוֹזְזֶיהָ נֶאֱלָמָה
- **Note**: The Hebrew correctly reproduces the Isaiah 53:7-8 quotation in a form close to the Masoretic text rather than a back-translation from Greek. This is appropriate since the passage is being read from the Hebrew scroll in the narrative.

## Other Notes

### 8:26-40 — Philip and the Ethiopian eunuch: LXX connection
- **Note**: The entire passage hinges on the Ethiopian reading from the Greek Septuagint of Isaiah 53. The quotation in 8:32-33 follows the LXX of Isa 53:7-8 more closely than the MT (particularly "in his humiliation his judgment was taken away"). This is one of the most important LXX-dependent passages in Acts and establishes the Septuagint as the authoritative Scripture text for the early Church.

### 8:37 — Baptismal formula significance
- **Note**: The confession "Credo Filium Dei esse Jesum Christum" preserved in the Vulgate is one of the earliest baptismal formulas. Its presence in the Western text tradition and the Vulgate gives it particular authority in the Catholic tradition, regardless of its absence from the Alexandrian Greek manuscripts.
